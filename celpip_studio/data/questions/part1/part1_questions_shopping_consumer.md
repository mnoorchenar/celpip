# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 13: Shopping & Consumer Decisions — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Major Purchase Regret — Spent Too Much**
> Your friend says: "I bought a brand new laptop three weeks ago and I deeply regret it. It was far more expensive than I needed and now I'm struggling financially. I can't return it because I've already used it extensively. I feel foolish and I don't know how to handle the financial pressure I've created. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Purchase regret is near-universal; involves emotional response, financial consequences, and practical recovery.

---

**Q2. Pressured Into Buying Something They Don't Need**
> Your friend says: "I went to a store to buy one small item and a very persuasive salesperson convinced me to buy a much more expensive package with features I don't need. I agreed in the moment but now I'm home and I regret it completely. The return window is three days and I feel embarrassed going back. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Salesperson pressure and buyer's remorse is a widely shared experience; practical steps and confidence-building advice needed.

---

**Q3. Online Purchase Gone Wrong — Seller Won't Refund**
> Your friend says: "I bought an item online that looked exactly like what I needed, but what arrived was completely different from the description and photos. The seller refuses to offer a refund and has stopped responding to my messages. I paid $200 and I feel cheated. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Online shopping disputes are increasingly common; practical consumer rights advice needed.

---

**Q4. Impulse Buying Ruining the Budget**
> Your friend says: "I cannot walk through a mall or browse online without buying things I don't need. I buy things impulsively when I'm bored, stressed, or even happy. By the end of the month I have bags of things I barely use and an empty bank account. I've tried budgeting but I always find a justification to spend. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Impulse buying is a defining consumer behaviour issue; psychological and practical advice both needed.

---

**Q5. Buying New vs. Buying Used — Can't Decide**
> Your friend says: "I need to replace my old appliance and I'm torn between buying new with a warranty or buying used to save money. Every new thing I consider seems overpriced but I've also heard horror stories about used appliances failing quickly. I can only afford to make this decision once right now. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* New vs. used decisions are faced constantly by families; practical, universally relatable.

---

**Q6. Subscription Services Piling Up — Unnoticed Charges**
> Your friend says: "I recently went through my bank statements and discovered I'm paying for seven subscription services — some I haven't used in months and one I forgot I even signed up for. The total is over $150 a month. I don't know how to decide what to cut or how to stop this from happening again. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Subscription creep is a very modern and common financial drain; practical and behavioural advice needed.

---

**Q7. Falling for an Advertised Deal That Turned Out to Be a Scam**
> Your friend says: "I saw an incredible deal online for a product I'd been wanting for a long time. I ordered it, paid $300, and nothing ever arrived. The website has since disappeared. I feel humiliated and angry. I don't know if there's any way to recover my money. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Online shopping fraud is widespread and growing; emotional shame combined with financial loss.

---

**Q8. Extended Warranty — Worth It or Not?**
> Your friend says: "I'm about to buy a major appliance and the store is strongly pushing a three-year extended warranty for an extra $200. I don't know if these warranties are ever worth the money or just profit for the store. The salesperson is being very persistent. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Extended warranty decisions come up constantly in consumer purchases; tests practical reasoning.

---

**Q9. Keeping Up With Trends — Spending Beyond Their Means**
> Your friend says: "I feel pressure to always have the latest phone, the newest fashion, and the most current tech. My friends and social media make me feel outdated if I don't keep up. I know I'm spending money I don't have but I can't seem to stop. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Consumer identity and social pressure around purchases is a deeply modern issue.

---

**Q10. Returning a Defective Product — Store Refusing**
> Your friend says: "I bought an expensive item that stopped working after three weeks. When I went back to the store, they told me the damage looked like user error and refused to refund or exchange it. I believe it was a manufacturing defect. I don't know what my rights are or how to fight this. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Consumer rights disputes with retailers are common and frustrating; practical advice on escalation needed.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Gifting Dilemma — Spending Too Much to Meet Expectations**
> Your friend says: "Every holiday season and birthday, I feel pressured to buy expensive gifts for family and friends to match what they spend on me. I genuinely cannot afford it but I feel that giving a modest gift will make me look cheap or uncaring. I dread every celebration now. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Hired a Service Provider Who Did Poor Work**
> Your friend says: "I hired a cleaner, painter, and handyman through an app and paid upfront. The work was far below what was promised — shortcuts were taken, some tasks weren't finished at all, and they left a mess. They've refused to come back and fix it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Second-Hand Marketplace Purchase — Item Was Misrepresented**
> Your friend says: "I bought a piece of furniture from someone on an online marketplace. When I picked it up, it had significant damage that wasn't visible in the photos and wasn't mentioned in the listing. The seller is now saying it was sold as-is. I feel like I was deceived. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Grocery Budget Spiralling — Food Costs Too High**
> Your friend says: "My grocery bill has doubled over the past two years and I'm really struggling to feed my family on my current budget. I'm buying the same things as before but spending almost twice as much. I don't know how to reduce costs without compromising nutrition or making my family miserable at mealtimes. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q15. Brand Loyalty vs. Better Value Alternatives**
> Your friend says: "I've always bought the same brands out of habit — cleaning products, food, clothing. A friend showed me that generic or store brands are often significantly cheaper and sometimes identical in quality. But I feel irrationally attached to brands I've used my whole life. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Rent-to-Own Scheme — Is It Worth It?**
> Your friend says: "A furniture store is offering a rent-to-own deal on a sofa set I really want but can't afford upfront. The monthly payments seem manageable, but when I calculated the total I'd pay, it's almost three times the retail price. I still find myself tempted because I need furniture now. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q17. Charity Solicitations — Feeling Pressured to Donate**
> Your friend says: "I'm approached constantly — at stores, online, by phone — to donate to charities. I want to be generous but I'm on a tight budget and I feel guilty every time I say no. Some of these organizations also feel questionable to me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Black Friday / Sale Event — Overspending Every Year**
> Your friend says: "Every year during major sale events I go overboard — I buy things I don't need just because they're discounted. Afterwards I always regret it. I tell myself I'll show restraint this year but when the sales arrive I get swept up in the excitement. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Buying in Bulk — Worth It for a Small Household?**
> Your friend says: "Friends keep telling me to shop at warehouse stores and buy in bulk to save money. But I live alone and I'm worried about waste — food going bad, using storage space, and spending large amounts upfront even if the per-unit price is lower. Is it actually worth it for someone like me? What advice would you give?"

---

**Q20. Sustainable and Ethical Shopping — Hard to Maintain on a Budget**
> Your friend says: "I want to shop ethically — sustainable brands, fair trade, local businesses — but the prices are significantly higher than mainstream alternatives. I care deeply about these values but I'm on a limited budget and I feel guilty every time I choose the cheaper, less ethical option. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Luxury Item Purchase — Hard to Justify but Deeply Wanted**
> Your friend says: "I've been saving for a luxury item that I've wanted for years. I can technically afford it without going into debt, but I feel guilty spending that much on something non-essential when I could put the money toward something more practical. My partner says I've earned it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Too Many Clothes — Can't Stop Shopping for Fashion**
> Your friend says: "My wardrobe is overflowing with clothes I rarely wear, yet I keep buying more. I tell myself I need something for a specific occasion and then it sits unused. I've tried a shopping ban before but it never lasts more than two weeks. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Misleading Advertising — Product Didn't Match Claims**
> Your friend says: "I bought a health product based on bold advertising claims that promised specific results. After three months of using it correctly, I've seen no results whatsoever. The small print says results are not guaranteed. I feel misled. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Peer Pressure to Buy Into a Group Purchase**
> Your friend says: "My group of friends wants to make a large joint purchase — a shared vacation rental property — and they're pressuring me to contribute my share. I like the idea in principle but I have concerns about the legal arrangements and what happens if relationships change. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Children Demanding Expensive Items**
> Your friend says: "My children constantly ask for expensive toys, gadgets, and brand-name clothing because their classmates have them. I don't want my children to feel left out but I also can't afford to keep up with every trend and I worry about teaching them unhealthy values. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Product Recalled After Purchase — Unsure What to Do**
> Your friend says: "I recently saw a news report that a product I own has been recalled due to a safety issue. The recall process seems complicated, the company has been hard to reach, and I'm not sure if my specific model is affected. I'm worried but I don't know what steps to take. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Getting Out of a Gym Contract**
> Your friend says: "I signed a two-year gym contract last year and I've only been three times. My life circumstances changed — a new job, a baby — and I simply cannot use it. The cancellation fee is $400 and the gym says there are no exceptions. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Price Gouging During a Shortage**
> Your friend says: "During a recent shortage, prices for essential items in my area skyrocketed at some local stores — items that normally cost $5 were being sold for $25. I need these items but I feel it's wrong to pay inflated prices and reward this behaviour. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Identity Stolen Via a Store Data Breach**
> Your friend says: "I received a letter from a retailer saying my personal and financial information was exposed in a data breach. I've already noticed one suspicious charge on my credit card. I don't know what I need to do right now to protect myself and what steps to take over the coming weeks. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q30. Overconsumption — Trying to Live With Less**
> Your friend says: "I've been reading about minimalism and I want to drastically reduce what I own and buy. But I live with family members who don't share this philosophy and our home is full of things they're not willing to part with. I also don't know how to maintain this in a consumer culture. What advice would you give?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Bidding War at an Auction — Got Carried Away**
> Your friend says: "I went to an auction intending to spend $500 maximum. In the excitement of bidding I got completely carried away and ended up winning an item for $2,200 that I don't even need that much. I feel embarrassed and financially stressed. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Counterfeit Product Purchased Unknowingly**
> Your friend says: "I bought what I believed was an authentic branded product online at a discounted price. I recently discovered it's a counterfeit — the quality is poor and it may even be unsafe. I'm angry at being misled but I'm also not sure I have much recourse since I bought from an overseas seller. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q33. Hoarding Purchases Out of Fear of Shortage**
> Your friend says: "After experiencing shortages a few years ago, I started stockpiling things compulsively — non-perishables, toiletries, cleaning supplies. My storage space is overflowing and I'm still buying more even though the shortages are long over. My family thinks it's become excessive. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Supporting Local vs. Buying Cheaper From Large Chains**
> Your friend says: "I feel a genuine responsibility to support local small businesses in my community, but their prices are often significantly higher than large chains. On my budget, consistently choosing local means sacrificing in other areas of my life. I feel guilty every time I choose the cheaper option. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q35. Toy or Equipment Injury — Considering Legal Action**
> Your friend says: "My child was injured by a toy that I believe had a design flaw. The injury was minor but could have been much more serious. I've contacted the company and they offered a small voucher as compensation. I'm wondering whether I should pursue this further legally. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Subscription Services Accumulating Unnoticed**
> Your friend says: "I recently went through my bank statements and discovered I'm paying for eight subscription services, several of which I barely use anymore. I've been meaning to cancel them but the services make it deliberately complicated, and honestly I keep putting it off. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Buyer Remorse on a Major Purchase Past the Return Window**
> Your friend says: "Two months ago I bought a very expensive piece of furniture that I thought I loved, but I've realized it doesn't work in my space and I genuinely regret it. It's past the return period and I can't easily resell it. I feel foolish and I don't know what to do. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q38. Feeling Pressured to Spend on Gifts Beyond Your Budget**
> Your friend says: "Every holiday season and every birthday, I feel pressured to buy expensive gifts to match what others in my social circle spend. I genuinely can't afford it but I feel embarrassed saying so, and I don't want anyone to think less of me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Discovering a Favourite Brand Uses Unethical Practices**
> Your friend says: "I recently learned that a clothing brand I've been loyal to for years has been linked to serious labour exploitation overseas. I feel genuinely upset but I also love their products and I shop there regularly. I'm not sure what to do with this information. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Online Marketplace Dispute — Seller Will Not Refund**
> Your friend says: "I bought an expensive item through an online marketplace and it arrived damaged. The seller is refusing to refund me and the platform's dispute process has gone nowhere after three weeks. I'm out a significant amount of money and I feel powerless. What advice would you give me?"
