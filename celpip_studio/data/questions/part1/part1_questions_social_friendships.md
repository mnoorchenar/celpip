# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 9: Social Life & Friendships — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Making Friends After Moving to a New City**
> Your friend says: "I moved to a new city eight months ago for work and I still haven't made a single real friend. I go to work, come home, and spend my weekends alone. I've tried joining a gym and going to community events but I haven't clicked with anyone. I'm starting to feel very lonely and isolated. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* One of the most universal struggles for immigrants and newcomers to Canada; deeply relatable with multiple practical advice paths.

---

**Q2. Growing Apart From a Long-Time Friend**
> Your friend says: "My best friend of 15 years and I have been drifting apart. Our lives have gone in very different directions — different values, different priorities, and we have less and less to talk about. I miss what we had but every time we meet it feels forced. I don't know whether to invest more in the friendship or let it fade. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Friendship evolution is a universal life experience; emotionally layered with no clear right answer.

---

**Q3. Friend Who Is Always Negative and Draining**
> Your friend says: "I have a friend who is constantly negative — they complain about everything, never have anything good to say, and leave me feeling exhausted and drained after every interaction. I care about them but spending time with them has become something I dread. I don't want to abandon them. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Emotionally draining friendships are widely relatable; tests empathy alongside self-care advice.

---

**Q4. Being Left Out by a Friend Group**
> Your friend says: "I've noticed that my group of friends has been making plans without inviting me. I've seen photos on social media of gatherings I wasn't told about. I don't know if I've done something wrong or if they're just drifting away from me. It's really hurting me but I don't know how to bring it up. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Social exclusion is deeply painful and universally experienced; combines emotional and practical advice.

---

**Q5. Extreme Shyness Preventing a Social Life**
> Your friend says: "I'm extremely shy and it has prevented me from building meaningful friendships my whole life. I want to connect with people but I freeze in social situations, say the wrong thing, and go home feeling embarrassed. I've been this way my whole life and I'm starting to accept loneliness as my normal. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Social anxiety and shyness affect a large portion of the population; deeply personal with multiple advice angles.

---

**Q6. Friend Asking for Help Too Often — One-Sided Friendship**
> Your friend says: "I have a friend who only contacts me when they need something — help moving, borrowing money, emotional support during a crisis. But when I need support, they're never available or interested. I've been a loyal friend for years but I feel completely taken for granted. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* One-sided friendships are a very common source of frustration; tests ability to advise on boundaries.

---

**Q7. Reconnecting With Old Friends After Years Apart**
> Your friend says: "I've lost touch with a group of close friends from university. We used to be inseparable but life pulled us in different directions and now it's been almost seven years with barely any contact. I recently thought about reaching out but I'm worried it'll be awkward or that they've moved on completely. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Reconnecting after a long gap is a relatable experience; emotional vulnerability makes it rich for advice.

---

**Q8. Friend Going Through a Crisis — Not Sure How to Help**
> Your friend says: "My close friend is going through a very difficult time — they lost their job, their relationship ended, and they seem deeply depressed. Every time I try to help they push me away. I don't want to say the wrong thing or make things worse, but I also can't just do nothing. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Supporting a friend in crisis tests emotional intelligence and practical knowledge; no easy answer.

---

**Q9. Social Anxiety at Work Events and Gatherings**
> Your friend says: "My workplace has frequent social events — team lunches, happy hours, holiday parties — and I dread every single one. I never know what to talk about, I stand awkwardly in corners, and I always leave early. My manager has noticed my absence and mentioned it. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Workplace social pressure is a very common and specific anxiety; practical and emotional advice both needed.

---

**Q10. New to Canada — Struggling to Connect With Locals**
> Your friend says: "I've been in Canada for almost two years and I find it very hard to make genuine connections with people born here. People are polite on the surface but it rarely goes deeper than small talk. I have friends from my home country but I want to integrate more fully. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Directly mirrors the experience of the majority of CELPIP test-takers; cultural nuance makes it especially rich.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Friend Group Conflict — Being Caught in the Middle**
> Your friend says: "Two of my closest friends have had a serious falling out and both of them are pressuring me to take sides. I care about both of them equally and I don't want to lose either friendship, but the pressure to choose is becoming unbearable. Every time I see one, the other gets upset. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q12. Friend Making a Serious Mistake — Should They Say Something?**
> Your friend says: "I can see that my friend is making a decision that I believe will seriously harm them — a risky business investment with a questionable person. They haven't asked for my opinion and they seem very excited about it. I'm worried that speaking up will damage our friendship. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Introvert Feeling Pressure to Socialise More**
> Your friend says: "I'm a natural introvert and I genuinely recharge by being alone. But my friends, partner, and even my family constantly tell me I need to get out more, that I'm antisocial, and that something must be wrong with me. I feel pressured to be someone I'm not. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Workplace Friendship That Has Become Complicated**
> Your friend says: "I became very close friends with a colleague and we used to spend time together outside of work. But since they were promoted above me, things have changed — they've become more distant and professional. I miss the friendship but I don't know how to navigate the new dynamic. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q15. Friend Who Gossips and Cannot Keep Secrets**
> Your friend says: "I shared something personal with a close friend in confidence and found out they told several other people. This isn't the first time. I'm hurt and I've lost trust in them, but we have a long history and many mutual friends. I don't know whether to confront them or quietly create more distance. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q16. Ending a Toxic Friendship**
> Your friend says: "I have a friend who is manipulative, frequently puts me down in subtle ways, and creates drama wherever they go. I know the friendship is unhealthy but we've been friends for a long time and letting go feels disloyal. I feel trapped. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q17. Making Friends as a Stay-at-Home Parent**
> Your friend says: "Since I stopped working to raise my young children, I've lost almost all of my social connections. My friendships were mostly work-based and I don't have the time or energy for the social life I used to have. I feel isolated and like my world has become very small. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Friend Who Constantly Cancels Plans**
> Your friend says: "I have a friend who agrees to every plan we make but cancels at the last minute almost every time, usually with a vague excuse. I've started to feel like I can't count on them and I'm beginning to stop making the effort. I don't want to give up on the friendship. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q19. Outgrowing a Childhood Friendship**
> Your friend says: "My oldest friend and I have known each other since we were children, but as adults we have very little in common anymore. Our conversations are shallow and I sometimes feel like we're only still friends out of habit and shared history rather than genuine connection. What would you advise me to do?"

---

**Q20. Friend Group Pressure to Drink or Party**
> Your friend says: "My social circle revolves almost entirely around drinking and late-night parties. I've been trying to live a healthier lifestyle and I feel uncomfortable in these situations now. But every time I decline or suggest something different, I'm teased or pressured. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Jealousy Toward a More Socially Successful Friend**
> Your friend says: "One of my friends seems to effortlessly attract people and is always surrounded by a large social circle. I feel invisible by comparison and sometimes feel jealous when I'm with them, which makes me feel ashamed. It's starting to affect how much I enjoy their company. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q22. Making Friends at an Older Age**
> Your friend says: "I'm 55 years old and I've recently realised that most of my friendships have faded and I have very few close friends left. I want to build new connections but I feel awkward about making friends at my age — it feels unnatural and forced. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Online Friends vs. In-Person Friendships**
> Your friend says: "Most of my closest friendships exist entirely online — people I've never met in person but have known for years through gaming and online communities. My family says these aren't real friendships and that I need to get out more. I genuinely value these relationships. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Friend in Denial About a Problem**
> Your friend says: "My friend has been drinking heavily for over a year and they completely deny that it's a problem. Whenever I bring it up they get angry and say I'm exaggerating. Other mutual friends have noticed but no one wants to confront them. I'm scared for their health. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q25. Navigating Friendships After a Divorce**
> Your friend says: "Since my divorce most of our mutual friends have sided with my ex-partner, which I didn't expect. I've found myself suddenly very isolated after a decade of what I thought were solid friendships. I don't know how to rebuild a social life from scratch at 40. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Cultural Differences Causing Misunderstandings With Friends**
> Your friend says: "I've noticed that some of my misunderstandings with local Canadian friends come from cultural differences — in communication style, humour, and social expectations. I sometimes say the wrong thing without realizing it and I'm not sure how to bridge this gap. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Friend Who Constantly Competes With You**
> Your friend says: "Every time I share good news with a particular friend, they immediately try to one-up me or redirect the conversation to their own achievements. I can never just share something without it becoming a competition. I'm starting to avoid telling them things. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Spending Too Much to Keep Up With a Wealthier Friend Group**
> Your friend says: "My close friends have much higher incomes than I do and they regularly suggest expensive restaurants, trips, and activities. I've been spending beyond my means to keep up because I don't want to feel embarrassed or left out. My finances are suffering. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Friend Moving Away — Afraid of Losing the Relationship**
> Your friend says: "My closest friend is moving to another country next month for a job opportunity. I'm genuinely happy for them but I'm terrified that the friendship won't survive the distance. Most of my other long-distance friendships have faded over time. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q30. Being Too Honest and Losing Friends Because of It**
> Your friend says: "I have a reputation for being very direct and honest, which I believe is a positive trait. But I've recently lost two friendships because people said I was harsh or insensitive. I'm not sure whether I need to change how I communicate or whether those weren't the right friendships anyway. What advice would you give?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Friendship With Someone Much Older or Younger**
> Your friend says: "I've developed a close friendship with someone 25 years older than me. We have a lot in common and I genuinely value the relationship, but people keep making assumptions about it or questioning my motives. It's starting to affect how comfortable I feel in the friendship. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Friend Group Excluding a New Member**
> Your friend says: "A new person has joined our friend group and most of the group has been cold toward them and excludes them from conversations. The new person has done nothing wrong — I think the group is just resistant to change. I feel uncomfortable with the situation. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Reconnecting With a Friend After a Serious Falling Out**
> Your friend says: "Three years ago I had a serious argument with a close friend that ended the friendship completely. We both said things we regret. I've been thinking about reaching out to apologize and try to repair things, but I'm scared of being rejected or reopening old wounds. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q34. Social Isolation During Illness or Recovery**
> Your friend says: "After a long illness that kept me homebound for six months, I've lost touch with almost all my friends. Most stopped reaching out after a while. Now that I'm recovering, I feel invisible — like I've been forgotten. I don't know how to rebuild my social life. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Fear of Vulnerability — Preventing Deep Friendships**
> Your friend says: "I have many acquaintances but no truly close friends. I've realised that I keep everyone at arm's length because I'm afraid of being hurt or judged if I show my real self. I've been like this for as long as I can remember. I'm lonely but I don't know how to change. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Friend Who Constantly Cancels Plans at the Last Minute**
> Your friend says: "I have a close friend who cancels our plans almost every time — sometimes with only an hour's notice. I've rearranged my schedule repeatedly and I feel disrespected, but whenever I see them in person everything is great between us. I don't know whether to say something or just stop making plans. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Being Phased Out of a Friend Group Without Explanation**
> Your friend says: "I've noticed that I'm being left out of events and group chats that I used to be part of. No one has said anything directly and when I bring it up they deny anything is wrong. It hurts deeply and I don't know if I've done something, if this is drifting apart, or if something else is going on. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q38. Long-Distance Friendship Fading — How to Maintain It**
> Your friend says: "My closest friend moved to another country two years ago and although we had every intention of staying close, we now go weeks without speaking. When we do talk it feels slightly awkward, like we're losing our connection. I don't want to lose this friendship but I don't know how to maintain it across distance. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Friend Group Dynamics Shifting After Major Life Event**
> Your friend says: "Since one member of our friend group had a baby, the entire dynamic has shifted — most plans now revolve around the baby and those who don't have children feel increasingly left out. I care about everyone but I'm feeling disconnected from people I used to be very close to. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q40. Making New Friends as an Adult — Nothing Sticking**
> Your friend says: "I've been trying to make new friends since moving to a new city — I join events, I meet people, but nothing ever becomes a real friendship. People are friendly at the time but no one follows up. I'm starting to wonder if there's something wrong with me. What advice would you give me?"
