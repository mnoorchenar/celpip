# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Main Topic Categories

Part 1 requires giving advice to a friend, family member, or colleague about a real-life situation.
Each category below represents a distinct topic area for generating practice content.

---

### 1. Career & Work
Advice on job changes, workplace conflicts, promotions, resignations, starting a business, dealing with a difficult boss or coworker, remote work challenges, work-life balance.

### 2. Health & Lifestyle
Advice on diet, exercise habits, sleep, quitting smoking, managing a chronic condition, going to the doctor, stress reduction, healthy aging.

### 3. Family & Relationships
Advice on marriage, divorce, in-law conflicts, long-distance relationships, breakups, sibling disputes, supporting an aging parent, reconnecting with family.

### 4. Education & Learning
Advice on choosing a program, dropping out vs. continuing, online learning, managing study load, changing majors, applying to graduate school, learning a new skill.

### 5. Finance & Money
Advice on budgeting, debt management, saving for retirement, investing for the first time, dealing with unexpected expenses, asking for a raise, lending money to a friend.

### 6. Housing & Home
Advice on renting vs. buying, dealing with a difficult landlord, home renovations, moving to a new city, downsizing, finding a roommate, neighborhood safety concerns.

### 7. Travel & Vacation
Advice on planning a trip on a budget, solo travel safety, travelling with kids, choosing a destination, travel insurance, what to do when plans go wrong.

### 8. Technology & Digital Life
Advice on screen time, online privacy, social media use, cyberbullying, choosing a new device, working from home setups, protecting against scams.

### 9. Social Life & Friendships
Advice on making new friends after moving, handling a friendship conflict, saying no to social pressure, dealing with a toxic friend, building a social network in a new city.

### 10. Parenting & Children
Advice on discipline, screen time for kids, school struggles, raising teenagers, co-parenting after separation, childcare choices, supporting a child with anxiety.

### 11. Stress & Mental Wellbeing
Advice on burnout, anxiety, loneliness, grief, work-related depression, seeking therapy, self-care routines, coping with major life changes.

### 12. Transportation & Commuting
Advice on buying a car vs. using transit, dealing with a long commute, learning to drive, biking to work, road trip planning.

### 13. Shopping & Consumer Decisions
Advice on a major purchase (car, appliance, furniture), avoiding impulse buying, returning a defective product, comparing brands, online shopping safety.

### 14. Environment & Community
Advice on reducing waste, composting, volunteering in the neighbourhood, dealing with a noisy neighbour, joining a community group, sustainable living on a budget.

### 15. Cultural & Social Adaptation
Advice on adjusting to a new country or city, overcoming language barriers, navigating cultural differences at work or school, homesickness, building confidence in a new environment.

### 16. Food & Nutrition
Advice on meal planning, switching to a healthier diet, cooking at home vs. eating out, food allergies, picky eating in children, managing weight.

### 17. Sports & Recreation
Advice on getting started with a sport or hobby, recovering from an injury, joining a team, balancing exercise with a busy schedule, choosing the right activity for fitness goals.

### 18. Personal Development
Advice on building confidence, public speaking fear, time management, goal setting, overcoming procrastination, journaling, developing a growth mindset.

### 19. Communication & Conflict Resolution
Advice on how to have a difficult conversation, apologizing sincerely, setting boundaries, dealing with passive-aggressive behaviour, navigating a disagreement at work or home.

### 20. Volunteer & Community Service
Advice on finding volunteer opportunities, balancing volunteering with other commitments, the benefits of community involvement, what to do when a volunteer role becomes overwhelming.
