# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 1: Career & Work — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, relatable, multiple valid advice paths, common in Canadian workplace context*

---

**Q1. Quitting a Job Without a New One Lined Up**
> Your friend says: "I hate my job and I can't take it anymore. The work is stressful, my boss is demanding, and I feel burned out every day. I'm thinking about quitting right away, even though I don't have another job yet. What do you think I should do?"

*Why high:* Extremely relatable, emotionally loaded, requires balanced advice (financial risk vs. mental health).

---

**Q2. Choosing Between Two Job Offers**
> Your friend says: "I just got two job offers at the same time. The first one pays much more money but the hours are long and the commute is far. The second pays less but has a better work environment and is close to home. I don't know which one to take. What would you advise me to do?"

*Why high:* Classic dilemma with no single right answer — ideal for demonstrating reasoning skills.

---

**Q3. Asking the Boss for a Raise**
> Your colleague says: "I've been at this company for three years and I've never asked for a raise. I do good work and I know others in similar roles earn more than me. I'm nervous about bringing it up with my manager. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Very common workplace situation, candidates from all backgrounds can relate.

---

**Q4. Dealing With a Difficult Coworker**
> Your friend says: "There's a coworker who constantly takes credit for my ideas in team meetings. My manager doesn't seem to notice, and it's affecting my confidence and motivation. I don't want to cause conflict. What should I do?"

*Why high:* Interpersonal conflict at work is universally relatable and has several advice angles.

---

**Q5. Starting a New Job — Feeling Overwhelmed**
> Your friend says: "I started a new job two weeks ago and I feel completely overwhelmed. There's so much to learn, my teammates seem to already know everything, and I'm afraid of making mistakes. I'm even thinking of leaving. What advice can you give me?"

*Why high:* New job anxiety is near-universal; tests empathy and practical advice.

---

**Q6. Work-Life Balance Problems**
> Your friend says: "My job is taking over my life. I work late almost every night, I skip meals, and I haven't had a day off in months. My family is complaining that I'm never home. I don't want to lose my job but I feel like I'm losing everything else. What should I do?"

*Why high:* Extremely common in Canada, especially among immigrants; emotionally rich scenario.

---

**Q7. Returning to Work After a Long Break**
> Your friend says: "I took two years off work to raise my children and now I want to go back. But I'm worried that my skills are outdated, that employers won't hire me, and that I've lost my confidence. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Highly relevant to CELPIP test-taker demographics (many are new immigrants or returnees).

---

**Q8. Remote Work vs. Going Back to the Office**
> Your colleague says: "My company is asking everyone to come back to the office full-time after two years of working from home. I've been more productive at home and my commute is two hours a day. I'm thinking about looking for a fully remote job instead. What do you think?"

*Why high:* Post-pandemic topic that is very current and relatable.

---

**Q9. Being Laid Off — What to Do Next**
> Your friend says: "I was laid off from my job last month. I've been applying everywhere but haven't heard back. I'm starting to run out of savings and I'm feeling hopeless. My family depends on me financially. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Financial pressure + emotional distress = rich scenario for advice.

---

**Q10. Changing Careers at 40**
> Your friend says: "I'm 40 years old and I've been working in accounting for 15 years, but I've never enjoyed it. I want to switch to something I'm passionate about, like photography or design. My family thinks it's too risky at my age. What would you advise me to do?"

*Why high:* Touches values, risk, family expectations — lots to advise on.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Job Interview Preparation**
> Your friend says: "I have a job interview next week for my dream position, but I'm terrible at interviews. I get nervous, forget what to say, and stumble over my words. The last three times I interviewed, I didn't get the job. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q12. Starting a Small Business**
> Your friend says: "I've always wanted to start my own business selling handmade goods online. I have some savings and a good product idea, but I've never run a business before and I'm afraid of failing. Should I go for it or is it too risky?"

---

**Q13. Conflict With a Manager**
> Your friend says: "My manager has been treating me unfairly. She gives me the most work, rarely acknowledges my contributions, and sometimes speaks to me rudely in front of the team. I don't know whether to talk to her directly, go to HR, or just start looking for another job. What should I do?"

---

**Q14. Feeling Stuck in a Dead-End Job**
> Your friend says: "I've been in the same position for six years with no promotions and no salary increase. When I ask about growth opportunities, my manager just says 'be patient.' I feel like I'm wasting my best years. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q15. Taking a Pay Cut for a Better Opportunity**
> Your friend says: "I've been offered a job at a startup I really believe in, but the salary is 20% less than what I make now. The upside is more responsibility, flexibility, and the chance to grow with the company. My partner thinks it's a bad idea. What do you think I should do?"

---

**Q16. Workplace Harassment**
> Your friend says: "A colleague at work keeps making inappropriate comments about my appearance and personal life. I've asked them to stop but they continue. I'm uncomfortable going to work now but I'm afraid that reporting it will make things worse. What would you advise me to do?"

---

**Q17. Freelancing vs. Full-Time Employment**
> Your friend says: "I've been offered a full-time job with benefits, but I'm also considering staying freelance where I earn more per hour but have no job security or benefits. I have a family to support. Which path would you recommend?"

---

**Q18. Going Back to School While Working Full-Time**
> Your friend says: "I want to get a certification to advance my career, but my program requires evening and weekend classes for two years. My job is demanding and I have young kids at home. Is it worth doing? How would I manage everything?"

---

**Q19. Moving to Another City for a Job**
> Your friend says: "I've been offered my dream job in another city, six hours away from where my family lives. The pay is great but I'd have to leave my parents, my friends, and the community I've built here. I can't decide what to do. What would you advise?"

---

**Q20. Volunteering vs. Paid Work for Career Entry**
> Your friend says: "I just moved to Canada and can't find a paid job in my field because I have no Canadian experience. Someone suggested I volunteer in my industry to build connections and a local resume. But I need income. What advice would you give me?"

*Why relevant:* Directly reflects the experience of many CELPIP test-takers (new immigrants).

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Being Passed Over for a Promotion**
> Your colleague says: "I was passed over for a promotion again, and the person who got it has less experience than me. My manager said I need to 'work on my leadership skills' but gave me no specifics. I feel disrespected and unmotivated. What should I do?"

---

**Q22. Excessive Overtime — Saying No to the Boss**
> Your friend says: "My boss keeps assigning me overtime work and expects me to be available on weekends. I'm afraid to say no because I don't want to be seen as not committed. But it's affecting my health and my family life. How should I handle this?"

---

**Q23. Colleague Spreading Rumours**
> Your friend says: "A coworker has been spreading false rumours about me at work — saying I'm lazy and unreliable, which is completely untrue. It's starting to affect how my manager sees me. I don't want to stoop to their level. What would you advise?"

---

**Q24. Salary Negotiation for a New Job**
> Your friend says: "I have a job offer and I'm happy with everything except the salary. I want to negotiate for more money but I've never done it before and I'm afraid they'll withdraw the offer if I ask. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Imposter Syndrome at a New Senior Role**
> Your friend says: "I just got promoted to a manager position, but I feel like I'm not qualified and that everyone will soon realize I don't know what I'm doing. I'm afraid to make decisions in case I get it wrong. How can I get past this feeling?"

---

**Q26. Working With People From Different Cultures**
> Your colleague says: "I joined a very diverse team and I sometimes misunderstand my teammates because of cultural differences in communication styles. I don't want to offend anyone but I also struggle to get my point across. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Taking Parental Leave — Worried About Career Impact**
> Your friend says: "I just had a baby and I'm planning to take parental leave for a year. But I'm worried that my employer will forget about me, that I'll miss promotions, and that my skills will be outdated when I return. What would you advise?"

---

**Q28. Dealing With Burnout**
> Your friend says: "I've been working non-stop for two years and lately I feel completely empty — no motivation, no energy, and no joy in anything I do, even outside of work. My doctor says I'm burned out. But I'm afraid to take time off because of what my boss will think. What should I do?"

---

**Q29. Job Offer During Probation Period**
> Your friend says: "I started a new job three months ago and I'm still on probation. Now I've received a much better job offer from another company. I feel guilty leaving so soon, but this new opportunity is too good to ignore. What would you advise me to do?"

---

**Q30. Networking — Feeling Uncomfortable**
> Your friend says: "Everyone tells me I need to network to advance my career, but I'm very introverted and I find networking events extremely uncomfortable. I don't know what to say to strangers and I always end up leaving early. Any advice?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Employee Stealing Credit for Work**
> Your friend says: "My manager presented my entire project to senior leadership without mentioning my name once. Everyone congratulated him, not me. I feel angry and invisible. Should I speak up, and if so, how?"

---

**Q32. Retiring Early vs. Continuing to Work**
> Your friend says: "I'm 55 and I have enough savings to retire early, but I'm not sure I'm ready to stop working. My family wants me to retire and travel with them, but I love my job and feel I still have a lot to contribute. What would you advise?"

---

**Q33. Whistleblowing — Reporting Wrongdoing at Work**
> Your friend says: "I discovered that my company is doing something illegal — falsifying reports. I have evidence but I'm afraid to report it because I might lose my job and face retaliation. I also feel like I can't just stay quiet. What should I do?"

---

**Q34. Being Asked to Do Something Unethical**
> Your friend says: "My manager has asked me to alter some numbers in a report to make the results look better to clients. It's not technically illegal but it feels dishonest. I don't want to do it but I also don't want to lose my job. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Long Commute — Is It Worth It?**
> Your friend says: "I got a great job but the commute is three hours a day by public transit. After two months I'm exhausted, I'm not sleeping enough, and I feel like I spend my whole life on the bus. Is there a way to make this work, or should I look for something closer to home?"

---

**Q36. Offered a Lateral Move — Not Sure if It's a Step Forward**
> Your friend says: "My company has offered me a lateral transfer to a different department. The role is at the same level and pay, but it would give me new skills and more visibility. Some colleagues say it's a dead end; others say it's a smart move. I have to decide by the end of the week. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. New Manager With a Very Different Style — Hard to Adapt**
> Your friend says: "My previous manager and I had a great working relationship. My new manager micromanages everything, second-guesses my decisions, and has a very different communication style. My productivity and motivation have both dropped. I don't know how to adapt or whether to escalate. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Being Passed Over for a Project You Were Best Suited For**
> Your friend says: "A high-profile project came up that I was clearly the most qualified for, and I expressed my interest to my manager. It was given to a colleague with less experience. I don't know if there's a hidden message in this decision or if I should raise it directly. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Freelance Client Refusing to Pay After Completing the Work**
> Your friend says: "I completed a large project for a freelance client and submitted my invoice, but they are now disputing the amount and refusing to pay. I have the original agreement in writing but I don't know how to proceed — I've never dealt with a non-payment situation before. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q40. Career Change Seen as a Step Down by Family**
> Your friend says: "I've decided to leave a high-paying corporate job to do work that genuinely matters to me, even though it pays significantly less. My family thinks I'm throwing away everything I worked for. I'm confident in my decision but their disapproval is affecting me more than I expected. What advice would you give me?"
