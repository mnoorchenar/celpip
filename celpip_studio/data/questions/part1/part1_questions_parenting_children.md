# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 10: Parenting & Children — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Disciplining a Child — Disagreement Between Parents**
> Your friend says: "My husband and I completely disagree on how to discipline our children. I believe in setting firm boundaries and consequences, while he thinks I'm too strict and prefers to let things go. The kids have noticed the inconsistency and are starting to play us against each other. Our arguments about this are getting worse. What advice would you give us?"

*Why high:* Parenting disagreements between partners are universal; no single right approach; emotionally loaded.

---

**Q2. Child Refusing to Go to School — Separation Anxiety**
> Your friend says: "Every morning is a battle to get my 7-year-old to school. They cry, cling to me, complain of stomachaches, and beg to stay home. The school says they settle down after I leave but the daily struggle is exhausting me and I'm not sure whether to push harder or be more gentle. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* School anxiety in young children is extremely common; tests empathy and practical strategies.

---

**Q3. Teenager Completely Withdrawn — Worried About Mental Health**
> Your friend says: "My 15-year-old has become increasingly withdrawn over the past several months. They stay in their room, barely speak to us, have lost interest in things they used to love, and seem sad all the time. When I try to talk to them they say everything is fine. I'm scared but I don't want to push them away. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Teen mental health is one of the most pressing parenting concerns today; emotionally urgent.

---

**Q4. Raising Children With Two Different Cultural Identities**
> Your friend says: "My children were born in Canada but my husband and I come from a very different cultural background. We want our children to be proud of their heritage and speak our language, but they're becoming increasingly resistant — they want to be like their Canadian friends and see our traditions as embarrassing. What advice would you give us?"

*Why high:* Directly reflects the identity tension experienced by virtually every immigrant family in Canada — uniquely powerful for CELPIP.

---

**Q5. Child Being Bullied at School — Parent Unsure How to Help**
> Your friend says: "My 11-year-old has been coming home upset for weeks. I recently found out they are being bullied at school — name-calling, exclusion from groups, and social media comments. I've spoken to the teacher but the situation hasn't improved. My child begs me not to escalate it. I feel helpless. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Bullying affects families at every level; combines school system navigation with emotional support.

---

**Q6. Comparing Children to Each Other — One Feels Like the Favourite**
> Your friend says: "My two children are very different — one is academically strong and socially confident, while the other struggles at school and is shy. I try to treat them equally but my younger child has told me they feel less loved and less valued than their sibling. This has broken my heart. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Sibling comparison and perceived favouritism is a deeply painful and common family dynamic.

---

**Q7. Balancing Work and Being Present for Children**
> Your friend says: "I work long hours to provide for my family but my children have started saying they never see me. Last week my 8-year-old said they wished I worked less and spent more time with them. I felt terrible but I genuinely don't know how to change my situation without risking our financial stability. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* The work-parent balance tension is near-universal; emotionally heavy with no easy solution.

---

**Q8. Child Struggling Academically — To Tutor or Not**
> Your friend says: "My 10-year-old has been falling behind in math and reading for two years. Their teacher says they need extra support. I want to get a tutor but I'm worried about putting too much pressure on a young child. I also can't afford a tutor indefinitely. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Academic struggles in children are extremely common; involves finances, pressure, and child wellbeing.

---

**Q9. Permissive vs. Strict Parenting — Questioning Their Approach**
> Your friend says: "I've always tried to be an understanding and flexible parent — I don't want to be as strict as my own parents were. But lately I wonder if I've been too lenient. My teenage children don't respect boundaries, don't do chores, and expect me to solve all their problems. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Parenting style self-doubt is something almost every parent experiences at some point.

---

**Q10. Child With Special Needs — Navigating School and Support Services**
> Your friend says: "My 8-year-old was recently diagnosed with ADHD and a learning disability. The school says they qualify for support but the process is slow, unclear, and bureaucratic. I don't know what questions to ask, what I'm entitled to, or how to advocate for my child effectively. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Special needs navigation in the Canadian school system is a significant and emotionally heavy challenge for many families.

**Q11. Teenager Lying Constantly — Loss of Trust**
> Your friend says: "My 16-year-old lies to me about almost everything — where they're going, who they're with, whether they've done their homework. I've caught them multiple times and the consequences don't seem to change anything. I feel like I can't trust a word they say. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Co-Parenting After Separation — Ongoing Conflict**
> Your friend says: "My ex-partner and I separated two years ago and we share custody of our two children. But every interaction between us turns into an argument and the children are clearly caught in the middle. I want to do what's best for them but I find it very hard to communicate with my ex civilly. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Child Who Has No Friends — Socially Isolated**
> Your friend says: "My 9-year-old has no friends at school. They eat lunch alone, are never invited to birthday parties, and come home sad almost every day. They're a kind and gentle child and I don't understand why other children don't include them. I'm heartbroken and I don't know how to help. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Teenager Wanting Too Much Independence Too Soon**
> Your friend says: "My 14-year-old wants to take public transit alone across the city, stay out until late with friends, and have no curfew. They say all their friends have this freedom and I'm being overprotective. I want to respect their growing independence but I'm genuinely worried about their safety. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Child Exposed to Parents' Argument — Damage Control**
> Your friend says: "My husband and I had a very serious argument last week and our two children, aged 8 and 11, heard everything. Since then they've been quieter than usual and my youngest has been clingy and anxious. I don't know what to say to them or whether to address it directly. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Child Asking Difficult Questions About Death or Loss**
> Your friend says: "My 6-year-old's grandfather recently passed away and now they won't stop asking me questions about death — where Grandpa went, whether I will die, whether they will die. I'm grieving myself and I don't know how to answer these questions in a way that is honest but not frightening. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q17. Teenager Involved With a Bad Crowd**
> Your friend says: "My 17-year-old has started spending time with a group of friends I'm very concerned about. Their behaviour has changed — they're secretive, coming home late, and I suspect they may be experimenting with alcohol or drugs. Every time I raise it we have a major argument. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Pressure to Enrol Child in Too Many Activities**
> Your friend says: "My child is in soccer, piano, swimming, and tutoring — all things other parents in our community said were essential. But my child is exhausted, complains about going to everything, and we spend every evening rushing from one activity to the next. I don't know what to cut. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Child Refusing to Eat — Mealtime Battles Every Day**
> Your friend says: "Every meal is a battle with my 5-year-old. They refuse to eat almost everything except a handful of foods, throw tantrums at the table, and I end up making separate meals just to get something into them. My mother-in-law says I'm spoiling them. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q20. Single Parent — Exhausted and Overwhelmed**
> Your friend says: "I'm a single parent raising two children under 8 with very little support. I work full-time, manage the home alone, help with homework, and try to be emotionally present — all without a partner. I love my children deeply but I'm running on empty and I feel guilty every time I fall short. What advice would you give me?"

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Explaining a Serious Family Situation to Young Children**
> Your friend says: "My husband and I are going through a separation and I don't know how to explain it to our children aged 5 and 7. I want to be honest but I don't want to frighten them or make them feel responsible. I also don't know how much detail is appropriate for their ages. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Child Showing Signs of Anxiety**
> Your friend says: "My 9-year-old has been showing signs of anxiety for months — avoiding new situations, excessive worrying, physical complaints before school events, and trouble sleeping. The school hasn't flagged anything serious but I can see my child is struggling. Should I seek professional help? What advice would you give?"

---

**Q23. Grandparents Undermining Parenting Rules**
> Your friend says: "Every time my children visit my parents, the rules I've set at home go out the window — no bedtime, unlimited sugar, screens all day. My children then come home expecting the same at home. I don't want to damage my relationship with my parents but I'm frustrated. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Child Asking About Adoption or Their Origins**
> Your friend says: "My adopted child, now 10, has started asking more detailed questions about their birth parents and why they were adopted. I want to be honest and age-appropriate, but some of the truth is painful and I'm not sure how much to share. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Teenager Wanting to Drop Out of High School**
> Your friend says: "My 17-year-old has told me they want to drop out of high school and start working full-time. They say school is pointless and they want to earn their own money. I'm firmly against it but every argument just makes them more determined. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Child Developing a Fear After a Traumatic Event**
> Your friend says: "After a minor car accident six months ago, my 7-year-old has developed an intense fear of riding in cars. They panic and cry every time we need to go somewhere by car. It's affecting our daily life significantly and the fear doesn't seem to be fading on its own. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q27. Balancing Attention Between a New Baby and an Older Child**
> Your friend says: "Since we brought our new baby home, our 4-year-old has been acting out — having tantrums, regressing to baby-like behaviour, and saying they wish the baby would go away. I understand why but I'm exhausted and finding it very hard to give both children what they need. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q28. Child Addicted to Video Games — Affecting Everything**
> Your friend says: "My 12-year-old plays video games from the moment school ends until I force them to stop at night. Their grades are suffering, they've lost interest in outdoor activities, and they become angry and aggressive when I limit their time. I feel like the games have taken over my child. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q29. Raising Children in a Religious Household When One Parent is Not Religious**
> Your friend says: "I was raised in a religious household and I want to raise my children with the same faith and values. My partner is not religious and disagrees with formal religious education for the children. This is becoming a serious point of conflict between us. What advice would you give us?"

---

**Q30. Child Who Is Gifted but Bored and Misbehaving at School**
> Your friend says: "My daughter's teacher says she is academically very advanced but is becoming disruptive in class because she finishes work quickly and gets bored. The school doesn't have a gifted program. I'm not sure whether to push for a grade skip, find a different school, or take another approach. What advice would you give?"

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Child Asking About Race or Racism After an Incident**
> Your friend says: "My 8-year-old came home from school upset after a classmate made a racist comment about our family's background. They're asking me questions I don't know how to answer — why people say things like that and whether it means we don't belong here. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Deciding Whether to Have Another Child**
> Your friend says: "My husband wants a third child and I'm genuinely undecided. I love our two children but I'm already stretched thin — emotionally, financially, and physically. I don't want to regret not having a third, but I'm also afraid of how it would affect our family balance. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Child Witness to Domestic Conflict — Seeking Help**
> Your friend says: "My children have witnessed several serious arguments between my partner and me, including one that became physically threatening. I've been in denial about how bad things have gotten, but I can see my children are frightened and changing. I don't know where to start. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Parenting a Child With an Eating Disorder**
> Your friend says: "I've noticed that my 14-year-old has been restricting what they eat, hiding food, and becoming very anxious around mealtimes. When I try to discuss it they deny everything and get very upset. I suspect it may be an eating disorder but I don't want to make things worse by handling it badly. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q35. Sending a Child to Board School or Away for Education**
> Your friend says: "My 13-year-old has been offered a place at an excellent boarding school in another city. It's a great academic opportunity but they would be away from home nine months of the year. I'm not sure my child is ready emotionally, and honestly neither am I. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Child Coming Out — Parent Unsure How to Respond**
> Your friend says: "My teenage child came out to me recently. I love them unconditionally and I want to be supportive, but I'm honestly struggling internally — with what this means for their future, with my own feelings, and with how to navigate extended family and our cultural community. I don't want to say the wrong thing. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Toddler With Severe Tantrums — Parent Questioning Own Patience**
> Your friend says: "My two-year-old has meltdowns that are intense and frequent — screaming, throwing things, hitting. I've read the books, I try to stay calm, but sometimes I reach my limit and I lose my patience in ways I'm not proud of. I feel like I'm failing as a parent. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Adult Child Refusing to Work or Study**
> Your friend says: "My 24-year-old has been living at home for two years with no job and no intention of studying. They spend their days online and show no interest in changing. I love my child but I don't know whether to keep supporting them, set a deadline, or something else entirely. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q39. Child Diagnosed With ADHD — Family Disagreement About Medication**
> Your friend says: "Our child has been diagnosed with ADHD and the specialist recommends medication. I'm open to trying it but my partner is completely opposed — they believe it's overdiagnosed and that medication will change our child's personality. We are at an impasse. What advice would you give us?"

---

**Q40. Co-Parenting With a Difficult Ex-Partner**
> Your friend says: "I share custody of my children with my ex-partner and it has been a constant source of conflict. They undermine the rules I set, speak negatively about me to the kids, and refuse to communicate unless forced to. I don't want to take legal action but things cannot stay like this. What advice would you give me?"
