# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 14: Environment & Community — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Wanting to Live More Sustainably but Unsure Where to Start**
> Your friend says: "I feel genuinely worried about climate change and I want to make changes in my daily life to reduce my environmental impact. But every time I research it I get overwhelmed — there's too much information, conflicting advice, and I'm not sure which changes actually make a meaningful difference. I don't want to just feel good about doing nothing. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Environmental concern is mainstream in Canada; practical guidance on starting small vs. big changes is universally useful.

---

**Q2. Neighbour Dispute Over Noise or Behaviour**
> Your friend says: "My next-door neighbour plays loud music several nights a week until past midnight. I've knocked on their door twice and they apologize but then do it again within days. I haven't slept properly in weeks and I'm dreading going home. I don't want to escalate things but I can't continue like this. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Neighbour conflict is one of the most common community issues; requires both assertiveness and diplomacy advice.

---

**Q3. Feeling No Sense of Belonging in the Neighbourhood**
> Your friend says: "I've lived in my neighbourhood for two years but I don't know a single neighbour by name. I feel anonymous and disconnected. People walk past each other without acknowledgement and there's no sense of community at all. I'd like to change that but I don't know how to start without seeming strange. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Community disconnection is a major Canadian urban experience, particularly for newcomers.

---

**Q4. Excessive Household Waste — Family Not On Board**
> Your friend says: "I've been trying to reduce our household waste — composting, recycling properly, buying less packaging — but my family isn't cooperating. My partner thinks I'm being extreme and my children refuse to follow the new rules. I'm making changes alone and getting frustrated. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Household sustainability conflict is a genuine and growing tension in many Canadian families.

---

**Q5. Local Area Getting Worse — Feeling Unsafe**
> Your friend says: "The neighbourhood I've lived in for seven years has been changing — there's more crime, more visible drug use, and public spaces have deteriorated. I no longer feel safe walking after dark and my children can't play outside freely. I feel helpless about whether things can actually change. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Neighbourhood safety decline is a major concern in many Canadian cities; practical and community-level advice needed.

---

**Q6. Witnessing Someone Littering or Polluting — Should They Say Something?**
> Your friend says: "I regularly see people dumping garbage in a park near my home — sometimes it's individuals littering, but recently I saw someone illegally dumping construction waste. I want to do something but I'm not sure whether to confront them directly, report it, or just clean it up myself. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Environmental civic responsibility vs. personal safety is a relatable everyday dilemma.

---

**Q7. Getting Involved in the Community — Doesn't Know How**
> Your friend says: "I've been living in Canada for three years and I want to become more involved in my local community — volunteering, joining a group, attending events — but I don't know where to start, I'm shy in new social situations, and I'm not sure where I would fit in or be welcome. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Community integration is a central challenge for immigrants; directly relevant to the CELPIP demographic.

---

**Q8. Local Business Being Replaced by a Large Chain — Should They Fight It?**
> Your friend says: "A beloved local grocery store in my neighbourhood that I've shopped at for years is closing because a large supermarket chain is moving in. Many residents are upset and there's talk of a petition and protest. I care about the community but I'm not sure if opposing it will make any difference. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Gentrification and local business displacement is a live issue in virtually every Canadian city.

---

**Q9. Climate Anxiety — Overwhelmed by Environmental News**
> Your friend says: "I've been reading a lot about climate change and environmental destruction and I'm overwhelmed by feelings of hopelessness. I feel guilty about my own impact, anxious about the future, and paralysed — like nothing I do makes a difference. It's affecting my daily mood. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Climate anxiety is now a recognised psychological phenomenon, especially among younger adults.

---

**Q10. Strata or Condo Association Being Unresponsive to Residents**
> Your friend says: "I live in a condominium and there are several maintenance issues in the building that have been raised multiple times with no action from the condo board. Residents are frustrated but no one knows how to push back effectively. I'm paying monthly fees and getting nothing in return. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Condo and strata issues are extremely common in Canadian urban centres; practical civic advice needed.

**Q11. Reducing Car Use — Partner Not Willing**
> Your friend says: "I've decided I want to significantly reduce how much we drive as a family to lower our carbon footprint. But my partner says it's impractical given our work schedules, our children's activities, and where we live. We're disagreeing frequently over this. What advice would you give us?"

---

**Q12. Community Garden — Neighbour Conflict Over Space**
> Your friend says: "I joined a community garden in my neighbourhood and one member has been taking more than their allocated space, using others' tools without asking, and ignoring the group's rules. Other members are uncomfortable but no one wants to say anything directly. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Should They Report a Neighbour to the City?**
> Your friend says: "My neighbour has been making illegal home renovations — building a structure without permits that blocks light into my yard and may affect the drainage in our shared area. I'm not sure whether reporting them to the city is the right move or whether it will just create lasting hostility between us. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q14. Child's School Has No Recycling Program — Parent Wants to Change It**
> Your friend says: "My child's school produces an enormous amount of waste but has no composting or recycling program. I want to advocate for change but I've never approached the school administration before and I don't want to be dismissed or labelled a troublemaker. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Newcomer Trying to Understand Canadian Civic Culture**
> Your friend says: "I'm new to Canada and I don't fully understand how civic life here works — how local government makes decisions, how residents get involved, what influence regular people actually have. I care about my community but I feel like an outsider in the processes that shape it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Homeless Person Regularly Outside Their Building**
> Your friend says: "There is a person experiencing homelessness who regularly sleeps in the doorway of my apartment building. Some residents want to call the authorities to have them removed. I don't think that's right but I also don't know what the appropriate or helpful response actually is. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q17. Fast Fashion Habit — Wants to Change**
> Your friend says: "I buy cheap, trendy clothing constantly and I know the fashion industry is one of the most polluting in the world. I've been trying to change my habits but I find it hard to justify spending three times more on an ethical brand for the same type of item. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Organising a Community Cleanup — No One Is Interested**
> Your friend says: "I tried to organise a neighbourhood cleanup event. I posted on community boards, knocked on doors, and made flyers — and only two people showed up. I care deeply about this but I feel like no one else does. I don't know whether to keep trying or accept it won't happen. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q19. Petitioning Against a Development — Worth the Effort?**
> Your friend says: "The city has approved a large warehouse development near our residential street that will bring heavy truck traffic, noise, and pollution. Residents have started a petition but most people I speak to are doubtful it will change anything. I believe it's worth fighting. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q20. Plastic Use at Work — Colleagues Resistant to Change**
> Your friend says: "My workplace uses enormous amounts of single-use plastic — disposable cups, cutlery, packaging — and I've suggested more sustainable alternatives multiple times. My manager is politely dismissive and colleagues seem indifferent. I feel like I'm fighting alone. What advice would you give me?"

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Noise From a Nearby Construction Project**
> Your friend says: "A major construction project has started outside my apartment and the noise begins every morning at 7am and continues until 6pm. I work from home and I can barely function. I've checked and the hours are apparently within city bylaws. What can I do? What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Neighbour's Dog Causing Problems**
> Your friend says: "My neighbour's dog barks for hours whenever they leave the house, and has twice dug under the fence into my yard, frightening my children. My neighbour is defensive when I bring it up and insists their dog is well-behaved. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Feeling Guilty About Flying Given Environmental Values**
> Your friend says: "I care deeply about the environment and I know that flying is one of the most carbon-intensive things a person can do. But I also want to visit family abroad and explore the world. I feel like a hypocrite every time I book a flight. How do I reconcile this? What advice would you give?"

---

**Q24. Invasive Species in the Garden — What to Do**
> Your friend says: "My backyard has been taken over by an invasive plant species that keeps spreading despite my efforts to remove it. It's now spreading into neighbouring yards and one neighbour has complained. I don't know whether to hire a specialist, use chemical treatments, or keep trying naturally. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q25. Community Association Dominated by a Few Voices**
> Your friend says: "I've attended a few meetings of my neighbourhood community association and the same three people dominate every discussion and effectively make all the decisions. Newer residents like me feel our perspectives are ignored. I want to contribute but I don't know how to get my voice heard. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Energy Consumption — Hydro Bill Shocking**
> Your friend says: "My electricity bill has tripled since last winter and I can't figure out why. My habits haven't changed significantly. I've asked the utility company and they say usage seems normal for my area, but the numbers don't make sense to me. I'm worried about costs going forward. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Rodent or Pest Problem in the Neighbourhood**
> Your friend says: "There has been a significant increase in rats in my neighbourhood recently, linked to a construction site nearby. My own yard has been affected and I've started seeing them near my home. I don't know whether this is something individuals can address or whether the city needs to be involved. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q28. Buying Local and Seasonal Food — Hard to Maintain**
> Your friend says: "I committed to buying only locally grown and seasonal food for environmental reasons, but I'm finding it very limiting, more expensive, and harder to maintain than I expected. My family has complained about the reduced variety. I don't want to completely abandon my values. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Light Pollution Affecting Sleep and Wildlife**
> Your friend says: "A new commercial building near my home has bright lights that shine directly into our bedroom windows all night. I've contacted the building management twice with no response. It's affecting our sleep and I've read it's harmful to local wildlife too. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q30. Feeling Overwhelmed by Community Problems — Compassion Fatigue**
> Your friend says: "I've been deeply involved in several community causes for years — environmental advocacy, food bank volunteering, neighbourhood safety — and I've reached a point where I feel completely burnt out. I care as much as I ever did but I have nothing left to give. What advice would you give me?"

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Rewilding the Garden — Neighbours Complaining**
> Your friend says: "I've converted my front lawn into a wildflower meadow and natural garden to support pollinators and reduce water use. I love it, but several neighbours have complained that it looks untidy and one has threatened to report it to the city. I believe strongly in what I'm doing. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Becoming Vegan for Environmental Reasons — Family Resistance**
> Your friend says: "I've decided to become vegan primarily for environmental reasons. My family is resistant — they don't want to change the food we share at meals and my parents see it as a rejection of our cultural food traditions. It's becoming a source of serious conflict. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Corporate Greenwashing — Feeling Manipulated**
> Your friend says: "I've been buying products from a company that marketed itself as environmentally responsible. I recently discovered that most of their green claims are exaggerated or outright misleading. I feel manipulated and I don't know which companies I can actually trust anymore. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Abandoned Property in the Neighbourhood — What Can Residents Do?**
> Your friend says: "There is an abandoned property on my street that has become an eyesore — overgrown, attracting pests, and used by strangers at night. It makes our street feel unsafe. I've tried to find out who owns it with no luck. Is there anything residents can actually do about this? What advice would you give?"

---

**Q35. Water Shortage — Neighbours Ignoring Restrictions**
> Your friend says: "Our municipality has issued mandatory water use restrictions due to a drought, but several neighbours are openly ignoring them — watering their lawns daily and washing their cars. I don't know whether to report them or speak to them directly, and I'm not sure either approach will accomplish anything. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Neighbour's Tree Damaging Shared Property**
> Your friend says: "A very large tree on my neighbour's property has branches constantly falling into my yard, and the roots are now visibly cracking our shared fence and may be affecting my home's foundation. I've brought it up twice and my neighbour insists the tree is fine. I don't know whether I can legally force them to act or what my options are. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. No Safe Play Space for Children in the Neighbourhood**
> Your friend says: "I live in a dense urban neighbourhood and there is no safe outdoor space nearby where my children can play freely. The nearest park is a 20-minute walk through busy streets. I want to push for a local playground or green space but I don't know how to start or who to approach. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Wildfire Smoke Season Affecting Daily Life and Health**
> Your friend says: "Every summer, wildfire smoke fills the air for weeks at a time. My children can't go outside, I've developed respiratory symptoms, and working from home with the windows sealed in the heat is miserable. I feel powerless against something this large. What practical advice would you give me for protecting my family and managing this situation?"

---

**Q39. Short-Term Rentals Changing the Character of the Building**
> Your friend says: "Several units in my apartment building have been converted to short-term rentals and it has changed everything — there are strangers in the hallways every week, noise at all hours, and no one feels responsible for common areas. The building no longer feels like a community. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Shared Alley Used as an Illegal Dumping Ground**
> Your friend says: "The alleyway shared by the homes on my street has become a dumping ground — people are leaving broken furniture, bags of garbage, and construction materials. It's attracting rats and creating a health hazard. I've reported it to the city twice but nothing has been done. What advice would you give me?"
