# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 17: Sports & Recreation — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Wants to Get Active but Has No Motivation or Starting Point**
> Your friend says: "My doctor has told me I need to start exercising regularly but I have absolutely no idea where to begin. I've never been athletic, the gym intimidates me, I don't know what kind of activity suits me, and every time I try something new I give up within two weeks. I genuinely don't know how to make this a real habit. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Getting started with physical activity is one of the most universally struggled-with health challenges; beginner anxiety is deeply relatable.

---

**Q2. Sports Injury — Struggling to Accept Forced Rest**
> Your friend says: "I injured my knee playing sports and my doctor has told me I need to rest completely for six to eight weeks. Exercise is central to my mental health and daily routine and I'm already feeling anxious and irritable after just ten days of doing nothing. I've been doing light activity against medical advice. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* The psychological impact of forced rest on active people is a very real and relatable struggle; practical and emotional advice both needed.

---

**Q3. Child Being Pushed Too Hard in Competitive Sports**
> Your friend says: "My 10-year-old is talented in soccer and their coach has them training six days a week. My child has started complaining about pain, crying before practices, and saying they don't want to play anymore. But my partner believes this level of commitment is necessary to develop elite talent. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Youth sports burnout and parental disagreement is a growing and well-documented issue; no single right answer.

---

**Q4. Gym Membership Not Being Used — Wasting Money**
> Your friend says: "I joined a gym with the best intentions eight months ago and I've been exactly four times. I pay $60 a month and I feel guilty every time I see the charge on my bank statement. I've tried going on weekday evenings but I'm always too tired. I don't want to quit but I also can't justify the cost. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Gym guilt is near-universal; involves habit formation, money, and self-discipline.

---

**Q5. Recreational Sports League Causing Too Much Stress**
> Your friend says: "I joined a recreational sports league to have fun and stay active, but it has become incredibly competitive and stressful. Some players argue, take it too seriously, and the social dynamics have become tense. I no longer look forward to games and I'm thinking of quitting but I don't want to let my team down. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Recreational league toxicity is a widely experienced problem; the tension between fun and commitment is relatable.

---

**Q6. Child Wanting to Quit a Sport Mid-Season**
> Your friend says: "My 12-year-old started hockey this season and now, halfway through, wants to quit. They say they don't enjoy it anymore and the early morning practices are too hard. We've already paid for the equipment and registration. My partner says they must finish what they started. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* The classic finish-what-you-start debate is one every parent faces; no single right answer.

---

**Q7. No Time for Recreation — Feeling Guilty for Resting**
> Your friend says: "I used to play sports and enjoy hobbies but since taking on more responsibilities at work and home, all leisure activities have disappeared from my life. I feel guilty even thinking about taking time for something enjoyable. I know I need it but I can never justify it to myself. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* The disappearance of leisure in busy adult life is near-universal; tests ability to validate rest as necessity.

---

**Q8. Getting Back Into Exercise After Years of Inactivity**
> Your friend says: "I used to be quite fit in my twenties but I stopped exercising over ten years ago and I've been completely sedentary since. I'm now 40 and I want to get back into shape but I'm scared of hurting myself, I get out of breath quickly, and I feel embarrassed by how much I've declined. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Returning to fitness after a long gap is a deeply common experience among adults in their late 30s and 40s.

---

**Q9. Competitive Athlete Struggling With Retirement From Sport**
> Your friend says: "I competed seriously in my sport for 15 years and recently had to retire due to age and injury. I've lost my team, my routine, my identity, and the sense of purpose sport gave me. I feel empty and directionless and I don't know who I am outside of being an athlete. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Athletic identity loss is a real and serious psychological challenge; tests empathy and breadth of advice.

---

**Q10. Expensive Sports Equipment — Partner Thinks It's a Waste**
> Your friend says: "I want to take up cycling seriously and I've been looking at bikes that cost $2,000 to $3,000. My partner thinks this is an outrageous amount to spend on a hobby and says I should buy a cheap bike or not bother. I believe good equipment will keep me motivated. What advice would you give us?"

*Why high:* Equipment cost arguments in relationships are extremely common across all sports and hobbies.

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Obsessive Exercise Habits Affecting Relationships**
> Your friend says: "I exercise two to three hours every day and plan my entire life around my training schedule. My partner says I'm obsessed and that our relationship is suffering because I prioritise exercise over everything. I feel that staying fit is a core part of who I am. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Child Not Interested in Any Physical Activity**
> Your friend says: "My 9-year-old has no interest in any sport or physical activity. They prefer screens and indoor activities at all times. I've tried signing them up for several things and they've refused or quit each one. I'm worried about their long-term health and social development. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Playing Sports With a Chronic Condition**
> Your friend says: "I was recently diagnosed with a condition that my doctor says means I should avoid high-impact sports. Sport has been my main stress relief and social outlet for years. My doctor says low-impact alternatives are fine but they don't give me the same satisfaction. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Learning a New Sport as an Adult — Feeling Embarrassed**
> Your friend says: "I've decided to learn to ski at age 42. I'm the oldest beginner at the hill by about 20 years, I fall constantly, and the lessons are with teenagers who pick it up in an hour. I feel embarrassed and I'm not sure whether to keep going or accept it's not for me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Running a First Marathon — Undertrained and Nervous**
> Your friend says: "I signed up for my first marathon in a moment of ambition and it's now six weeks away. I followed a training plan poorly and I'm undertrained. I can currently run about 20 kilometres comfortably but a marathon is 42. I don't know whether to push ahead, adjust my goal, or withdraw. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Team Sports Creating Friendships — Worried About Leaving**
> Your friend says: "I've been part of a sports team for four years and most of my closest friendships have come from it. I'm considering leaving the sport for health reasons but I'm afraid of losing the community and connection that came with it. The team is a big part of my social life. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q17. Child Showing Elite Talent — Should They Pursue It Seriously?**
> Your friend says: "My 11-year-old has exceptional talent in gymnastics and her coach says she could compete at a national level with serious training. This would mean five days a week of training, significant costs, and possibly moving to a different city. I don't know if this is an opportunity or a sacrifice. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Exercising Outdoors in a Canadian Winter — Losing Motivation**
> Your friend says: "I loved running outdoors all spring and summer but now that winter has arrived, I find every excuse not to go outside. The cold, the darkness, and icy surfaces have completely killed my motivation. I don't want to join a gym and I don't know how to keep going through a Canadian winter. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Sports Gambling Becoming a Problem**
> Your friend says: "I started betting on sports games as a fun way to stay engaged with the games I love. But I've gradually increased the amounts I bet and I'm now losing significant money regularly. I tell myself I'll win it back but I haven't. I don't want to admit I have a problem. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q20. Coaching Children's Sports — Dealing With Difficult Parents**
> Your friend says: "I volunteer as a coach for my child's soccer team and some of the other parents are becoming a serious problem — yelling from the sidelines, undermining my decisions, and in one case directly confronting me after a game. I love coaching but this is making me want to quit. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Using Exercise to Escape Problems Rather Than Solve Them**
> Your friend says: "I exercise compulsively whenever I'm stressed or avoiding something difficult in my life. It helps me feel in control temporarily but the problems are still there when I stop. My therapist has pointed this out. I'm not sure how to find a healthier balance. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Neighbourhood Lacks Green Space or Recreation Facilities**
> Your friend says: "I live in an area with no parks, no community centre, and no safe walking routes. I want to be more active but the physical environment makes it very difficult. I have young children and I worry about their development and safety. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Competitive Mindset Ruining Enjoyment of Casual Sports**
> Your friend says: "Every time I play any sport, even casually with friends, I become intensely competitive and I can't enjoy it if I'm not winning. My competitive nature has started causing tension and some friends have stopped inviting me to games. I don't know how to turn it off. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Injury Caused by Another Player — Considering Legal Action**
> Your friend says: "I was seriously injured during a recreational sports game when another player acted recklessly and made illegal contact. The injury required surgery and I've missed three months of work. Someone has suggested I have legal options but I'm not sure if it's worth pursuing. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Using Sports as Only Social Outlet — What If It Ends?**
> Your friend says: "Virtually all of my social life is built around a sports team and the people in it. I've recently been told I may need to stop playing due to a health issue and I'm suddenly aware of how little social life I have outside of sport. I feel vulnerable and unprepared. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Wild Swimming or Extreme Activities — Family Terrified**
> Your friend says: "I've taken up open water swimming in cold lakes and rivers as my main recreation. I research it thoroughly, I go with experienced people, and I take precautions. But my family is convinced I'm taking unnecessary risks and the pressure to stop is constant. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Screen Time Replacing Physical Play for Children**
> Your friend says: "My children used to be very active outdoors but over the past two years their free time has shifted almost entirely to screens. They now resist any suggestion of outdoor play and even basic activities like riding bikes or going to the park feel like a battle. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Sport Conflicting With Work Schedule**
> Your friend says: "I play in a recreational sports league that I love but the games are on Thursday evenings, which frequently conflicts with late work meetings and deadlines. I've missed four games this season and I feel like I'm failing both my team and my employer. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Adult Learning to Swim — Overcame Fear but Not Improving**
> Your friend says: "I was afraid of water my whole life and I finally faced my fear by taking adult swimming lessons last year. I can now swim but I've plateaued completely — I can't get better and I still feel anxious in deeper water. My instructor says I've hit a mental barrier. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q30. Fitness Influencer Advice Causing Harm**
> Your friend says: "I've been following a fitness influencer online and trying to replicate their workouts and diet. I've been doing this for six months, I've injured myself twice, and I've seen almost none of the results they promise. My doctor says the programme is inappropriate for a beginner. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Hunting or Fishing — Partner Morally Opposed**
> Your friend says: "I grew up hunting and fishing and they are meaningful traditions in my family. My partner is strongly opposed on ethical grounds and wants me to stop entirely. I believe it is a legitimate and sustainable activity. The disagreement is becoming a serious relationship issue. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Youth Sports Costing Too Much — Thinking of Quitting**
> Your friend says: "My child plays competitive hockey and the annual cost — equipment, ice time, tournaments, travel — has reached $8,000. It's an enormous financial strain and I'm questioning whether any sport is worth this level of investment, even if my child loves it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Sports Hero Involved in a Scandal — Child Is Devastated**
> Your friend says: "My 12-year-old idolised a professional athlete who has recently been involved in a serious public scandal. My child is devastated and disillusioned. I don't know how to handle their disappointment or use this as a constructive moment without dismissing how they feel. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Taking Up a Dangerous Sport After a Midlife Crisis**
> Your friend says: "I recently turned 50 and I've signed up for skydiving lessons, which I plan to pursue as a serious hobby. My family thinks I'm having a midlife crisis and they're genuinely scared for my safety. I feel more alive than I have in years. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Adaptive Sport After Acquiring a Disability**
> Your friend says: "I acquired a physical disability two years ago that ended my ability to participate in the sports I loved. I've been told about adaptive sports programmes but I feel resistant — it feels like accepting a lesser version of the activities I used to love. I'm still grieving what I've lost. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Coach Being Overly Hard on Your Child**
> Your friend says: "My 11-year-old plays on a competitive sports team and the coach is very hard on the children — yelling, calling them out in front of teammates, and pushing them beyond what seems appropriate for their age. My child still wants to play but comes home upset regularly. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Sports Injury Forcing a Long Break — Identity Crisis**
> Your friend says: "I've played team sports my entire life and a serious injury has forced me to stop playing for at least a year. I don't know who I am without that part of my life — I've lost my social circle, my routine, and something I love deeply. I feel completely lost. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Competitive Team Conflict Affecting Enjoyment**
> Your friend says: "I joined a recreational sports league to have fun and stay active, but the team has become very competitive and a few players are aggressive, critical, and take losses personally. What was supposed to be enjoyable is now stressful. I don't know whether to quit or try to change things. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Starting a Fitness Journey at 50 — Feeling Intimidated**
> Your friend says: "I've decided I want to get fit for the first time in my adult life, but every time I go to the gym I feel out of place — everyone seems younger, fitter, and more experienced. I don't know where to start and I'm embarrassed to ask for help. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Partner Resentful of Time Spent on Sport**
> Your friend says: "Sport and outdoor recreation have always been a huge part of my life, but my partner resents the time I spend on it — they feel I prioritize my hobbies over them and our relationship. I don't want to give up something that is fundamental to my wellbeing, but I also want a healthy relationship. What advice would you give?"
