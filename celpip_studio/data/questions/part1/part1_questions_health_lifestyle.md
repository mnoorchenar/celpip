# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 2: Health & Lifestyle — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, relatable, multiple valid advice paths, common in Canadian health context*

---

**Q1. Sedentary Lifestyle — No Time to Exercise**
> Your friend says: "I know I need to exercise but I work long hours, come home exhausted, and can never find the motivation to go to the gym. I've tried starting routines before but always quit after a week. My doctor says I need to get active or my health will suffer. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Nearly universal situation; easy to give practical advice; emotionally relatable.

---

**Q2. Quitting Smoking**
> Your friend says: "I've been smoking for 15 years and I know it's destroying my health. I've tried to quit three times already but always go back after a few weeks. The stress at work makes the cravings worse. I want to quit for good this time but I don't know where to start. What would you advise?"

*Why high:* Classic advice scenario with emotional weight and clear stakes.

---

**Q3. Unhealthy Eating Habits and Weight Gain**
> Your friend says: "I've gained a lot of weight over the past year because of stress eating and fast food. I know it's bad for me but healthy food is expensive and I don't have time to cook. My clothes don't fit anymore and I feel terrible about myself. What advice can you give me?"

*Why high:* Extremely relatable; involves practical advice on diet, budget, and self-esteem.

---

**Q4. Chronic Stress and Anxiety**
> Your friend says: "I've been feeling anxious all the time lately — my heart races, I can't focus, and I wake up in the middle of the night worrying about everything. I know it's stress but I don't know how to manage it. I don't want to take medication. What would you suggest?"

*Why high:* Mental-health adjacent but framed as lifestyle; very relatable; tests empathy and range of advice.

---

**Q5. Not Going to the Doctor — Avoiding Medical Help**
> Your friend says: "I've had a nagging pain in my back for months and I keep putting off going to the doctor. I'm scared of what they might find, and honestly I don't like hospitals. My family keeps telling me to go but I always make excuses. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Fear-based avoidance is widely relatable; allows advice about overcoming fear and health responsibility.

---

**Q6. Sleep Problems and Insomnia**
> Your friend says: "I haven't been sleeping well for months. I lie awake for hours, my mind keeps racing, and I wake up exhausted. I've tried sleeping pills but I don't want to rely on them. It's affecting my work and my mood. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Sleep issues are extremely common; practical and lifestyle advice both apply.

---

**Q7. Caring for an Ill Parent While Managing Your Own Life**
> Your friend says: "My mother was diagnosed with a serious illness and I've become her main caregiver. I'm spending all my free time taking care of her, neglecting my own health, and I feel guilty any time I do something for myself. I'm exhausted but I don't know how to ask for help. What should I do?"

*Why high:* Deeply emotional; tests ability to give sensitive and practical advice.

---

**Q8. Alcohol as a Coping Mechanism**
> Your friend says: "Lately I've been drinking a glass of wine every night after work to relax and de-stress. It's become a habit I rely on, and I've noticed I need more than before to feel the same effect. My partner has mentioned it a few times. I don't think I have a problem, but maybe I do. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Sensitive but common; has multiple advice directions; no single right answer.

---

**Q9. Mental Health — Feeling Depressed but Refusing Help**
> Your friend says: "I've been feeling really low for a long time. I don't enjoy anything anymore, I've stopped seeing friends, and I cry for no reason. My family says I should see a therapist but I feel embarrassed and I don't think talking to a stranger will help. What would you advise?"

*Why high:* Mental health is a major topic in Canada; tests empathy and knowledge of resources.

---

**Q10. Burnout and Exhaustion — Needing Rest but Can't Stop**
> Your friend says: "I run on coffee and adrenaline every day. I skip lunch, stay up late, and even work on weekends. My body is telling me to slow down — I get headaches, I'm always tired, and I keep getting sick. But I feel like I can't afford to rest. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Overlaps work-life balance and health; extremely common and relatable.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Healthy Eating on a Tight Budget**
> Your friend says: "I want to eat healthier but nutritious food is so expensive. I'm a student on a very limited budget and most of the healthy options at the grocery store are out of my price range. I end up buying cheap processed food because it's all I can afford. Any advice?"

---

**Q12. Screen Time Addiction — Phone and Social Media**
> Your friend says: "I spend hours every day scrolling through my phone and social media. I pick it up first thing in the morning and last thing at night. I know it's affecting my sleep, my attention span, and my real-life relationships, but I can't seem to stop. What would you advise?"

---

**Q13. Starting an Exercise Routine — No Motivation**
> Your friend says: "I bought a gym membership six months ago and I've only gone three times. Every time I plan to go, something comes up or I just don't feel like it. I really want to get fit but I can't make myself do it consistently. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Chronic Pain — Living With Ongoing Physical Discomfort**
> Your friend says: "I've had chronic back pain for two years. The doctors have done tests and there's nothing seriously wrong, but the pain never goes away. It's stopping me from doing the things I love and making me irritable and miserable. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q15. Resisting a Doctor's Advice — Refusing Treatment**
> Your friend says: "My doctor recommended a procedure for a health issue I have, but I've been reading online and I'm nervous about side effects. I keep delaying the appointment. My family is worried but I feel like I need more information before agreeing. What would you advise?"

---

**Q16. Extreme Dieting and Body Image Issues**
> Your friend says: "I've been trying every diet I can find — cutting carbs, fasting, meal replacement drinks. I lose a little weight and then gain it all back. I feel obsessed with food and my weight, and it's making me miserable. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q17. Quitting Alcohol Completely**
> Your friend says: "I've decided to stop drinking alcohol entirely because I feel it's been negatively affecting my health and relationships. But all my social events revolve around drinking and I'm afraid of being excluded or judged. How can I handle this?"

---

**Q18. Overweight Child — Parent Asking for Advice**
> Your friend says: "My 10-year-old child has gained a lot of weight and the doctor says it could affect their health. I don't want to make them feel bad about their body, but I'm worried. I'm not sure how to encourage healthier habits without damaging their self-esteem. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q19. Managing Diabetes or Chronic Illness Through Lifestyle**
> Your friend says: "I was recently diagnosed with Type 2 diabetes. My doctor says I can manage it with diet and exercise changes without medication — but I don't know where to begin. I love food and I've never exercised regularly. How would you suggest I approach this?"

---

**Q20. Taking Mental Health Days Off Work**
> Your friend says: "I'm mentally exhausted and I need a day off just to rest and recover, but I feel guilty taking a sick day when I'm not physically ill. My workplace doesn't really recognize mental health as a valid reason to be absent. What would you advise me to do?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Partner Has Unhealthy Habits — How to Help Without Conflict**
> Your friend says: "My partner eats badly, never exercises, and refuses to go to the doctor even though they have a few concerning symptoms. Every time I try to bring it up, they get defensive and we argue. I'm worried but I don't know how to help without creating conflict. What should I do?"

---

**Q22. Mindfulness and Meditation — Sceptical but Struggling**
> Your friend says: "My doctor suggested I try mindfulness or meditation for my stress and anxiety. I'm sceptical — I've never done it and I feel like it won't work for someone like me. But I'm willing to try anything at this point. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q23. Health Anxiety — Constantly Worried About Illness**
> Your friend says: "I spend a lot of time researching symptoms online and I constantly worry that I have a serious disease. I've visited the doctor several times and tests come back normal, but the worry never goes away. It's affecting my daily life. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Recovering From a Sports or Physical Injury**
> Your friend says: "I injured my knee playing sports three months ago and the doctor said I need to rest completely, but I miss exercising so much that I've been sneaking short runs. The pain keeps coming back. I know I should rest but I can't help myself. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q25. Trying Alternative Medicine vs. Conventional Treatment**
> Your friend says: "I've been struggling with a health issue for a year and conventional medicine hasn't helped much. A friend recommended acupuncture and herbal remedies. My doctor thinks it's a waste of money, but I'm open to trying anything. What would you advise?"

---

**Q26. Helping an Elderly Parent Stay Active and Healthy**
> Your friend says: "My father is 75 and since he retired, he barely leaves the house. He watches TV all day, eats poorly, and has become isolated. He refuses to join any senior activities. His health is declining and I'm worried. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Postpartum Recovery — Struggling After Having a Baby**
> Your friend says: "I gave birth four months ago and I still feel physically and emotionally exhausted. I'm not sleeping, I've lost my sense of self, and I feel like I should be happy but I'm not. My partner doesn't fully understand what I'm going through. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q28. Cutting Out Sugar — Struggling With Cravings**
> Your friend says: "I've decided to cut out sugar from my diet because of my health, but the cravings are overwhelming. I do well for a few days and then have a complete binge. I feel like I have no willpower. How can I get past this?"

---

**Q29. Balancing Social Drinking With a Healthy Lifestyle**
> Your friend says: "I'm trying to live healthier and I've cut back on alcohol, but my friends and family always pressure me to drink at gatherings. I don't want to be antisocial or offend anyone, but I also want to stick to my health goals. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q30. Fear of Aging and Declining Health**
> Your friend says: "I just turned 50 and I'm suddenly very aware of getting older. My energy levels have dropped, I've started getting aches and pains, and I feel like my health is declining rapidly. It's making me anxious and a bit depressed. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Vaccination Hesitancy**
> Your friend says: "I'm unsure about whether to get a recommended vaccine. I've read some concerns online and I'm not sure who to trust. My doctor is recommending it, but I'm nervous. How would you advise me to make this decision?"

---

**Q32. Extreme Sports and Physical Risk**
> Your friend says: "I've taken up rock climbing and my family is terrified I'll get seriously hurt. It's the only thing that makes me feel truly alive, but I understand their concern. My partner has asked me to stop. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Grief Affecting Physical Health**
> Your friend says: "Since my father passed away six months ago, my health has deteriorated. I've lost my appetite, I'm not sleeping, and I feel physically unwell most of the time. My doctor says grief is affecting my body. How can I take care of myself while I'm grieving?"

---

**Q34. Trying to Conceive — Lifestyle Changes Needed**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I have been trying to have a baby for a year without success. The doctor said we both need to make significant lifestyle changes — less stress, better diet, more sleep, no alcohol. The changes feel overwhelming. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q35. Chronic Fatigue Syndrome or Unexplained Exhaustion**
> Your friend says: "I've had blood tests and all my results are normal, but I'm exhausted every single day regardless of how much I sleep. My doctors seem confused and I feel dismissed. It's affecting every part of my life. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Trying Alternative Medicine After Conventional Treatment Failed**
> Your friend says: "I've been dealing with a chronic condition for three years and conventional medicine hasn't helped much. A friend recommended an alternative practitioner and I'm tempted to try it, but I'm not sure if it's safe or evidence-based, and I'm afraid my doctor will disapprove. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Impatient to Return to Normal After Surgery**
> Your friend says: "I had major surgery six weeks ago and I know I need more recovery time, but I'm going stir-crazy at home, I'm falling behind at work, and I keep pushing my body before it's ready. My doctor says I'm not following the recovery plan properly. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Partner Ignoring Signs of a Serious Health Problem**
> Your friend says: "My husband has had symptoms that concern me for months — fatigue, unexplained weight loss, and pain he tries to laugh off. He refuses to see a doctor because he's afraid of what they might find. I'm scared but I can't force him to go. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Medical Test Results Causing Disproportionate Anxiety**
> Your friend says: "I had a routine health screening and one of the results was slightly outside the normal range. My doctor says it's not a concern and I should monitor it, but I've spent weeks researching worst-case scenarios online and I'm in a constant state of panic. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q40. Feeling Judged for Lifestyle Choices at the Doctor Office**
> Your friend says: "Every time I visit my doctor, I feel judged about my weight, my diet, and my lifestyle. The comments feel dismissive of whatever I've actually come in for. I'm starting to avoid going even when I really need to. What advice would you give me?"
