# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 6: Housing & Home — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Landlord Refusing to Fix Serious Problems**
> Your friend says: "My apartment has had a mold problem in the bathroom for four months and my landlord keeps promising to fix it but never does. My children have been getting sick and I'm worried about our health. I don't want to cause trouble but I also can't keep living like this. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Tenant rights issues are extremely common in Canada; involves health, family, and legal dimensions.

---

**Q2. Rent Increase — Can No Longer Afford the Apartment**
> Your friend says: "My landlord just told me my rent is going up by 25% next year. I've lived here for five years and I love the apartment, but I simply cannot afford the new price. I don't know whether to try to negotiate, look for a new place, or look into my legal rights as a tenant. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Rent increases are one of the most pressing housing issues in Canadian cities right now — highly topical.

---

**Q3. Noisy or Disruptive Neighbours**
> Your friend says: "My upstairs neighbours are extremely loud — they stomp around late at night, play loud music, and have parties on weeknights. I've knocked on their door twice and they were polite but nothing has changed. I'm not sleeping well and it's affecting my work. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Neighbour conflicts are near-universal; tests ability to balance confrontation with practical solutions.

---

**Q4. Buying a First Home — Feeling Overwhelmed**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I are trying to buy our first home and we feel completely overwhelmed. We don't understand mortgages, we're not sure how much we can afford, and the market moves so fast that we keep losing out on properties. We're exhausted and discouraged. What advice would you give us?"

*Why high:* First-time homebuyer anxiety is extremely common; practical and emotional advice both needed.

---

**Q5. Roommate Not Paying Their Share**
> Your friend says: "I share an apartment with a friend and for the past three months they've been late or short on their share of the rent. I've been covering the difference to avoid problems with our landlord, but I'm running out of patience and money. I don't want to lose the friendship but I can't keep doing this. What advice can you give me?"

*Why high:* Shared living conflicts are widely relatable, especially among young adults and students.

---

**Q6. Home Renovation Gone Wrong — Contractor Issues**
> Your friend says: "I hired a contractor to renovate my kitchen. They took a large deposit, started the work, and then disappeared for three weeks. Now they've returned and the work is poor quality — nothing is as agreed. I've already paid $15,000 and I'm not sure how to handle this legally or practically. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Contractor fraud and renovation disputes are very common in Canada; high financial stakes.

---

**Q7. Moving to a New City — Where to Live?**
> Your friend says: "I'm moving to a new city for a job and I have no idea where to look for housing. I don't know the neighbourhoods, I can't visit in person before the move, and I'm worried about ending up somewhere unsafe, too expensive, or too far from work. I only have two weeks to decide. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Relocation is a very common experience, especially for immigrants and young professionals.

---

**Q8. Living With In-Laws — Creating Tension**
> Your friend says: "My spouse and I moved in with my in-laws six months ago to save money for a home of our own. But the living situation has become very tense — my mother-in-law interferes in our daily life and my spouse doesn't see the problem. I feel like I have no privacy or autonomy. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Multi-generational living is common in immigrant families; combines housing with relationship conflict.

---

**Q9. Neighbour Dispute Over Property Boundaries**
> Your friend says: "My neighbour recently built a fence that I believe extends into my property. I've tried to bring it up politely but they insist the fence is in the right place. The dispute is getting hostile and I'm not sure whether to get a surveyor, involve the city, or try to work it out another way. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Property boundary disputes are common homeowner conflicts; practical and legal advice needed.

---

**Q10. Unsafe or Substandard Rental — Afraid to Complain**
> Your friend says: "The apartment I'm renting has several serious problems — a broken heating system, electrical issues, and a door lock that doesn't work properly. My landlord is aggressive whenever I bring anything up and I'm afraid that if I push too hard, they'll find a reason to evict me. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Fear of retaliation in rental situations is a very real concern, especially for newcomers to Canada.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Should They Sell the Family Home After a Divorce?**
> Your friend says: "My spouse and I are separating and we can't agree on what to do with the house we own together. I want to sell and split the proceeds but my spouse wants to stay in the house and buy me out — at a price I think is too low. We can't afford to keep arguing and paying two lawyers. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q12. Deciding Whether to Renovate or Move**
> Your friend says: "Our home has become too small for our growing family but we love our neighbourhood and don't want to move. Renovating would cost about $80,000 and cause months of disruption. Moving to a bigger home would mean a much higher mortgage. We can't decide what to do. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q13. Short-Term Rental Neighbour Causing Problems**
> Your friend says: "My next-door neighbour has been renting their home on a short-term rental platform and there are new strangers coming and going every few days. There have been noise issues, garbage problems, and I no longer feel safe in my own neighbourhood. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. First-Time Renter — Worried About Being Taken Advantage Of**
> Your friend says: "I'm looking for my first apartment and I'm nervous about signing a lease. I don't know what to look for, what my rights are, or how to avoid bad landlords. I've already seen a couple of places that felt suspicious. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q15. Home Flooding or Serious Damage — Insurance Dispute**
> Your friend says: "My basement flooded during a heavy rainstorm and caused significant damage. When I filed an insurance claim, the company said the damage isn't covered under my policy. I'm looking at $30,000 in repairs and I feel completely helpless. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Downsizing After Children Leave Home**
> Your friend says: "My children have all moved out and my husband and I are now rattling around in a five-bedroom house. The maintenance costs are high and the upkeep is exhausting, but we've lived here for 25 years and the thought of leaving is emotional. My husband doesn't want to move. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q17. Student Housing — Overcrowded and Difficult Conditions**
> Your friend says: "I'm a university student and I'm living in a house with five other people. It's overcrowded, dirty, and my housemates don't respect common areas. I can't afford to move because it's close to campus and cheap. I still have eight months left on my lease. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q18. Elderly Parent's Home Is No Longer Safe**
> Your friend says: "My father lives alone in the family home and recently had a fall. The house has stairs, poor lighting, and no safety features. He refuses to move or make modifications because he wants to preserve the home exactly as it was when my mother was alive. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q19. Buying a Home With a Friend — Having Second Thoughts**
> Your friend says: "Six months ago I agreed to buy a property jointly with a close friend because neither of us could afford to buy alone. But now I'm having serious doubts — we have very different lifestyles and I'm worried about what happens if one of us wants to sell. We haven't signed yet. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q20. Neighbourhood Has Changed — Feeling Unsafe**
> Your friend says: "The neighbourhood I've lived in for eight years has changed significantly — there's been an increase in crime, my car was broken into twice, and I don't let my kids play outside anymore. I love my home but I don't feel safe. Moving would mean a much higher mortgage. What advice can you give?"

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Hoarder Family Member — Home Has Become Unlivable**
> Your friend says: "My mother has developed a serious hoarding problem. Her home is so full of objects that it's become a health and safety hazard. She refuses to let anyone throw anything away and becomes very distressed when we bring it up. We're worried about her safety. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q22. Pest Infestation — Landlord Blaming Tenant**
> Your friend says: "My apartment has a serious cockroach infestation and my landlord is claiming it's my fault and refusing to pay for extermination. I keep my apartment very clean and I believe the problem is coming from the building itself. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Buying in a Hot Market — Feeling Pressured to Waive Conditions**
> Your friend says: "We found a house we love but our realtor is telling us we need to make an offer without a home inspection condition to be competitive. I'm nervous about buying without knowing the condition of the home, but I also don't want to lose it. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q24. Dispute With a Condo Board**
> Your friend says: "I own a condo and the condo board has approved a special assessment fee of $8,000 for building repairs that I think are unnecessary. I disagree with the decision but I don't know what my rights are as an owner or whether it's worth challenging. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q25. Multigenerational Home — Building a Secondary Suite**
> Your friend says: "We're considering converting part of our home into a separate suite for my aging parents to move into. It would cost around $50,000 and requires permits. My spouse is supportive but worried about loss of privacy. My parents are hesitant about losing their independence. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q26. Remote Work Changing Housing Needs**
> Your friend says: "Since I started working from home full-time, my apartment feels too small and I have no dedicated workspace. I'm considering moving to a larger place in a cheaper area farther from the city, but I'm worried my company might eventually call everyone back to the office. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q27. Landlord Entering Without Notice**
> Your friend says: "My landlord has been entering my apartment without giving me proper notice — sometimes showing up unannounced to check on things. I feel my privacy is being violated but I'm afraid that if I confront them, they'll make my living situation difficult. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q28. Mortgage Renewal — Interest Rates Have Risen**
> Your friend says: "My mortgage is coming up for renewal and interest rates have risen significantly since I first bought my home. My monthly payment is going to increase by hundreds of dollars, which I'm not sure I can afford. I don't know whether to renew, switch lenders, or sell. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q29. Neighbour's Tree Damaging Your Property**
> Your friend says: "My neighbour has a large old tree whose roots are cracking my driveway and branches are falling on my roof. I've mentioned it to them but they say it's not their responsibility. My insurance company says the same. I'm facing expensive repairs. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q30. Living Far From Family — Considering Moving Back**
> Your friend says: "I moved to a new city five years ago for work and built a life here, but my parents are getting older and I feel guilty being so far away. My hometown doesn't have great job opportunities in my field. I feel torn between my career and my family. What advice can you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Tiny Home or Alternative Living — Family Disapproves**
> Your friend says: "I'm seriously considering buying a tiny home or moving into an intentional community to reduce my expenses and environmental footprint. My family thinks I've lost my mind and that it will affect my career and social life. I genuinely believe it's the right choice for me. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q32. Eviction — Facing Homelessness**
> Your friend says: "I received an eviction notice because my landlord says they need the unit for a family member. I've lived here for seven years and I can't find anything affordable in the same area. I have two young children and I'm terrified about where we'll go. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Historical or Heritage Home — Restoration Challenges**
> Your friend says: "I bought an old heritage home because I love its character, but the restoration costs are far higher than I expected. I'm not allowed to make certain changes because of heritage restrictions, and every repair is more complex and expensive than a regular home. I'm starting to regret the purchase. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q34. Considering Moving to Another Country for Housing Affordability**
> Your friend says: "Housing in Canada has become so expensive that I'm seriously considering moving to another country where I could actually afford to own a home and have a comfortable life. My family and friends are all here and I'd be starting over. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Neighbours Opposing a New Development Nearby**
> Your friend says: "The city has approved a large apartment complex to be built right next to my house. I'm worried about noise during construction, decreased property value, more traffic, and less privacy. Some neighbours are organizing to oppose it. Should I join them? What advice would you give?"

---

**Q36. Basement Suite With Mold Problem — Landlord Will Not Act**
> Your friend says: "I've been living in a basement suite for two years and there is visible mold in the bathroom and bedroom. I've reported it to my landlord three times in writing but nothing has been done. I'm worried about my health but I can't afford to move right now. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Neighbourhood Changing Fast — Considering Moving**
> Your friend says: "The neighbourhood I've lived in for ten years has changed dramatically — long-time residents have moved away, local businesses have closed, and I no longer feel at home here. I'm considering moving but I've built community roots here and I don't know if that's the right call. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q38. Parents Wanting to Move In — No Space**
> Your friend says: "My aging parents want to move in with us, and I understand why — they need support and the arrangement would help financially. But our home is small, we have two young children, and I'm genuinely worried about how it would work. I don't want to seem selfish. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Home Renovation Project Spiralling Over Budget**
> Your friend says: "We started a kitchen renovation that was budgeted at $15,000 but we're now $30,000 in with no clear end in sight. The contractor keeps finding additional problems and asking for more money. We can't stop midway but we're running out of funds. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q40. Renting Out a Room — Difficult Tenant Situation**
> Your friend says: "I rented out a room in my home to help cover my mortgage, but my tenant is frequently late with rent, ignores the house rules, and makes me uncomfortable in my own home. I don't want to go through the formal eviction process but I need this to change. What advice would you give me?"
