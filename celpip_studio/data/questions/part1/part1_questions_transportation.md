# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 12: Transportation & Commuting — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Exhausting Daily Commute Affecting Quality of Life**
> Your friend says: "My daily commute is three hours each way by public transit. After two years of this, I'm constantly exhausted, I have no time for family or personal life, and my health is suffering. My job is good but I'm not sure any job is worth this. I don't know whether to move closer, find another job, or find a way to cope. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Long commutes are a defining reality of Canadian city life; involves housing, career, and wellbeing trade-offs.

---

**Q2. Buying a First Car — Overwhelmed by Choices**
> Your friend says: "I need to buy my first car but I'm completely overwhelmed — new vs. used, lease vs. buy, what brand, what features. I don't know much about cars and I'm afraid of being taken advantage of by a dealer or ending up with something unreliable. Where do I even start? What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* First car purchase is a major life milestone; many newcomers to Canada face this for the first time; practical advice needed.

---

**Q3. Car vs. Public Transit — Can't Decide**
> Your friend says: "I'm trying to decide whether to buy a car or rely entirely on public transit. A car would give me freedom and save time, but the cost of insurance, gas, and parking is enormous. Public transit is cheaper but slow and unreliable in my area. I can't decide which is better for my situation. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* A classic Canadian cost-of-living dilemma; relevant to nearly every working adult in a major city.

---

**Q4. Learning to Drive as an Adult — Too Nervous**
> Your friend says: "I'm 35 years old and I've never learned to drive. Back home I didn't need to, but here in Canada I feel completely dependent on others and limited in where I can work and live. Every time I try a driving lesson I become extremely anxious and my instructor is losing patience. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Directly mirrors the experience of many adult immigrants in Canada who never needed to drive in their home country.

---

**Q5. Car Accident — Dealing With Insurance and Stress**
> Your friend says: "I was in a car accident last month. Thankfully no one was seriously hurt but my car was badly damaged and the insurance process has been a nightmare. The other driver's insurance is disputing fault and my own insurer seems unhelpful. I'm driving a rental and I don't know how to navigate this. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Car accidents and insurance disputes are common experiences; tests knowledge of practical next steps.

---

**Q6. Elderly Parent No Longer Safe to Drive — Taking Away the Keys**
> Your friend says: "My 78-year-old father has had two minor accidents recently and his reaction time is clearly slowing. I believe he should stop driving but he refuses — his car is his independence and he becomes very angry whenever I raise the topic. I'm scared he'll hurt himself or someone else. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* One of the most emotionally difficult conversations adult children face; no easy solution.

---

**Q7. Unreliable Car Causing Stress and Job Risk**
> Your friend says: "My car keeps breaking down and I've spent over $3,000 on repairs in the past year. Last week it broke down on my way to work and I was late for an important meeting. I can't afford a new car right now but I also can't keep relying on something so unreliable. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Vehicle reliability vs. financial constraints is a common and stressful dilemma.

---

**Q8. Driving Anxiety After an Accident**
> Your friend says: "Since I was in a car accident eight months ago I've developed a serious fear of driving. I used to drive confidently every day but now I avoid highways, panic at intersections, and have even had to pull over because of anxiety. It's affecting my job and my independence. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Driving anxiety following trauma is common and often untreated; practical and psychological advice both apply.

---

**Q9. New Driver — High Insurance Costs**
> Your friend says: "I just got my full driver's licence and I was shocked by how expensive insurance is for new drivers in Canada. The quotes I'm getting are higher than my rent. I need a car for work but I don't know how I can possibly afford this. Are there ways to reduce the cost? What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* High insurance for new drivers is a genuine pain point in Canada, especially for newcomers who start from scratch.

---

**Q10. Commuting by Bike — Family Thinks It's Too Dangerous**
> Your friend says: "I've started cycling to work to save money and improve my fitness. The route is manageable but my family is convinced it's dangerously risky and puts pressure on me every day to stop. I've had one minor incident but nothing serious. I find cycling energising and I don't want to give it up. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Cycling as commuting is growing rapidly in Canadian cities; safety vs. freedom tension is rich for advice.

**Q11. Road Rage — Struggling to Control Anger While Driving**
> Your friend says: "I've always been a calm person but behind the wheel I become a completely different person — I get furious at other drivers, tailgate, and have made gestures I'm ashamed of. I've never had an incident but I'm scared of where my anger could lead. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Carpooling With a Coworker — Creating Tension**
> Your friend says: "I've been sharing a daily carpool with a coworker for six months to save on costs, but it's becoming a problem. They're frequently late, play music I find grating, and use the commute time to vent about work in a way that stresses me out before I've even arrived. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q13. Moving Closer to Work — Leaving a Neighbourhood They Love**
> Your friend says: "I've been offered a significant promotion but the new office is on the other side of the city. If I take it without moving, my commute doubles to two and a half hours each way. Moving would mean leaving a neighbourhood I love and my children's school. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Considering an Electric Vehicle — Unsure if It's Practical**
> Your friend says: "I'm thinking about buying an electric vehicle because of the fuel savings and environmental benefits, but I'm worried about the charging infrastructure, range anxiety on long trips, and the higher upfront cost. My current car is old and needs replacing. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Public Transit in a New City — Completely Lost**
> Your friend says: "I recently moved to a large Canadian city and the public transit system is nothing like what I used back home. I've been late to work three times in my first two weeks because I took the wrong route or missed my transfer. I feel embarrassed and stressed every morning. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Driving Record Affecting Employment — Licence Suspension**
> Your friend says: "I received several speeding tickets last year and my licence was suspended for three months. My driving record is now poor and I've found out it's affecting my ability to get certain jobs that require driving. I've learned my lesson but I don't know how to move forward. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q17. Teen Wanting to Learn to Drive — Parent Terrified**
> Your friend says: "My 16-year-old is legally old enough to start driving lessons and is very eager to get their licence. I'm terrified — I know the statistics about young drivers and I can barely watch when they talk about getting behind the wheel. I don't want to hold them back but I'm struggling. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Parking Problems in the City — Affecting Daily Life**
> Your friend says: "Parking in my area has become a constant source of stress. I've received several expensive parking tickets, been towed once, and spend 20 minutes circling my neighbourhood every evening. It's costing me money and patience. I don't know whether the solution is to get rid of my car or find another way. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q19. Remote Area With No Public Transit — Feeling Isolated Without a Car**
> Your friend says: "I live in a suburban area where there is essentially no public transit. Without a car I can't get to work, grocery stores, or appointments. I don't drive and can't currently afford a car. I feel completely stranded and dependent on the goodwill of others. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q20. Distracted Driving — Knows It's Wrong but Can't Stop Checking Phone**
> Your friend says: "I know checking my phone while driving is dangerous and illegal, but I do it constantly — messages, notifications, sometimes even browsing. I've been lucky so far but I know I'm putting myself and others at risk. I've told myself to stop dozens of times. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q21. Vehicle Purchase Regret — Bought the Wrong Car**
> Your friend says: "I bought a large SUV six months ago and I regret it. The fuel costs are much higher than I expected, parking it in the city is a nightmare, and I feel like I overpaid. I still owe money on the loan so I can't easily sell it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Commuting With Young Children — Exhausting Logistics**
> Your friend says: "Every morning I have to drop two children at different daycares before getting myself to work by transit. The whole process takes two hours and I arrive at work already exhausted and stressed. I feel like my day is over before it begins. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Used Car Purchase — Discovered Hidden Problems**
> Your friend says: "I bought a used car two weeks ago from a private seller and since then I've discovered several serious mechanical issues that weren't disclosed. I paid $8,000 and now I'm facing repair costs of $3,000. I don't know if I have any recourse. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q24. Considering Giving Up Driving for Environmental Reasons**
> Your friend says: "I've decided I want to stop driving entirely because of the environmental impact of car ownership. I live in a city where it's theoretically possible to manage without one, but it would require significant lifestyle changes. My family thinks it's extreme. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Work Trip Reimbursement Dispute With Employer**
> Your friend says: "I regularly use my personal car for work-related travel and my employer is consistently late reimbursing me, or disputes the mileage I claim. I've spent hundreds of dollars out of pocket waiting for reimbursement. I don't know whether to push back harder or just stop using my car for work. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q26. Driving in Winter Conditions — First Winter in Canada**
> Your friend says: "This is my first winter in Canada and I'm terrified of driving in snow and ice. I've slid on icy roads twice already and once nearly had an accident. People here seem completely unfazed by conditions that I find genuinely dangerous. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Rideshare Dependency — Spending Too Much**
> Your friend says: "I don't have a car and I rely on rideshare apps for almost everything — commuting, groceries, appointments. My monthly spend on rideshares is more than a car payment would cost. I know it's not financially sustainable but I'm not sure I'm ready for the responsibility of owning a car. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Flight Cancelled — Stranded in Another City**
> Your friend says: "My flight home was cancelled due to bad weather and the airline is not offering hotels or compensation — they say it's a weather event and out of their control. I'm stranded in a city I don't know with no accommodation arranged and a limited budget. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Work Requires Frequent Long-Distance Travel — Family Suffering**
> Your friend says: "My job requires me to travel across the country every week — I'm away Monday to Thursday almost every week. My spouse and children miss me, important family events keep happening without me, and I'm exhausted from constant travel. The pay is good but I'm questioning whether it's worth it. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q30. Running a Red Light — Caught on Camera, Disputing the Fine**
> Your friend says: "I received a red light camera fine in the mail. I believe I was already through the intersection legally before the light changed but the photo looks ambiguous. The fine is $400 and disputing it means taking time off work to go to court. Is it worth contesting? What advice would you give me?"

**Q33. Driving With a Medical Condition — Doctor Advises Against It**
> Your friend says: "My doctor recently told me that a medical condition I have may affect my reaction time and that I should consider stopping driving. I live in a suburban area with no transit options. My entire life — work, family, independence — depends on being able to drive. What advice would you give me?"

**Q34. Road Trip Across Canada — Planning From Scratch**
> Your friend says: "I want to do a solo road trip across Canada — something I've dreamed of since immigrating here. I have four weeks of vacation and a reliable car, but I've never planned a road trip of this scale and I don't know where to start. My family thinks it's too ambitious. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Autonomous and Self-Driving Vehicles — Afraid to Try**
> Your friend says: "My employer has provided a company car that has a semi-autonomous driving mode. My colleagues love using it on the highway, but I'm uncomfortable handing control to a machine and have refused to use the feature. I feel left behind but I genuinely don't trust the technology yet. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Car Accident Causing Ongoing Fear of Driving**
> Your friend says: "I was involved in a car accident six months ago and I've been unable to drive since. Even as a passenger I feel panicked. I need to drive for work and family obligations but the anxiety is overwhelming. I've been told it will pass but it hasn't. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Long Commute Destroying Work-Life Balance**
> Your friend says: "My daily commute is three hours round trip and it's taking a serious toll on my health, my family time, and my happiness. My employer won't allow remote work and I can't afford to move closer. I feel trapped in a situation that is slowly wearing me down. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Teen Driver Using the Family Car Irresponsibly**
> Your friend says: "My 17-year-old has a licence and uses our family car, but I've found out they've been speeding and driving with friends late at night in violation of the rules we set. I'm terrified something will happen. Taking the car away creates huge conflict. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Unreliable Public Transit Making You Late to Work Repeatedly**
> Your friend says: "The bus I rely on is late or doesn't show up multiple times a week and I've been late for work repeatedly as a result. My employer is losing patience and I'm stressed every morning. I can't afford a car and I don't have other reliable options. What advice would you give me?"

I can't afford to make a costly mistake on this purchase. What advice would you give me?"
