# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 20: Volunteer & Community Service — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Wants to Volunteer but Doesn't Know Where to Start**
> Your friend says: "I've been wanting to volunteer in my community for a long time — I feel like I should be giving back, especially since moving to Canada. But every time I look into it, I'm overwhelmed by the options and I'm not sure which cause or organisation is a good fit for me, my schedule, or my skills. I don't know how to begin. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Wanting to volunteer but not knowing how to start is the single most common barrier to community engagement; especially relevant for newcomers building roots in Canada.

---

**Q2. Volunteer Role Becoming as Demanding as a Second Job**
> Your friend says: "I started volunteering at a local organisation about a year ago and over time I've taken on more and more responsibilities. It now demands 15 to 20 hours of my week — I'm attending meetings, managing projects, and being relied upon as if I were a paid staff member. I don't want to let people down but I'm burning out. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Volunteer scope creep is an extremely common experience; tests advice on boundaries, burnout prevention, and saying no with integrity.

---

**Q3. Feeling Undervalued and Unappreciated as a Volunteer**
> Your friend says: "I've been volunteering at the same organisation for two years and I feel completely taken for granted. My contributions are never acknowledged, my suggestions are ignored, and I've watched paid staff get credit for work I've done. I care about the cause but I'm starting to feel invisible. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Volunteer recognition and appreciation is a well-documented issue in the nonprofit sector; emotional resonance is high.

---

**Q4. Pressure From Employer to Volunteer — Feels Inauthentic**
> Your friend says: "My company has a corporate social responsibility programme and there is strong implicit pressure to participate in company-organised volunteer days. I feel the volunteering is performative and I'd rather choose my own cause. But opting out feels like it could hurt my standing at work. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Corporate volunteering pressure vs. authentic engagement is a growing and widely experienced tension in Canadian workplaces.

---

**Q5. Volunteer Organisation With Poor Leadership — Should They Stay or Leave?**
> Your friend says: "I volunteer for an organisation whose mission I deeply believe in, but the leadership is disorganised, decisions are made without transparency, and there is clear favouritism among volunteers. I've tried raising concerns and been brushed off. I don't know whether to stay and keep trying or leave and find somewhere better. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Dysfunctional volunteer organisations are extremely common; the tension between mission loyalty and self-respect is emotionally rich.

---

**Q6. Child Not Interested in Any Community Involvement**
> Your friend says: "I believe strongly in community service and I want my teenage child to develop that same sense of civic responsibility. But every time I suggest volunteering or community involvement they refuse or show complete indifference. I don't want to force it but I also don't want to give up on instilling this value. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Passing civic values to children is a near-universal parenting concern; the coercion vs. inspiration dilemma has no single right answer.

---

**Q7. Witnessing Misconduct While Volunteering — What to Do**
> Your friend says: "While volunteering at a local charity I've noticed what I believe are financial irregularities — donations not being properly recorded and expenses that seem personal being approved. I'm not certain I'm interpreting it correctly but I'm troubled. I don't know whether to report it, raise it internally, or stay silent and keep my head down. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Ethical dilemmas in volunteer settings are serious and real; tests ability to give careful, responsible advice.

---

**Q8. Volunteering to Build Canadian Experience — Feels Exploitative**
> Your friend says: "I'm a recent immigrant and I've been volunteering in my professional field to gain Canadian experience because employers keep telling me I lack it. But I'm doing skilled work for free that paid employees do, and I'm not sure if I'm building experience or just being exploited. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Unpaid volunteering as a pathway to employment is a defining and controversial experience for many immigrant professionals in Canada.

---

**Q9. Conflict Between Two Volunteers Affecting the Whole Group**
> Your friend says: "I'm involved in a volunteer group where two long-standing members are in a serious personal conflict. It's created factions, poisoned the atmosphere, and several volunteers have already quit. I'm not the leader but I care about the group and I want to help without making things worse. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Interpersonal conflict in volunteer groups is a leading cause of volunteer attrition; tests both conflict resolution and bystander responsibility advice.

---

**Q10. Wanting to Start a Community Initiative but Lacking Confidence**
> Your friend says: "I've identified a genuine gap in my community — a need that isn't being addressed by any existing organisation — and I have an idea for a small initiative to help. But I have no experience starting anything, I'm not sure anyone would follow my lead, and the idea of putting myself out there feels terrifying. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* The desire to lead community change alongside fear of initiative and failure is deeply relatable; empowering and practical advice both needed.

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Volunteering Abroad — Is It Actually Helpful?**
> Your friend says: "I've been offered the chance to volunteer abroad for two weeks at an orphanage in a developing country. I've been reading articles suggesting that short-term voluntourism can do more harm than good. I want to help but I don't want my good intentions to cause harm. How do I decide? What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Volunteer Role Involving Vulnerable People — Feeling Out of Depth**
> Your friend says: "I recently started volunteering at a shelter for people experiencing homelessness and mental health crises. I care deeply about the cause but I often feel completely out of my depth — I don't know how to respond to distressing situations and I worry I'm doing more harm than good. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Being Asked to Take on a Leadership Role in a Volunteer Group**
> Your friend says: "The organisation I volunteer for has asked me to take on a leadership role — coordinating other volunteers, running meetings, and liaising with the board. I'm honoured but I have no leadership experience and I'm worried about failing people who are counting on me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Family Doesn't Support Time Spent Volunteering**
> Your friend says: "I spend several hours a week volunteering and my partner and family see it as wasted time — time I could be spending with them, building my career, or earning money. They support the idea of helping others in principle but resent the actual time it takes. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Volunteer Burnout After an Emotionally Demanding Role**
> Your friend says: "I've been volunteering as a crisis line responder for 18 months. The work is meaningful but I've absorbed a great deal of secondary trauma — I think about the calls at night, I feel drained before every shift, and I've lost the sense of purpose I started with. I don't know if I should push through or step back. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Donating Money vs. Donating Time — Which Matters More?**
> Your friend says: "I want to contribute to causes I care about but I genuinely don't have much free time — I work long hours and have young children. A friend tells me donating money is actually more impactful than volunteering time. I feel guilty for not showing up in person. How should I think about this? What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q17. Volunteer Group Divided Over Direction and Values**
> Your friend says: "The volunteer group I'm part of has split into two factions over the direction the organisation should take — one side wants to expand the scope significantly and the other wants to stay focused on the original mission. I care about both perspectives but the conflict is paralysing the group's ability to do anything. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Using Volunteering to Cope With Loneliness — Becoming Dependent**
> Your friend says: "After moving to a new city where I knew no one, I threw myself into volunteering as a way to meet people and feel connected. It worked — but I've realised I now use it as a substitute for building deeper personal relationships. I'm surrounded by people in my volunteer roles but I'm still lonely. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Asked to Volunteer for Something Against Personal Values**
> Your friend says: "A community organisation I respect has asked me to volunteer at an event that raises funds for a cause I have personal objections to. I want to maintain the relationship with the organisation and the people involved, but I can't in good conscience support this particular cause. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q20. Difficulty Maintaining Commitment When Motivation Fades**
> Your friend says: "I signed up to volunteer for a year-long commitment but six months in my motivation has dropped significantly. Life has gotten busier, the work is more repetitive than I expected, and I'm starting to resent the obligation. But I made a promise and people are counting on me. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Volunteering in a Role That Doesn't Match Skills or Interests**
> Your friend says: "I signed up to volunteer at an organisation I admire and was assigned to a role that has nothing to do with my skills or interests — administrative data entry instead of the community outreach I wanted to do. I've been told there's no other role available for now. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Raising Money for Charity — Feeling Awkward Asking People**
> Your friend says: "I've signed up for a charity fundraising run and I need to raise $500 from people I know. I find asking people for money deeply uncomfortable, even for a good cause. I'm worried about coming across as pushy, and I've barely raised anything with three weeks to go. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Volunteer Group Excluding Newcomers**
> Your friend says: "I joined a volunteer group six months ago and there is a clear inner circle of long-standing members who make decisions informally among themselves and exclude newer volunteers. I want to contribute fully but I feel like an outsider with no path in. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Community Project Failing Due to Lack of Participation**
> Your friend says: "I helped organise a community project — a local resource exchange — that started with real energy but participation has steadily dropped. Fewer and fewer people are showing up and I'm close to doing all the work alone. I'm not ready to give up but I don't know how to re-engage the community. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Managing Disagreements Between Volunteers and Paid Staff**
> Your friend says: "In the organisation where I volunteer, there is visible tension between the paid staff and volunteers — staff seem to resent volunteers being given responsibilities, and volunteers feel disrespected by staff. I'm caught in the middle and it makes the environment uncomfortable for everyone. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Volunteering With Children — Unexpected Emotional Weight**
> Your friend says: "I volunteer at an after-school programme for children from difficult home situations. I genuinely love the work but I've become very attached to some of the children and the stories I hear stay with me long after I leave. I'm not sure how to maintain healthy emotional boundaries without becoming detached. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. School Requiring Volunteer Hours — Child Treating It as a Chore**
> Your friend says: "My child's school requires 40 hours of community service to graduate. My child is doing the minimum possible, choosing the easiest options, and treating it as a bureaucratic obligation. I want them to genuinely engage with the experience, not just tick a box. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Stepping Back From a Volunteer Role Without Burning Bridges**
> Your friend says: "I've decided I need to step back from my volunteer role because of changes in my personal life. But the organisation depends on me heavily and I know my leaving will create problems. I want to step back with integrity but I'm worried about the fallout. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Volunteering at the Same Organisation as a Difficult Person**
> Your friend says: "I've recently discovered that someone I have a troubled personal history with has joined the same volunteer organisation as me. I don't want to leave a cause I care about, but working alongside this person creates serious discomfort. I don't think a confrontation would help. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q30. Recognition for Volunteer Work Causing Jealousy Among Others**
> Your friend says: "I was publicly recognised by my volunteer organisation for my contributions and since then a few other volunteers have become noticeably cold and distant toward me. I didn't seek the recognition and I feel uncomfortable that something positive has created this tension. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Volunteer Organisation Asking for Personal Donations Too**
> Your friend says: "The organisation I volunteer many hours for has begun asking me to also make financial donations. I give my time generously but I'm not in a position to donate money as well. The asks are getting harder to decline and I'm starting to feel that my time contribution isn't considered enough. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Disagreeing With the Methods of a Cause You Support**
> Your friend says: "I deeply believe in the cause an advocacy organisation is working toward, but I find their tactics — aggressive public campaigns, confrontational messaging — counterproductive and uncomfortable to be associated with. I want to support the goal without endorsing the methods. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Volunteer Work Being Used on a Resume — Is It Appropriate?**
> Your friend says: "I've been doing significant and skilled volunteer work for two years — project management, communications, and event coordination — that is directly relevant to the jobs I'm applying for. I'm not sure whether including it as experience on my resume is appropriate or whether employers will dismiss it as 'just volunteering.' What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Organising a Charity Event That Is Going Over Budget**
> Your friend says: "I volunteered to organise a charity fundraiser for my community and costs have escalated beyond what we budgeted. We're already committed to vendors and venues and the shortfall could mean the event raises nothing for the charity at all. I'm the one who took on this responsibility and I don't know how to fix it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Feeling That Volunteering Isn't Making Any Real Difference**
> Your friend says: "I've been volunteering regularly for three years across a couple of organisations and I've started to feel deeply cynical — like the problems I'm trying to help with are structural and systemic, and that individual volunteer efforts are ultimately insignificant. I don't want to stop caring but I'm struggling to find meaning in the small contributions I make. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Volunteer Role Given to Someone Less Experienced**
> Your friend says: "I've been volunteering with an organization for two years and I recently found out that a leadership role I wanted and was qualified for was given to someone with much less experience, apparently because of personal connections. I'm disappointed and questioning whether to stay. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Burning Out From Too Many Volunteer Commitments**
> Your friend says: "I've taken on so many volunteer roles over the years that I'm now completely exhausted and starting to resent the work I once loved. But I feel guilty even thinking about stepping back because I know these organizations depend on me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Volunteer Organization Found to Have Ethical Problems**
> Your friend says: "I've been volunteering with an organization I believed in deeply, but I recently discovered credible reports that their leadership has been misappropriating funds and treating staff poorly. I'm shocked and don't know whether to resign, speak up internally, or report it externally. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Fundraising for a Cause No One in Your Network Supports**
> Your friend says: "I'm fundraising for a cause that is genuinely important to me, but it's not one that my friends, family, or colleagues seem to care about — most of them don't respond to my requests and a few have been dismissive. I'm disheartened but I still believe in the cause. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Asked to Lead a Community Group — Feeling Unqualified**
> Your friend says: "The members of a community group I've been part of for a year are asking me to step up as their new leader. I care about the group and I'd like to contribute more, but I genuinely don't feel qualified to lead — I don't have formal training and I'm afraid of letting people down. What advice would you give me?"
