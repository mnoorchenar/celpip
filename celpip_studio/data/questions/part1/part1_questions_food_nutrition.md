# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 16: Food & Nutrition — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Too Busy to Cook — Relying on Fast Food Every Day**
> Your friend says: "Between work, commuting, and looking after my children, I have almost no time to cook. I've been relying on fast food and takeout almost every day and I know it's affecting my health and my wallet. I want to eat better but by the time I get home I have no energy to cook a proper meal. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Time poverty and poor eating habits is one of the most universal health and lifestyle challenges; practical and behavioural advice both apply.

---

**Q2. Trying to Lose Weight but Nothing is Working**
> Your friend says: "I've been trying to lose weight for two years. I've tried cutting carbs, intermittent fasting, meal replacement shakes, and joining a gym. I lose a little weight each time and then gain it all back. I'm frustrated, discouraged, and I'm starting to believe my body just won't change. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Weight management frustration is near-universal; rich emotional component combined with practical advice.

---

**Q3. Picky Eater Child — Every Meal Is a Battle**
> Your friend says: "My 7-year-old will eat only about six different foods and refuses everything else — sometimes gagging dramatically when new food is placed in front of them. Mealtimes have become miserable. My mother-in-law says I've spoiled them. My paediatrician says not to force it. I don't know what to do. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Picky eating in children is a near-universal parenting struggle; emotionally loaded with conflicting advice from every direction.

---

**Q4. Partner Has Completely Different Eating Habits**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I have completely incompatible eating styles — I'm trying to eat healthily and they want comfort food every night. Cooking two different meals every day is exhausting and expensive, but whenever I try to introduce healthier options we end up arguing. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Food incompatibility in relationships is extremely common; involves compromise, health, and relationship dynamics.

---

**Q5. Grocery Budget Too High — Struggling to Eat Well Affordably**
> Your friend says: "I'm trying to feed a family of four on a very tight budget. Healthy food is expensive and I feel like I have to choose between spending too much or feeding my family poorly. I see people talk about meal planning but I don't know where to start or whether it actually makes a difference. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Food affordability is a top-of-mind issue for many Canadian families right now; practical advice needed.

---

**Q6. Diagnosed With a Condition Requiring a Dietary Change**
> Your friend says: "My doctor recently told me I need to make significant changes to my diet because of a health condition — less salt, less sugar, no processed food, more vegetables. I know I need to do this but I genuinely don't enjoy healthy food and I have no idea how to cook these kinds of meals. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Medical dietary change is a common life event; involves motivation, cooking skills, and long-term habits.

---

**Q7. Emotional Eating — Food as a Response to Stress**
> Your friend says: "Whenever I'm stressed, anxious, or sad, I eat — large amounts of whatever is available, usually unhealthy food, often in secret. I feel ashamed and out of control but I can't seem to stop. Food is the only thing that reliably makes me feel better in the moment. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Emotional eating is one of the most common disordered eating patterns; psychological and practical advice both needed.

---

**Q8. Teenager Refusing to Eat Healthily — Surviving on Junk Food**
> Your friend says: "My 15-year-old eats almost exclusively junk food — chips, energy drinks, fast food, and sugary snacks. They refuse anything I cook that isn't processed and unhealthy. I'm worried about their long-term health but every conversation about food becomes a fight. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Teen nutritional habits are a major parenting concern; generational conflict over food adds rich texture.

---

**Q9. Eating Traditional Food vs. Adapting to Canadian Diet**
> Your friend says: "Since moving to Canada, I've been trying to adapt my eating habits to fit in — trying local foods, changing what I cook. But I miss my traditional food deeply and I feel like I'm losing a piece of my identity every time I choose a sandwich over what I grew up eating. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Food as cultural identity is a profound and specific concern for immigrant CELPIP test-takers; emotionally layered.

---

**Q10. Severe Food Allergy — Managing Social and Work Situations**
> Your friend says: "I have a severe food allergy that requires me to be extremely careful at all times. I find social situations around food very stressful — dinner parties, work lunches, restaurants — because I worry about cross-contamination and I don't want to be seen as difficult or demanding. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Food allergies are increasingly common in Canada; social anxiety around dietary restrictions is widely relatable.

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Meal Planning — Can't Make It Stick**
> Your friend says: "I've tried meal planning many times because I know it would save me time and money. But I always start enthusiastically, plan for two weeks, and then abandon it by day four when something unexpected comes up or I just don't feel like eating what I planned. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Feeding a Family With Multiple Dietary Requirements**
> Your friend says: "In my household I have a vegetarian, one person with a dairy allergy, a picky toddler, and a partner who wants meat at every meal. Cooking has become an exhausting puzzle every night. I barely enjoy it anymore. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Considering Becoming Vegetarian or Vegan — Unsure How to Start**
> Your friend says: "I want to significantly reduce or eliminate meat from my diet for health and environmental reasons, but I've eaten meat my whole life and I have no idea how to get enough protein or cook satisfying meals without it. My family is also very resistant. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Skipping Breakfast — Doctor Says It's Affecting Health**
> Your friend says: "I never eat breakfast. I'm not hungry in the morning, I'm always rushed, and the thought of eating early makes me feel slightly nauseous. My doctor says this habit is contributing to my energy crashes and weight gain later in the day. I don't know how to change a habit I've had for 20 years. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Over-Reliance on Processed and Packaged Foods**
> Your friend says: "Almost everything I eat comes from a package — frozen meals, canned soups, instant noodles, pre-made sauces. I know real cooking would be healthier and cheaper but I grew up eating this way and I don't know how to cook from scratch. The idea of starting feels overwhelming. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Child Gaining Weight — Parent Doesn't Know How to Address It**
> Your friend says: "My 10-year-old has gained significant weight over the past year and the doctor says it is becoming a health concern. I want to make changes but I'm terrified of damaging their relationship with food or their body image. I don't know how to improve their diet without making them feel ashamed. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q17. Alcohol Calories Undermining Weight Loss Efforts**
> Your friend says: "I've been exercising regularly and eating better but I'm not losing weight. A friend pointed out that I drink wine most evenings and that the calories might be cancelling out my efforts. I enjoy my evening glass and I'm reluctant to cut it out. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Eating Disorders in the Family — How to Help**
> Your friend says: "I've noticed that my 17-year-old daughter is barely eating, obsessively reading nutrition labels, and becomes very anxious around food. She denies anything is wrong. I'm afraid of what this could be developing into but I don't want to make things worse by handling it badly. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Navigating Traditional Cultural Food at Family Gatherings**
> Your friend says: "I've been told I need to cut out certain foods for health reasons, but at every family gathering these are the main dishes. Refusing to eat feels offensive to my family and the host, and explaining my dietary restrictions causes tension. I don't know how to manage this respectfully. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q20. Children Refusing to Eat Vegetables — Every Trick Has Failed**
> Your friend says: "I've tried hiding vegetables in sauces, making them fun shapes, letting my children help cook, and offering rewards. Nothing works for long. My kids point-blank refuse to eat most vegetables and I'm genuinely worried about their nutrition. What advice can you give me?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Orthorexia — Becoming Obsessive About Healthy Eating**
> Your friend says: "I've been on a health kick for the past year and I've become very strict about what I eat — I've cut out dozens of foods, I research every ingredient, and I feel intense anxiety if I eat something I consider unhealthy. My friends say I've gone too far. I think I'm just being disciplined. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Expensive Health Food Trend — Worth the Cost?**
> Your friend says: "I've been buying superfoods, organic everything, specialty supplements, and health-store products on the advice of social media influencers. My grocery bill has tripled. I'm not sure how much of it is genuinely beneficial and how much is just marketing. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Night-Time Eating — Waking Up to Snack**
> Your friend says: "I wake up at 2 or 3 in the morning almost every night and eat — sometimes large amounts. I'm fully awake when it happens. I barely remember it sometimes and I'm not hungry during the day. My sleep is disrupted and my weight is increasing. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Cooking for One — Unmotivated and Wasteful**
> Your friend says: "I live alone and I find it completely demotivating to cook for just myself. I end up wasting food because recipes make too much, eating the same thing repeatedly, or just not bothering and eating crackers for dinner. I know it's not healthy but I can't seem to change. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Restaurant Portion Sizes and Overeating When Eating Out**
> Your friend says: "I eat out frequently for work and social reasons and I almost always overeat because portions are enormous and I feel awkward leaving food. The combination of rich food and large portions has been steadily affecting my weight and energy levels. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Toddler Not Eating Enough — Parent Panicking**
> Your friend says: "My 2-year-old has gone through a phase of barely eating anything for the past three weeks. Some days they have only a few bites all day. They're active and seem happy but I'm frightened they're not getting enough nutrition. My paediatrician says not to worry but I can't help it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Eating on a Night Shift — Struggling With Irregular Hours**
> Your friend says: "I work night shifts and my eating schedule is completely out of sync with my body. I eat large meals in the middle of the night, have no appetite for breakfast when I wake up, and I've noticed real changes in my energy and weight since I started this schedule. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Pressure to Diet From Family Members**
> Your friend says: "My mother and aunt constantly comment on my weight and tell me I need to go on a diet whenever we're together. Their comments are affecting my mental health and my relationship with food. I don't want to damage the family relationship but I can no longer tolerate it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Raw Food or Extreme Diet — Partner Worried About Nutrition**
> Your friend says: "I've started following a raw food diet that my partner thinks is nutritionally dangerous. I feel better than I have in years and I've done my research, but my partner is constantly worried and keeps sending me articles about deficiencies. The tension is affecting our relationship. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q30. Child With Food Allergies — School Environment Concerns**
> Your friend says: "My child has a severe nut allergy and I send them to school every day genuinely afraid. The school has allergy policies but I've seen children bring peanut products anyway, and the supervision at lunchtime doesn't feel adequate. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Fermented Foods and Gut Health — Confused by Conflicting Information**
> Your friend says: "I've been reading about gut health and the importance of fermented foods, probiotics, and the microbiome. I want to improve my health but the information is overwhelming and contradictory. I don't know what to actually change in my diet or whether any of it is reliable. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Food Insecurity — Not Having Enough to Eat**
> Your friend says: "I'm going through a very difficult financial period and there are days when I don't have enough food in the house. I have children to feed and I feel deeply ashamed. I don't know what resources are available to me or how to ask for help without feeling judged. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Cooking Traditional Food in Canada — Ingredients Hard to Find**
> Your friend says: "I love cooking traditional food from my home country but many of the key ingredients are difficult or expensive to find here. I've tried substitutes but the taste is never quite right. Cooking this food is important to me for cultural and emotional reasons. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Partner Following Fad Diet — Affecting the Whole Household**
> Your friend says: "My partner has started a very restrictive fad diet and now insists we only keep specific foods in the house and that I cook only diet-approved meals. I'm supportive of their health goals but the diet is extreme and I feel like my own food preferences no longer matter at all. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Difficulty Maintaining Diet While Travelling for Work**
> Your friend says: "I eat well at home but my job requires me to travel frequently and I completely fall apart nutritionally on the road — hotel breakfasts, client dinners, airport food. I come home from every trip feeling terrible and having undone a week of good habits. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Elimination Diet Causing Social Isolation**
> Your friend says: "I've been following a strict elimination diet for a health condition and it has completely changed my social life — I can't eat at most restaurants, I feel awkward at dinner parties, and people think I'm being difficult. I believe the diet is helping me but I feel increasingly isolated. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Child With Extremely Limited Diet — Parent Worried**
> Your friend says: "My seven-year-old will only eat about eight foods and refuses everything else. It's been this way for years — no vegetables, no new textures, nothing unfamiliar. My paediatrician is not concerned but I'm terrified about their nutrition and development. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Partner Dismissing Healthy Eating Efforts**
> Your friend says: "I've been making a real effort to cook healthier meals at home but my partner constantly complains about the food — they want their old favourites back and sometimes order fast food behind my back. I feel unsupported and I'm starting to lose my motivation. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q39. Food Influencer Making Dangerous Dietary Claims**
> Your friend says: "Someone I follow online is promoting an extreme diet that they claim cured their chronic illness. Their content is very convincing and I'm tempted to try it, but some of their advice seems to go against everything my doctor has told me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Pressure to Eat at Family Gatherings Despite Dietary Restrictions**
> Your friend says: "Every time I attend a family gathering, relatives pressure me to eat foods I've been avoiding for genuine health and personal reasons. When I decline they take it personally and there is always an argument. I love seeing my family but I dread the food battles. What advice would you give me?"
