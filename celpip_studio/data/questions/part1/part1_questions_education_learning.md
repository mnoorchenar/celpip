# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 4: Education & Learning — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Dropping Out of University — Feeling Lost**
> Your friend says: "I'm in my second year of university and I'm completely lost. I don't enjoy my program, my grades are poor, and I dread going to class every day. My parents sacrificed a lot to send me to school and I feel like a failure. I'm thinking about dropping out but I don't know if that's a terrible decision. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Extremely common among young adults; involves family expectations, finances, and identity — no easy answer.

---

**Q2. Choosing the Wrong Program — Wanting to Switch**
> Your friend says: "I'm halfway through a nursing degree and I've realized it's not for me — I find the work distressing and I don't feel suited for it. But I've already spent two years and a lot of money on this program. My parents want me to finish. I'm torn between continuing something I hate or starting over. What would you advise?"

*Why high:* Sunk cost dilemma combined with family pressure — a rich, realistic scenario.

---

**Q3. Going Back to School as an Adult**
> Your friend says: "I'm 38 years old and I want to go back to school to get a degree in a new field. My friends think it's too late and that I'll be in class with people half my age. I have a family and a part-time job. I'm excited but very nervous. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Directly relevant to many CELPIP test-takers; mature student experience is widely relatable.

---

**Q4. Child Struggling Academically — Parent Seeking Advice**
> Your friend says: "My 12-year-old has been struggling at school for the past year — failing several subjects, not doing homework, and saying they hate school. Their teacher says they might need to repeat the year. I'm worried and frustrated but I don't know if I should push harder or take a different approach. What advice can you give me?"

*Why high:* Parenting + education crossover; high emotional stakes; multiple valid responses.

---

**Q5. Pressure to Pursue a Prestigious Degree vs. Following Passion**
> Your friend says: "My parents want me to study medicine or law because they believe those are the only paths to success. But I'm passionate about art and design and I know that's where my talents are. I'm afraid of disappointing them but I also can't imagine spending years studying something I don't love. What would you advise?"

*Why high:* A defining cultural tension — particularly resonant for children of immigrants; deeply personal.

---

**Q6. Struggling With Online Learning**
> Your friend says: "I switched to an online degree program thinking it would give me more flexibility, but I can't stay focused at home, I procrastinate constantly, and I feel completely isolated without classmates around me. My grades are slipping and I'm falling behind. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Post-pandemic reality; widely experienced by students and adult learners alike.

---

**Q7. Taking a Gap Year — Family Disapproves**
> Your friend says: "I just finished high school and I want to take a year off before university to travel, work, and figure out what I really want to study. My parents are against it — they say I'll lose momentum and never go back to school. I feel I need this time but I don't want to cause conflict. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Classic generational debate; tests ability to balance both perspectives fairly.

---

**Q8. Foreign Credentials Not Recognized in Canada**
> Your friend says: "I worked as an engineer in my home country for 10 years, but after moving to Canada I was told my credentials are not recognized here. I would need to go back to school for at least two more years, which feels humiliating and exhausting. I don't know if it's worth it. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* One of the most common and painful experiences for skilled immigrants in Canada — directly relevant to CELPIP demographics.

---

**Q9. Student Loan Debt — Regretting the Degree**
> Your friend says: "I graduated two years ago with a degree that hasn't helped me find work in my field. Now I have $50,000 in student loan debt, I'm working a minimum wage job, and I wonder if university was worth it at all. I feel trapped. What advice can you give me?"

*Why high:* Financial regret over education is extremely common; tests practical and emotional advice.

---

**Q10. Child Being Bullied at School**
> Your friend says: "My 10-year-old has been coming home upset for weeks and I recently found out they're being bullied at school — name-calling and being excluded from groups. I've spoken to the teacher but nothing has changed. My child begs me not to make a big deal of it. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Every parent's fear; school + family overlap; no simple solution.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Choosing Between Two Very Different University Programs**
> Your friend says: "I've been accepted into two programs — computer science, which pays very well but I find quite dry, and psychology, which I find fascinating but has uncertain job prospects. I have to decide by next week. My parents say follow the money. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q12. Learning English as an Adult — Feeling Embarrassed**
> Your friend says: "I moved to Canada two years ago and my English is still not where I want it to be. I feel embarrassed speaking in public and I avoid situations where I have to talk to people I don't know. I've taken classes but I don't feel like I'm improving fast enough. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q13. Child Refusing to Do Homework**
> Your friend says: "Every evening is a battle with my 9-year-old over homework. They cry, argue, and stall for hours and eventually nothing gets done. Their teacher says their performance is suffering. I've tried rewards, punishments, and everything in between. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Postgraduate Studies — Worth the Investment?**
> Your friend says: "I finished my undergraduate degree and I'm considering a master's program, but it costs $30,000 and would take two more years. I'm not sure if it will actually improve my job prospects or just delay my life. Many people tell me a master's is essential; others say it's not worth it. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q15. Gifted Child Being Under-Challenged at School**
> Your friend says: "My daughter is extremely advanced for her age — she finishes her school work in minutes and is bored in class. Her teacher acknowledges it but says there's no special program available. I'm worried she'll lose her love of learning. What should I do?"

---

**Q16. Failing an Important Exam Repeatedly**
> Your friend says: "I've failed my professional licensing exam twice and I need to pass it to work in my field. I study hard but my mind goes blank during the test. I'm starting to doubt myself and wonder if this career is right for me. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q17. University Student With Severe Test Anxiety**
> Your friend says: "I understand the course material and do well on assignments, but I freeze completely during exams — my heart races, my mind goes blank, and I perform terribly. My grades don't reflect what I actually know. I don't know how to fix this. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Teenage Child Losing Interest in School**
> Your friend says: "My 16-year-old used to be an engaged student but in the last year they've completely lost interest. They say school is pointless, their grades have dropped, and they've been skipping classes. I don't know what's behind it or how to motivate them again. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q19. Workplace Training vs. Going Back to School**
> Your friend says: "I have the option to take free short-term training courses through my employer, or I can pursue a formal college diploma on my own time and expense. The diploma would take two years but might open more doors. I'm not sure which path makes more sense for my career. What would you advise?"

---

**Q20. Sending a Child to Private vs. Public School**
> Your friend says: "We've been offered a spot at a well-regarded private school for our child, but the tuition is $15,000 a year — a significant financial stretch for us. The public school nearby is decent but overcrowded. I'm not sure the private school is worth the financial pressure. What advice would you give?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Studying Abroad — Family Concerns**
> Your friend says: "I've been accepted into a one-year exchange program at a university in Europe. It's a fantastic opportunity but my parents are worried about my safety, the cost, and whether the credits will transfer. I don't want to miss this chance. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Special Needs Child — Navigating the School System**
> Your friend says: "My son has been diagnosed with a learning disability and the school says he needs a special education plan. I don't fully understand the process, I'm worried he'll be labeled or fall behind, and I don't know how to advocate for him. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q23. Professor Being Unfair — Should They Speak Up?**
> Your friend says: "I believe my professor has been grading my work unfairly compared to other students. When I asked for clarification, they dismissed me. I want to formally appeal but I'm afraid it will make things worse in the class for the rest of the semester. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q24. Homeschooling — Considering Pulling Child From School**
> Your friend says: "I've been seriously considering homeschooling my children because I'm unhappy with the quality of education at their current school. My partner thinks it's too extreme and that our kids need the social environment of school. I'm not sure what to do. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q25. Learning a New Language as an Adult**
> Your friend says: "I want to learn French to improve my job prospects in Canada, but I've tried two apps and one evening class and I never seem to make real progress. I get discouraged quickly and wonder if I'm just not a language person. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Teenager Obsessed With Gaming — Ignoring Studies**
> Your friend says: "My 17-year-old is deeply into gaming and spends every free moment playing online. Their grades have dropped significantly and they've stopped participating in any physical or social activities. They get angry whenever I try to set limits. What should I do?"

---

**Q27. Academic Dishonesty — Friend Asking for Help to Cheat**
> Your friend says: "A close friend asked me to let them copy my assignment. I said no, but they're still pressuring me, saying our friendship means I should help them. I don't want to lose the friendship but I also don't want to get in trouble or compromise my integrity. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q28. Scholarship Opportunity Requiring Relocation**
> Your friend says: "I've been offered a full scholarship to a university in another province, which would cover all my expenses. But it means leaving my family, my partner, and my support network behind. The local university would cost me $40,000 in loans. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q29. Child Starting School — Separation Anxiety**
> Your friend says: "My 5-year-old just started kindergarten and cries every single morning when I drop them off. The teacher says they settle down after I leave, but it breaks my heart every day. I've considered delaying school but I'm not sure that's the right answer. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q30. Mature Student Feeling Out of Place at University**
> Your friend says: "I went back to university at 45 and I feel completely out of place. The other students are young enough to be my children, I struggle with the technology, and I feel like the professors take me less seriously. I love learning but I feel invisible. What advice can you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Child Skipping School Without Parents Knowing**
> Your friend says: "I recently discovered that my 14-year-old has been skipping school for weeks and I had no idea. They've been telling me they're going but leaving and spending the day elsewhere. I'm shocked and hurt by the deception, and I don't know how to respond. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q32. Regretting Not Finishing High School — Considering Upgrading**
> Your friend says: "I dropped out of high school at 16 and now at 30, I realize it has limited my opportunities. I want to go back and get my diploma but I feel embarrassed about sitting in adult upgrading classes and I'm not sure I can handle studying after such a long break. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q33. University Research Misconduct — Witnessed It**
> Your friend says: "I'm a graduate student and I've discovered that someone in my research lab is falsifying data. I don't want to cause trouble, damage my supervisor's reputation, or jeopardize my own degree — but I know this is wrong. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Child With School Refusal — Anxiety-Driven**
> Your friend says: "My 13-year-old has developed such severe anxiety about school that they physically cannot bring themselves to go. They've missed over a month of classes. The school is not very supportive and my doctor has a long waiting list. I feel helpless. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q35. Pressure to Get Into a Top University — Student Breaking Down**
> Your friend says: "My 17-year-old is under enormous pressure to get into a top university — from us, from their school, and from themselves. They study until midnight every night, have stopped seeing friends, and recently broke down crying saying they feel worthless. I'm scared. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q36. Immigrant Child Placed in ESL Class — Parent's Concerns**
> Your friend says: "My child was placed in an ESL support program at school. I'm grateful the school noticed but I'm also worried — will being in ESL slow down their academic progress, label them, or separate them from other students? I want to support my child's English but not at the cost of their confidence or their school experience. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Wrongly Accused of Plagiarism — Panicking**
> Your friend says: "My professor has flagged my essay for plagiarism and I'm facing a formal hearing. I did not plagiarize — I wrote every word myself and cited my sources correctly. But I'm terrified of the process, I don't know how to defend myself, and I'm afraid no one will believe me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. International Student Unable to Afford Tuition — Considering Dropping Out**
> Your friend says: "I'm an international student in Canada and the tuition fees are much harder to manage than I expected — my home country's currency has dropped significantly and the cost of living here is high. I'm falling behind on payments and I may not be able to continue. I don't want to quit but I'm running out of options. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Managing Full-Time Work and Evening Studies — Exhausted**
> Your friend says: "I'm working full-time during the day and taking two courses in the evenings to finish my diploma. I am so exhausted that I can barely focus in class and my grades are suffering. I feel like I'm failing at both work and school but I need the income and I need the qualification. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Teacher Considering Leaving the Profession**
> Your friend says: "I became a teacher because I love working with children and I believe in education. But after eight years I'm completely burned out — the class sizes, the administrative demands, the lack of support, and the low pay have worn me down. I feel guilty for even thinking about leaving but I don't know how much longer I can do this. What advice would you give me?"
