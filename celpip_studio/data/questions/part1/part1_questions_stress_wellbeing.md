# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 11: Stress & Mental Wellbeing — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Burnout — Completely Exhausted but Cannot Stop**
> Your friend says: "I haven't had a proper break in two years. I work constantly, push through exhaustion every day, and I've been getting sick more often. I know I'm burning out but every time I think about slowing down I feel guilty — like resting means I'm failing. My body is sending me clear signals but I don't know how to stop. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Burnout is one of the most discussed health topics in Canada today; practical and psychological advice both essential.

---

**Q2. Anxiety Preventing Normal Daily Life**
> Your friend says: "My anxiety has been getting worse over the past year. I worry constantly about things I can't control — my health, my finances, the future. It affects my sleep, my concentration, and my relationships. I've tried to manage it on my own but nothing seems to work long-term. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Anxiety is the most common mental health challenge in Canada; universally relatable with rich advice potential.

---

**Q3. Depression — Refusing to Seek Professional Help**
> Your friend says: "I've been feeling deeply low for months. I don't enjoy anything anymore, I've pulled away from everyone I care about, and I feel hopeless most of the time. My family has suggested I see a therapist but I feel ashamed and I don't believe talking to a stranger will change anything. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Depression combined with stigma resistance is a powerful and nuanced scenario; very common in immigrant communities.

---

**Q4. Overwhelming Grief After Losing Someone Close**
> Your friend says: "My mother passed away three months ago and I cannot seem to function. I go through the motions at work but I feel completely empty inside. People keep telling me I need to move on but I'm not ready and I feel pressured to grieve on a schedule that isn't mine. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Grief is a universal human experience; the tension between personal grief timeline and social pressure is deeply relatable.

---

**Q5. Panic Attacks — Appearing Without Warning**
> Your friend says: "I've been having panic attacks that come out of nowhere — my heart races, I can't breathe, and I feel like I'm dying. They've happened at work, on public transit, and even at home. I'm starting to avoid places and situations where it has happened before. My world is getting smaller. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Panic disorder is widely misunderstood; the avoidance cycle makes it an especially rich advice scenario.

---

**Q6. Mental Health Stigma in the Family — Afraid to Ask for Help**
> Your friend says: "I've been struggling emotionally for a long time and I know I need professional help, but in my family and community, mental health issues are seen as weakness or something shameful that stays private. I'm afraid of how my parents and relatives will react if they find out I'm seeing a therapist. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Mental health stigma is especially prevalent in many immigrant communities represented among CELPIP test-takers — highly exam-relevant.

---

**Q7. Caregiver Burnout — Sacrificing Own Health for Others**
> Your friend says: "I've been the primary caregiver for my ill mother for three years. I've put my career, social life, and health on hold. Recently I've started feeling resentful and then guilty about feeling resentful. I love my mother but I feel like I'm disappearing as a person. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Caregiver burden is a growing and largely unspoken crisis; emotionally layered with guilt and identity loss.

---

**Q8. Postpartum Depression — Afraid to Admit It**
> Your friend says: "I had a baby four months ago and instead of feeling the joy everyone said I would feel, I feel disconnected from my baby, empty, and sometimes hopeless. I'm too afraid to tell my partner or doctor because I think they'll judge me as a bad mother. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Postpartum depression is common and frequently hidden due to shame; a scenario with urgent, compassionate advice needed.

---

**Q9. Perfectionism Causing Paralysis and Distress**
> Your friend says: "I've been a perfectionist my whole life and it used to drive me to succeed. But now it's causing me serious distress — I procrastinate on everything because I'm afraid of doing it imperfectly, I never feel satisfied with my work, and I've been passing up opportunities because I don't feel ready. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Perfectionism is a widespread and nuanced issue; touches identity, mental health, and productivity.

---

**Q10. Chronic Loneliness — Feeling Empty Despite Being Around People**
> Your friend says: "I'm surrounded by people — I have a family, colleagues, and acquaintances — but I feel profoundly lonely. No one truly knows me and I don't feel genuinely connected to anyone in my life. I've felt this way for years and I'm starting to wonder if something is fundamentally wrong with me. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Existential loneliness is one of the most underaddressed mental health issues; rich for compassionate advice.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Work-Related Stress Affecting Physical Health**
> Your friend says: "The stress from my job has started affecting my body — I have chronic headaches, my stomach is constantly upset, and my doctor says my blood pressure is too high for my age. I've been told the stress is the cause but I don't know how to manage the job pressure or whether to walk away from it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Trauma From the Past Resurfacing**
> Your friend says: "I experienced a very difficult period in my past — loss, displacement, and hardship — that I thought I had put behind me. Recently, certain situations and news events have been triggering strong emotional reactions that I don't know how to manage. It's affecting my daily life. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Fear of the Future — Paralysed by Uncertainty**
> Your friend says: "I spend a lot of time worrying about the future — financial security, health, global problems, what will happen to my children. The uncertainty feels unbearable and it stops me from enjoying the present. I know intellectually that I can't control everything but I can't seem to accept it emotionally. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q14. Substance Use to Cope With Stress**
> Your friend says: "I've been using alcohol and occasionally sleeping pills to manage my stress and anxiety. It helps in the short term but I wake up feeling worse and I know it's not a real solution. I've tried stopping but the stress comes back immediately and I reach for something to take the edge off. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q15. Returning to Work After Mental Health Leave**
> Your friend says: "I took three months off work for mental health reasons and I'm due to return next week. I feel better than I did, but I'm terrified of going back — worried about what colleagues think, whether I can handle the workload again, and whether the same stress will immediately undo my recovery. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Feeling Numb — No Joy in Anything**
> Your friend says: "For the past several months I haven't been able to feel much of anything — not sadness, not happiness, just a kind of grey emptiness. I go through my days mechanically. Things that used to bring me joy mean nothing to me now. I don't know what is wrong with me or what to do. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q17. Intrusive Thoughts — Frightened by Own Mind**
> Your friend says: "I keep having disturbing thoughts that I would never act on, but they pop into my head involuntarily and I can't make them stop. I'm ashamed and scared that having these thoughts means something is seriously wrong with me. I haven't told anyone. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Anger Management — Losing Control**
> Your friend says: "I have a serious problem with anger. Small things set me off and I say things I deeply regret. I've damaged important relationships because of my reactions and I hate who I become when I lose control. I've tried to manage it on my own but nothing has lasted. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Struggling With Identity After a Major Life Change**
> Your friend says: "Since immigrating to Canada two years ago, I've lost my sense of who I am. Back home I had a career, a role in my community, and a clear identity. Here I feel anonymous, undervalued, and disconnected from my own sense of purpose. What advice would you give me?"

*Note:* Directly reflects the identity disruption experienced by many CELPIP test-takers.

---

**Q20. Insomnia Driven by Racing Thoughts**
> Your friend says: "I haven't slept properly in months. The moment I lie down my mind starts racing — replaying the day, worrying about tomorrow, catastrophising about things that may never happen. I'm exhausted during the day but still can't sleep at night. Sleeping pills stop working after a few days. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Social Withdrawal After a Difficult Period**
> Your friend says: "After going through a very hard time last year I gradually withdrew from everyone — stopped answering messages, declined invitations, and isolated myself. Now I want to reconnect but I feel like so much time has passed that it would be awkward to just reappear. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Feeling Like a Failure Compared to Others**
> Your friend says: "I constantly compare myself to people around me and on social media and I always come up short. Everyone else seems to be achieving more, earning more, and living better. I know comparison isn't healthy but I can't stop. It's making me miserable. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Partner Dismissing Mental Health Struggles**
> Your friend says: "Whenever I try to talk to my partner about my anxiety and low mood, they tell me I'm being dramatic or that I should just think positively. They don't believe mental health struggles are real. I feel completely unsupported and more alone than if I hadn't said anything. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Refusing Medication for Mental Health — Afraid of Dependency**
> Your friend says: "My doctor has recommended I try medication for my depression and anxiety. I'm very reluctant — I'm afraid of becoming dependent on pills, of side effects, and of feeling like I can't manage without them. But I'm also not getting better on my own. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Grief That Never Resolved — Years Later Still Struggling**
> Your friend says: "My brother died five years ago and I still haven't come to terms with it. I put on a normal face for the world but inside I feel the loss as sharply as the day it happened. Everyone else in my family has moved on. I don't know if what I'm experiencing is normal. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Emotional Eating as a Response to Stress**
> Your friend says: "Whenever I feel stressed, anxious, or sad, I eat — large amounts of comfort food, often in secret. Afterwards I feel ashamed and disgusted with myself. I know food is my way of coping but I can't find another outlet that works. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Feeling Overwhelmed by Responsibilities — Nowhere to Turn**
> Your friend says: "I feel like I'm being crushed by responsibilities — a demanding job, young children, ageing parents, and financial pressures — all at the same time. I have no time for myself and no one I can truly lean on. I'm afraid of what will happen if I reach a breaking point. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Healing After an Emotionally Abusive Relationship**
> Your friend says: "I left an emotionally abusive relationship eight months ago. Although I know I made the right decision, I still have very low self-esteem, I distrust my own judgment, and I'm terrified of entering any new relationships. I feel stuck. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Mindfulness Not Working — Looking for Alternatives**
> Your friend says: "Everyone keeps recommending meditation and mindfulness for my stress and anxiety, but I've tried it sincerely for three months and I genuinely don't feel it works for me. My mind won't quiet down and I end up more frustrated after a session. What other approaches would you advise me to try?"

---

**Q30. Struggling With Self-Worth After Job Loss**
> Your friend says: "I was laid off six months ago and I've been surprised by how deeply it has affected my sense of self-worth. I defined myself largely through my career and without it I feel worthless and purposeless. The job search is going slowly and the rejection letters are making things worse. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Existential Crisis — Questioning the Point of Everything**
> Your friend says: "Lately I've been questioning everything — why I'm working so hard, what any of it means, whether the life I've built is really what I want. I feel disconnected from any sense of purpose and I don't know what to do with this feeling. People tell me I have a great life. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Fear of Therapy — Convinced It Won't Work for Them**
> Your friend says: "Several people have suggested I try therapy but I'm deeply sceptical. I feel like it's designed for a different type of person than me — I'm private, analytical, and I don't believe talking about feelings will fix practical problems. But I'm also not doing well on my own. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Compassion Fatigue — Working in a Caring Profession**
> Your friend says: "I'm a nurse and after three years of intense work I feel emotionally depleted. I used to feel deeply for my patients but now I feel detached and sometimes even resentful. I'm ashamed of these feelings but I simply have nothing left to give. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. PTSD Following a Traumatic Event**
> Your friend says: "Six months ago I was involved in a serious car accident. Physically I've recovered, but I still have nightmares, flashbacks, and I can't get into a car without extreme anxiety. My doctor says it may be PTSD but I don't want to believe something like that could affect someone like me. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q35. Struggling With Self-Harm — Wanting to Stop**
> Your friend says: "I've been hurting myself as a way to cope with emotional pain for the past year. I know it isn't healthy and I want to stop, but I don't know any other way to manage what I feel. I'm afraid of telling anyone because I don't want to be judged or hospitalised. What advice would you give me?"

*Note:* Handle with sensitivity. Advice should focus on encouraging professional support, removing judgment, and validating the person's courage in speaking up.

---

**Q36. Unable to Say No — Overwhelmed by Too Many Commitments**
> Your friend says: "I agree to everything — every request at work, every favour from friends, every family obligation — and I end up completely overwhelmed. When I try to say no I feel crushing guilt. My schedule is destroying my health and I don't know how to stop. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Using Alcohol to Cope With Stress**
> Your friend says: "I've been drinking most evenings to unwind and I know it's become a crutch. I'm not at a point where I think I have an addiction, but I rely on it more than I should. My partner is worried but I'm resistant to stopping because it's the only thing that switches my brain off. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Partner Minimising Your Mental Health Struggles**
> Your friend says: "I've been struggling with anxiety for a long time and when I try to talk about it with my partner, they tell me to just think positively, keep busy, and stop focusing on it. I don't feel heard or supported and the dismissal actually makes things worse. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Fear of Failure Preventing Any Action**
> Your friend says: "I have goals I genuinely want to pursue — career, creative, personal — but I'm so afraid of failing that I can't start anything. I spend enormous energy planning and imagining things but I never take the first step. I know this is irrational but I can't break the pattern. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Body Image Issues Affecting Daily Life**
> Your friend says: "I spend a significant portion of every day consumed by negative thoughts about my body. It affects what I eat, how I dress, which social situations I'll participate in, and how I feel in my relationship. I know it's not healthy but I don't know how to change the way I think. What advice would you give me?"
