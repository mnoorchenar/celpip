# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 7: Travel & Vacation — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Dream Vacation They Cannot Afford**
> Your friend says: "I've always dreamed of taking a big trip abroad but I never seem to have enough money saved. Every time I get close, something comes up — a car repair, a medical bill, an unexpected expense. I'm starting to wonder if I'll ever actually go. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Universal aspiration vs. financial reality; practical and motivational advice both apply.

---

**Q2. Travelling Solo for the First Time — Nervous**
> Your friend says: "I want to travel solo for the first time but I'm nervous about being alone in a foreign country — what if something goes wrong, what if I get lost or sick, what if it's just lonely? My family thinks it's dangerous, especially for a woman travelling alone. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Solo travel anxiety is widely relatable; gender safety dimension adds nuance; multiple advice angles.

---

**Q3. Vacation Plans Ruined at the Last Minute**
> Your friend says: "I planned and paid for a family vacation months in advance. One week before departure, the airline cancelled our flights and the hotel says they have no record of our booking. I've spent $4,000 and now I'm scrambling to salvage the trip or get my money back. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Travel disruption is a near-universal experience; practical problem-solving advice needed.

---

**Q4. Travelling With Young Children — Feeling Overwhelmed**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I want to take our first vacation since having kids, but our children are 2 and 4 years old. I'm worried the trip will be more stressful than relaxing — tantrums on planes, disrupted naps, and managing everything in an unfamiliar place. My partner thinks I'm overthinking it. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Family travel with toddlers is a common and relatable challenge; no single right answer.

---

**Q5. Partner Wants Different Type of Vacation**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I can never agree on vacations. I love outdoor adventures and exploring new cultures, but they prefer relaxing at an all-inclusive resort. Every time we try to plan a trip together we end up frustrated and sometimes just don't go at all. What advice would you give us?"

*Why high:* Travel compatibility in relationships is extremely common; tests ability to find balanced solutions.

---

**Q6. Visiting Home Country — Mixed Emotions**
> Your friend says: "I haven't been back to my home country in four years since immigrating to Canada. I miss my family terribly but I'm also nervous — things have changed there, some relationships have become distant, and I'm not sure how I'll feel going back. I also feel guilty that I've been away so long. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Directly reflects the emotional reality of many CELPIP test-takers; deeply personal and culturally resonant.

---

**Q7. Budget Travel — Making the Most of Limited Money**
> Your friend says: "I really want to travel but I'm on a very tight budget. I see friends posting about amazing trips and I feel like travel is only for people with money. I have two weeks of vacation and about $1,500 to spend including everything. Is it even worth trying? What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Budget constraints are universal; tests practical knowledge and encouragement.

---

**Q8. Travel Insurance — Didn't Buy It and Now Regrets It**
> Your friend says: "I went on vacation without buying travel insurance to save money, and while I was away I got sick and had to go to the hospital. Now I'm facing a $12,000 medical bill from a foreign hospital. I don't know how to handle this or if there's any way to reduce what I owe. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* A cautionary tale many can relate to; practical and financial advice both needed.

---

**Q9. Long-Haul Flight With a Fear of Flying**
> Your friend says: "I need to fly internationally to attend my cousin's wedding, but I have a severe fear of flying. The last time I flew I had a panic attack and had to be calmed down by a flight attendant. The flight is 14 hours and I'm already dreading it months in advance. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Fear of flying is extremely common; both practical and psychological advice apply.

---

**Q10. Bringing Elderly Parents on a Family Trip**
> Your friend says: "We're planning a family vacation and I want to bring my elderly parents along. They haven't travelled in years, one has mobility issues, and the other takes several medications. I want it to be enjoyable for everyone but I'm worried about managing their needs while also having fun with my kids. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Multigenerational travel reflects family values important in many cultures; logistically complex.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Backpacking Alone Through Developing Countries — Family Worried**
> Your friend says: "I want to take three months off to backpack through Southeast Asia and Central America on a shoestring budget. My parents think it's reckless and dangerous and are threatening to cut off financial support if I go. I've saved my own money for this trip. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Trip Ruined by a Travelling Companion**
> Your friend says: "I went on a two-week trip with a friend and it was a disaster — we had completely different travel styles, they complained constantly, changed plans without asking, and we argued almost every day. Now I'm home, the trip was ruined, and the friendship feels damaged. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Overspending on Vacation — Now in Debt**
> Your friend says: "I just got back from vacation and I spent almost twice my budget. I got caught up in the moment — nicer restaurants, extra excursions, shopping. Now I'm back home with $3,000 on my credit card and I feel terrible about it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Taking Time Off Work for a Long Trip — Worried About Job Security**
> Your friend says: "I want to take a six-week trip abroad but I'm afraid to ask my boss for that much time off. I only have three weeks of paid vacation and I'd need to request three additional unpaid weeks. I'm worried it will affect how I'm perceived at work. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Travelling While Pregnant — Is It Safe?**
> Your friend says: "I'm four months pregnant and my husband and I had planned a trip abroad before we found out. We've already paid deposits that are non-refundable. My doctor says I'm healthy but I'm nervous about travelling so far from home while pregnant. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Last-Minute Cheap Trip vs. Planned Expensive One**
> Your friend says: "I found an incredible last-minute flight deal — half the price of what I'd normally pay — but it leaves in five days and I haven't planned anything. I was saving up for a more carefully planned trip later in the year. Should I take the deal or stick to my original plan? What advice would you give?"

---

**Q17. Child Refusing to Travel — Disrupting Family Plans**
> Your friend says: "We planned an international family vacation but our 13-year-old is refusing to come — they say they'd rather stay home with their grandparents than spend two weeks travelling with the family. It's causing a lot of tension and my partner thinks we should cancel the whole trip. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q18. Jet Lag Ruining the Beginning of Every Trip**
> Your friend says: "Every time I travel across many time zones, I spend the first four or five days of my trip exhausted and disoriented. By the time I adjust, it's almost time to go home. It makes me wonder if long-haul travel is even worth it for a two-week trip. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Travelling to a Country With Political Instability**
> Your friend says: "I've had a trip booked to a country for six months, but the political situation there has deteriorated significantly. My government has issued a travel advisory but not a full ban. I've already paid for flights and accommodation. My family is begging me not to go. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q20. Vacation With Friends — Too Many People, Too Many Opinions**
> Your friend says: "A group of eight friends is trying to plan a vacation together and it's become a nightmare — no one can agree on a destination, budget, or dates. Every group chat message creates more conflict and we've been 'planning' for six months with nothing decided. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Feeling Guilty About Taking a Vacation**
> Your friend says: "I haven't taken a real vacation in three years because I always feel guilty leaving work. Even when I do take time off, I check my emails constantly and can't fully relax. My partner says I need to disconnect but I'm afraid things will fall apart without me. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q22. Sustainable Travel — Feeling Conflicted About Flying**
> Your friend says: "I love to travel but I've become increasingly concerned about the environmental impact of flying. I feel guilty every time I book a flight but I also don't want to stop exploring the world. I can't decide whether to change my habits or accept the contradiction. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Cruise vs. Independent Travel — Can't Decide**
> Your friend says: "My family is debating between booking an all-inclusive cruise or planning an independent trip to the same region. The cruise is simpler and covers everything, but some family members feel it's too structured and touristy. We can't agree. What advice would you give us?"

---

**Q24. Theft or Loss While Travelling — How to Recover**
> Your friend says: "While travelling abroad, my bag was stolen with my passport, wallet, and phone inside. I'm stranded in a foreign city with no money, no ID, and no way to contact my family. I'm panicking and I don't know what to do first. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Cultural Misstep While Travelling — Feeling Embarrassed**
> Your friend says: "While visiting a foreign country I accidentally offended the locals by doing something that's considered rude there — I had no idea it wasn't acceptable. The incident has made me very self-conscious about travelling and I'm afraid of making similar mistakes. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q26. Homesickness While Travelling Long-Term**
> Your friend says: "I'm three months into a year-long trip abroad and I'm deeply homesick. I miss my family, my routine, my food, and my language. I don't want to give up the trip but I also dread waking up every day feeling this way. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Road Trip Planning — Friends Have Different Expectations**
> Your friend says: "Three friends and I are planning a two-week road trip across Canada. We've started discussing the route and our expectations are wildly different — different budgets, different driving comfort levels, and different ideas of what to stop and see. I'm already stressed. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q28. Overpacked — Struggling With Luggage**
> Your friend says: "Every time I travel I bring way too much and I end up struggling with heavy bags everywhere I go. I've tried packing light before but I always panic and add things at the last minute. My back is suffering and I keep paying extra baggage fees. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Travel Addiction — Neglecting Responsibilities**
> Your friend says: "I've become obsessed with travelling and I take four or five trips a year. I love it but my savings are depleted, I've missed important family events, and my partner says I'm running away from real life rather than building one. I don't know how to find balance. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q30. Returning Home After a Long Trip — Reverse Culture Shock**
> Your friend says: "I've just returned home after eight months abroad and I feel completely out of place in my own city. Everything feels small and boring, I miss the freedom of travelling, and I can't seem to readjust to a normal routine. My friends don't really understand what I'm feeling. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Volunteering Abroad — Wondering if It Actually Helps**
> Your friend says: "I'm planning a volunteer trip abroad to help build homes in a developing country. A friend told me that these kinds of trips often do more harm than good — they take jobs away from locals and are more about making the volunteer feel good. Now I'm doubting whether to go. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q32. Digital Detox Travel — Partner Not on Board**
> Your friend says: "I want to take a vacation where I completely disconnect from technology — no phone, no social media, no email. My partner thinks it's impractical and a bit extreme, especially with the kids. I feel like I desperately need it. What advice would you give us?"

---

**Q33. Visa Denied — Trip Ruined**
> Your friend says: "I applied for a visa to visit a country I've always dreamed of seeing and my application was denied with no explanation. I've already taken time off work and I'm deeply disappointed. I don't know whether to appeal, reapply, or just pick a different destination. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q34. Travelling After a Serious Illness or Injury**
> Your friend says: "I went through a serious illness last year and I'm now in recovery. My doctor has cleared me to travel but I'm nervous — what if something goes wrong far from home, what if I overdo it? I've been putting life on hold for over a year and I really want to go. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Being Scammed While Travelling — Losing Trust in People**
> Your friend says: "I was badly scammed by someone I trusted while travelling — they posed as a tour guide and took my money, then disappeared. Beyond the financial loss, I feel foolish and I've lost my enthusiasm for travel entirely. I used to love it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Family Vacation With Completely Different Travel Styles**
> Your friend says: "My partner wants a relaxing beach holiday with no schedule, while I prefer exploring cities, visiting historical sites, and packing as much in as possible. Every time we travel together we end up compromising badly and both feeling unsatisfied. What advice would you give us?"

---

**Q37. Solo Travel as a Woman — Managing Safety Concerns**
> Your friend says: "I want to travel solo to several countries in Southeast Asia and South America. I'm excited about the independence but I'm also nervous about safety as a woman travelling alone. My family is very worried and keeps discouraging me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Travel Partner Backing Out at the Last Minute**
> Your friend says: "My close friend and I planned and booked a three-week international trip together, and two weeks before departure they've backed out due to a personal situation. The flights and accommodation are non-refundable and I'm left deciding whether to go alone, find someone else, or try to cancel. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Vacation Budget Ruined by Unexpected Costs**
> Your friend says: "We're halfway through a family vacation and unexpected costs — medical, transportation, accommodation changes — have left us with very little remaining budget. We still have a week left and I don't want to ruin it for everyone, but I'm stressed about the money. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Cultural Misstep While Travelling — Handling the Aftermath**
> Your friend says: "While travelling abroad I accidentally violated a local cultural custom in a public place and caused obvious offence to people around me. I felt terrible and didn't know how to handle it in the moment. I'm also worried that I may face similar situations again in other countries I plan to visit. What advice would you give me?"
