# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 5: Finance & Money — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Living Paycheque to Paycheque — No Savings**
> Your friend says: "I have a decent job and earn a reasonable salary, but by the end of every month I have almost nothing left. I don't know where the money goes. I've never been able to save and I have no emergency fund. If something unexpected happened, I'd be in serious trouble. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* One of the most universal financial situations; practical and emotional advice both apply; no single solution.

---

**Q2. Drowning in Credit Card Debt**
> Your friend says: "I've accumulated $20,000 in credit card debt over the past three years. I only pay the minimum each month, which barely covers the interest. I'm ashamed and stressed about it constantly. I don't know where to even begin to get out of this situation. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Credit card debt is extremely common in Canada; emotionally loaded with shame and anxiety.

---

**Q3. Renting vs. Buying a Home**
> Your friend says: "I've been renting for six years and my landlord just raised my rent again. My family keeps telling me to buy a home, but the prices in my city are very high and I'm not sure I'm ready for a mortgage. I also don't want to keep throwing money into rent forever. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Housing affordability is one of the biggest financial conversations in Canada — highly topical and relatable.

---

**Q4. Lending Money to a Friend or Family Member**
> Your friend says: "My brother has asked me to lend him $5,000. He says it's urgent and he'll pay me back within six months. I have the money saved but I've heard that lending money to family can destroy relationships. I don't want to say no and seem heartless, but I also can't afford to lose that money. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* The money-vs-relationship dilemma is universally relatable; no clean answer.

---

**Q5. Impulse Spending and No Budget**
> Your friend says: "I spend money impulsively — on clothes, gadgets, eating out, and things I don't really need. The moment I have money, I find ways to spend it. I've tried budgeting apps but I give up after a few days. I want to change but I don't know how. What advice can you give me?"

*Why high:* Behavioral finance — very relatable, tests practical and psychological advice.

---

**Q6. Job Loss — Managing Finances During Unemployment**
> Your friend says: "I lost my job three weeks ago and I'm not sure how long it will take to find a new one. I have two months of savings and a mortgage to pay. I'm terrified of falling behind on my bills. I don't know what to prioritize or how to stretch what I have. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Combines financial stress with job insecurity — urgent, practical, emotionally heavy.

---

**Q7. Investing for the First Time — Overwhelmed and Confused**
> Your friend says: "I've been told I should be investing my savings but I have no idea where to start. There are so many options — stocks, mutual funds, real estate, retirement accounts — and every article I read contradicts the last one. I'm afraid of making the wrong choice and losing everything. What advice can you give me?"

*Why high:* Investment confusion is widespread; tests ability to simplify and give actionable advice.

---

**Q8. Supporting Parents Financially While Struggling Yourself**
> Your friend says: "My parents back home rely on me to send money every month. I love them and I want to help, but the remittances are taking up a large portion of my income and I can barely save anything for myself. I feel guilty even thinking about reducing the amount. What would you advise me to do?"

*Why high:* Directly reflects the reality of many immigrants in Canada supporting families abroad — highly exam-relevant.

---

**Q9. Partner's Secret Spending Discovered**
> Your friend says: "I just found out that my partner has been hiding purchases from me for over a year — they've secretly spent thousands of dollars on things without telling me. We share finances and this has affected our savings plan. I feel betrayed. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Financial deception within relationships is a layered scenario combining trust, money, and communication.

---

**Q10. Retirement Savings — Starting Too Late**
> Your friend says: "I'm 45 years old and I've saved almost nothing for retirement. I kept telling myself I'd start later, but later is now. I feel panicked when I think about the future and I don't know if it's even possible to catch up at this point. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Retirement anxiety is extremely common; tests ability to give both reassuring and practical guidance.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Cosigning a Loan for Someone — Feeling Pressured**
> Your friend says: "My cousin has asked me to cosign a loan for a car because his credit score is too low to qualify on his own. He promises he'll make all the payments, but I know he's been irresponsible with money in the past. I don't want to damage our relationship by saying no. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Children's Education Savings — Where to Begin**
> Your friend says: "My child just turned two and everyone tells me I should already be saving for their education. But I'm struggling to cover my own monthly expenses. I feel like I'm already behind and my child isn't even in school yet. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q13. Gambling Problem Affecting Finances**
> Your friend says: "My husband has been gambling online and I only found out recently when I saw our bank account. He's spent thousands over the past year and we're now behind on our rent. He says he can stop on his own. I'm devastated and scared. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Being Offered a High-Interest Personal Loan**
> Your friend says: "I'm in a financial emergency and a lender is offering me a personal loan with very high interest rates. I know it's not ideal but I need the money now and I don't know what else to do. My bank already declined me. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q15. Spending Too Much on Lifestyle — Keeping Up With Others**
> Your friend says: "I spend a lot of money trying to keep up with my friends — dining at expensive restaurants, taking vacations I can't afford, buying luxury items. I know it's foolish but I feel left out when I say no. Now I'm in debt. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Financial Disagreement About Paying for a Wedding**
> Your friend says: "My fiancé and I want a big wedding but we have very different ideas about how much to spend. I want a modest celebration within our budget, but they want to go all out and take on debt to do it. This is causing serious tension between us. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q17. Elderly Parent With No Retirement Savings**
> Your friend says: "My mother is 70 years old and has almost no savings. She's been relying on a small pension that barely covers her basic needs. She may need to move in with me soon, which would be a significant financial and personal burden. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q18. Friend Promoting a Multi-Level Marketing Scheme**
> Your friend says: "A close friend keeps pushing me to join a business opportunity that sounds like a multi-level marketing scheme. They've already invested money and are pressuring me to join to help them succeed. I don't want to lose money or damage our friendship. What would you advise?"

---

**Q19. Sudden Windfall — What to Do With Unexpected Money**
> Your friend says: "I unexpectedly inherited $30,000 from a distant relative. I've never had this much money at once and I don't know what to do with it. Everyone around me has a different opinion — pay off debt, invest, buy a car, put it in savings. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q20. Splitting Finances in a New Relationship**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I recently moved in together and we can't agree on how to manage our money. I want to keep finances completely separate, but they think we should pool everything together. Neither of us wants to budge and it's creating tension. What advice can you give?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Living Beyond Means After a Promotion**
> Your friend says: "I got a significant promotion and salary increase last year, but instead of saving more, I upgraded my car, moved to a more expensive apartment, and started spending more on everything. My savings haven't grown at all. I feel like I'm on a treadmill. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q22. Bankruptcy — Considering It as a Last Resort**
> Your friend says: "I'm drowning in debt — credit cards, a personal loan, and back taxes. A friend mentioned that declaring bankruptcy might be an option. I'm terrified of what it would mean for my future but I also see no other way out. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Child Asking for Money Constantly**
> Your friend says: "My 25-year-old keeps asking us for money — for rent, groceries, and other expenses. We've helped many times but it never seems to end. My partner says we're enabling them, but I don't want my child to struggle. How do we handle this without damaging our relationship or our savings?"

---

**Q24. Considering a Risky Investment for Quick Returns**
> Your friend says: "A colleague told me about an investment opportunity that promises very high returns in a short time. I know it sounds too good to be true but I'm tempted — I've been struggling financially and this could change everything. Should I look into it further? What advice would you give?"

---

**Q25. No Life Insurance or Financial Plan**
> Your friend says: "I'm 40, I have a spouse and two kids, and I have no life insurance, no will, and no financial plan. I keep putting it off because it feels morbid and complicated. But a friend's husband passed away recently and left his family in financial chaos. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Spending Too Much on Children — Saying No Is Hard**
> Your friend says: "I spend far too much money on my children — private lessons, expensive clothes, the latest gadgets. I find it almost impossible to say no to them. My savings are suffering and I know I need to change but I don't know how to start. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q27. Freelancer With Irregular Income — Poor Financial Planning**
> Your friend says: "I work freelance and my income varies a lot from month to month. Some months are great and others barely cover my bills. I don't know how to budget when I never know what I'll earn. I've fallen behind on taxes and I have no savings buffer. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Paying Off Debt vs. Saving — Which Comes First?**
> Your friend says: "I have $15,000 in student loan debt and I've recently been able to put a bit of money aside each month. I can't decide whether to put everything toward paying off the debt as quickly as possible or start building savings at the same time. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Refusing to Discuss Finances With a Partner**
> Your friend says: "Whenever I try to talk to my partner about our finances — budgeting, savings, planning for the future — they shut down completely and say they find money talk stressful. But we have shared bills, a joint account, and no plan for emergencies. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q30. Donating to Charity While in Debt**
> Your friend says: "I give regularly to a charity I care deeply about, but I'm currently carrying significant debt. My financial advisor says I should stop donating until the debt is cleared, but it feels wrong to stop helping others just because I'm struggling. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Prenuptial Agreement — Partner Is Offended**
> Your friend says: "Before getting married I want to sign a prenuptial agreement because I own property and have significant savings. My partner is deeply offended and says asking for a prenup means I don't trust them or believe the marriage will last. I don't know how to handle this. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q32. Discovering a Parent Has Been Financially Exploited**
> Your friend says: "I recently found out that my elderly mother has been sending large sums of money to someone she met online who claims to love her. She's already sent $15,000 and refuses to believe it's a scam. She gets angry when I try to intervene. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Extreme Frugality Affecting Quality of Life**
> Your friend says: "I'm so afraid of financial insecurity that I've become extremely frugal — I never go out, I refuse to spend on anything beyond bare necessities, and I've pushed away friends because I won't join them for dinner or activities. My savings are healthy but I feel like I have no life. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q34. Currency and Savings From Home Country — What to Do**
> Your friend says: "I have significant savings in my home country's currency, but the exchange rate has been weakening and the political situation is unstable. I'm worried my savings could lose a lot of value. I don't know whether to transfer the money to Canada now or wait. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q35. Winning the Lottery — Overwhelmed by Sudden Wealth**
> Your friend says: "I recently won a significant amount of money in the lottery. Instead of feeling happy, I feel overwhelmed and anxious. Friends and relatives I barely speak to are asking for money, I don't know who to trust, and I'm terrified of making the wrong financial decisions. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Supporting a Sibling Who Won't Help Themselves Financially**
> Your friend says: "My younger brother has been financially irresponsible for years — he borrows money, makes no effort to save, and refuses advice. I've lent him money multiple times and he has never repaid me. He's asking again, and I know that if I say no he'll struggle, but if I say yes, nothing will change. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Reward Points and Cashback — Overspending to Earn Rewards**
> Your friend says: "I signed up for a rewards credit card and I've gotten so focused on earning points that I'm spending more than I normally would just to hit bonus thresholds. My husband says I'm spending two dollars to earn one dollar's worth of points. I know it sounds absurd but it's become a habit I can't shake. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Financial Advisor Gave Poor Advice — Feeling Deceived**
> Your friend says: "I followed the advice of a financial advisor I trusted for three years and invested a significant portion of my savings in products that performed very poorly. I recently discovered that the advisor earned large commissions from those products. I feel deceived and I've lost money I can't afford to lose. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Family Dispute Over Inheritance While Parent Is Still Alive**
> Your friend says: "My father is elderly and has begun discussing how his estate will be divided. My siblings and I have very different expectations and the conversations have become hostile. My father is distressed by the conflict and I feel ashamed that we're already arguing about money while he's still with us. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q40. Leaving Canada — What to Do With Savings and Assets**
> Your friend says: "My family and I are planning to move back to our home country in about a year. I have an RRSP, a TFSA, and some investments here in Canada and I have no idea what to do with them — whether to cash everything out, keep the accounts open, or transfer the money. I'm worried about taxes and making the wrong call. What advice would you give me?"
