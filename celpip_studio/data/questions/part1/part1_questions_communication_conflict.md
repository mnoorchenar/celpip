# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 19: Communication & Conflict Resolution — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Ongoing Conflict With a Coworker — Tension Affects the Whole Team**
> Your friend says: "I have a serious ongoing conflict with a coworker. We had a disagreement three months ago that was never resolved and now the tension between us affects every meeting and every project we share. My manager has noticed but hasn't intervened. I don't want to escalate it formally but the situation is getting worse every week. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Workplace interpersonal conflict is one of the most universally experienced and emotionally charged professional challenges; tests both assertiveness and diplomacy advice.

---

**Q2. Difficulty Expressing Feelings in Close Relationships**
> Your friend says: "I find it very hard to express my emotions to the people I'm closest to — my partner, my family, my best friends. I know what I feel but when I try to say it out loud, the words either don't come or come out wrong. People have told me I seem distant or cold and it's starting to affect my relationships. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Emotional expression difficulty is one of the most common relationship communication issues; psychological and practical advice both essential.

---

**Q3. Unable to Speak Up When Treated Unfairly**
> Your friend says: "When someone treats me unfairly — a rude colleague, an aggressive customer, an unreasonable manager — I freeze. I say nothing in the moment, then replay the situation for days thinking about what I should have said. I feel weak and I'm frustrated with myself. I want to be able to stand up for myself in the moment. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Inability to assert oneself in real-time conflict is an extremely common and personally painful experience; practical and psychological advice both needed.

---

**Q4. Serious Misunderstanding That Has Damaged a Friendship**
> Your friend says: "A close friend of mine completely misunderstood something I said several weeks ago and has been cold and distant ever since. I've tried to explain what I meant but they don't seem to believe my intentions were good. The friendship feels like it's slipping away over something I didn't mean. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Misunderstanding-based friendship damage is near-universal; tests advice on repair, persistence, and self-advocacy.

---

**Q5. Partner Who Shuts Down During Arguments — Never Resolves**
> Your friend says: "Whenever my partner and I have a serious disagreement, they go completely silent — they stop talking, leave the room, or say 'I'm fine' when they clearly aren't. Nothing ever gets resolved because we can't have a conversation about what's wrong. This pattern has been going on for two years. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Stonewalling and conflict avoidance in relationships is a leading cause of relationship breakdown; widely experienced.

---

**Q6. Giving Critical Feedback Without Damaging the Relationship**
> Your friend says: "I manage a small team and one of my employees is underperforming in ways that are affecting the whole group. I need to give direct, honest feedback but I'm afraid of damaging our relationship, making them feel bad, or creating an uncomfortable work environment. I've been putting it off for weeks. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Delivering difficult feedback is one of the most universally dreaded professional responsibilities; tests precision and nuance in advice.

---

**Q7. Family Member Who Never Listens — Conversations Go in Circles**
> Your friend says: "I have a family member who talks over me, dismisses what I say, and turns every conversation back to themselves. When I try to express something important, I end up feeling unheard and frustrated. I've tried being direct but it causes a fight. I love this person but the pattern is exhausting. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Non-listening family dynamics is a deeply familiar struggle in every culture; advice must balance honesty with relationship preservation.

---

**Q8. Texting and Digital Communication Causing Misunderstandings**
> Your friend says: "So many of my conflicts recently have been caused by misunderstandings over text or email — tone gets misread, words get taken out of context, and by the time I respond to a misreading the situation has already escalated. I've damaged two important relationships through digital miscommunication. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Digital communication conflict is a defining modern problem; practical advice on choosing communication channels is universally useful.

---

**Q9. Afraid to Disagree With Authority Figures**
> Your friend says: "I find it almost impossible to disagree with authority figures — my manager, my doctor, older family members. Even when I know they're wrong or their decision affects me directly, I nod and comply. Later I feel resentful and frustrated. I know I need to find my voice but I don't know how. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Deference to authority at the cost of self-advocacy is particularly strong in many immigrant communities; directly relevant to CELPIP demographic.

---

**Q10. Conflict With a Close Friend Over a Significant Issue**
> Your friend says: "My closest friend and I have had a serious falling out over something that genuinely matters to me — a decision they made that I felt was wrong and that affected me. They think I'm overreacting. We haven't spoken properly in a month and I miss the friendship but I'm also genuinely hurt. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Major friendship conflicts with unresolved hurt on both sides is one of the most emotionally complex relationship challenges; no single right answer.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Passive-Aggressive Colleague Making Work Difficult**
> Your friend says: "A colleague of mine never addresses problems directly — instead they make indirect comments, give the silent treatment, and undermine me in subtle ways that are hard to call out explicitly. When I try to address it directly they deny everything. I'm not sure how to handle someone who won't fight fairly. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Apologising When You Don't Believe You Were Wrong**
> Your friend says: "There is a conflict at work where I'm being pressured to apologise for something I genuinely don't believe I did wrong. My manager says it doesn't matter who was right — the relationship needs to be repaired. I'm conflicted between my integrity and the practical need to move on. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Raised Voices in Disagreements — Can't Stay Calm**
> Your friend says: "Whenever a serious disagreement escalates, I lose my composure — my voice rises, I say things I regret, and the conversation stops being productive. I know it's damaging my relationships and my reputation but I can't control it in the heat of the moment. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Having a Difficult Conversation With a Parent**
> Your friend says: "I need to have a very difficult conversation with my parent about a decision they are making that I believe is seriously harmful to themselves. I'm afraid they will dismiss me, become defensive, or damage our relationship if I push too hard. But I also can't stay silent. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Friend Group Conflict — Being Asked to Take Sides**
> Your friend says: "Two of my close friends have had a major falling out and both of them are separately asking me to take their side and validate that the other person is wrong. I care about both of them and I don't believe it's that simple. The pressure from both sides is making me want to withdraw from the whole group. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Talking to a Partner About an Emotional Need They're Not Meeting**
> Your friend says: "There is something emotionally important to me that my partner consistently fails to provide — acknowledgement, affirmation, a certain kind of support. I've hinted at it many times but never said it clearly because I feel needy and embarrassed. But the unmet need is building into resentment. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q17. Colleague Taking Credit for Your Work**
> Your friend says: "A colleague in my team has been presenting my ideas and contributions as their own — in meetings, in reports, and in conversations with senior management. It has happened three times now and my manager seems to believe their account. I don't know how to address this without looking bitter or petty. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Communicating Across a Language Barrier at Work**
> Your friend says: "Many of my colleagues speak a different first language than I do and important communication often happens in that language during meetings, break room conversations, and group chats. I'm left out of key information and feel like an outsider even though my English is strong. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Social Media Argument That Escalated Publicly**
> Your friend says: "I got into a public argument on social media with someone I know professionally. What started as a minor disagreement escalated into a thread that many mutual contacts saw and commented on. I regret how it unfolded but I'm not sure how to handle the aftermath professionally. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q20. Spouse Not Sharing Concerns Until They Become Crises**
> Your friend says: "My spouse never tells me when something is bothering them until it has become a major crisis. I only find out about a problem when they're already overwhelmed and resentful. By then, it's harder to resolve. I want us to communicate earlier and more openly, but I don't know how to change a pattern this entrenched. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Conflict With a Landlord — Needs to Advocate Without Jeopardising Tenancy**
> Your friend says: "My landlord has failed to make several necessary repairs and is now refusing to respond to my messages. I have a right to a liveable home but I'm also afraid that if I push too hard or escalate formally, they will try to find reasons to evict me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Publicly Embarrassed by Someone — Wants to Address It**
> Your friend says: "A colleague publicly embarrassed me during a team meeting by making a dismissive comment about my idea in front of everyone. I froze in the moment. I want to address it with them privately but I'm not sure whether to let it go, speak to them directly, or mention it to my manager. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Cross-Cultural Communication Misunderstanding at Work**
> Your friend says: "I've had several situations at work where something I said was interpreted completely differently than I intended because of cultural differences in directness and communication style. I now second-guess everything I say and it's making me less confident and less effective at work. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Someone Giving Unsolicited Advice Constantly**
> Your friend says: "Someone in my life — a family member — constantly gives me unsolicited opinions on my parenting, my career, my lifestyle, and my choices. When I try to set a limit they act hurt and say they're only trying to help. The dynamic is wearing me down. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Negotiating a Raise — Afraid to Ask**
> Your friend says: "I have not had a raise in three years despite strong performance reviews and increasing responsibilities. I know I need to ask but every time I prepare to have the conversation I lose my nerve and avoid it. I'm afraid of being told no or of being seen as ungrateful. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Being Too Agreeable — Always Saying Yes**
> Your friend says: "I've noticed that in professional and social conversations, I agree with almost everything people say even when I privately disagree. I don't want conflict, I want to be liked, and so I suppress my actual opinions. But I'm starting to feel invisible and inauthentic. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Repairing a Relationship After a Long Silence**
> Your friend says: "I had a falling out with someone who was once very close to me and we haven't spoken in almost two years. I've thought about reaching out many times but I don't know how to start after so long or whether the door is even open. I'm afraid of being rejected or making things worse. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Managing Interruptions and Talking Over in Group Settings**
> Your friend says: "In group conversations — whether at work or socially — I regularly get talked over or interrupted before I finish my thought. By the time there's a gap, the topic has moved on. I feel invisible in group settings and I don't know how to hold space for myself without seeming aggressive. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Dealing With a Chronic Complainer in the Workplace**
> Your friend says: "I share an office with a colleague who complains constantly — about the workload, the company, management, and colleagues. It's relentlessly negative and it's affecting my own mood and productivity. I don't want to be unkind but I can't absorb this negativity every day. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q30. Saying Something Hurtful in Anger — Trying to Repair It**
> Your friend says: "During a heated argument, I said something genuinely hurtful to someone I love — something I didn't mean but that they have been unable to forget. I've apologised sincerely but the damage remains. I don't know what else I can do to show I'm sorry and rebuild the trust. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Being Ghosted After a Conflict — No Closure**
> Your friend says: "After a disagreement with someone I was close to, they simply stopped responding to all contact — no explanation, no acknowledgment, nothing. It's been four months. I find the lack of closure deeply distressing and I don't know whether to keep trying, accept it, or find a way to move on without answers. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Whistleblowing — Witnessing Wrongdoing at Work**
> Your friend says: "I've become aware of something at my workplace that I believe is unethical — not illegal necessarily, but genuinely wrong. I don't know whether I should say something, to whom, or how. I'm afraid of being dismissed, retaliated against, or labelled a troublemaker. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Cultural Directness Being Mistaken for Rudeness**
> Your friend says: "In my culture, being direct is a sign of respect — it means you trust someone enough to be honest. But here in Canada, some colleagues interpret my directness as aggression or rudeness. I've been told I need to 'soften' my communication style and I find this deeply frustrating. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Intervening in Someone Else's Conflict**
> Your friend says: "Two people I care about are in a serious conflict and both are suffering. I believe I could help them communicate better but I'm not sure if intervening is appropriate or welcome. I'm afraid of making things worse or being blamed by both sides. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Online Harassment Following a Disagreement**
> Your friend says: "After a public disagreement online with someone I don't know personally, they have been leaving hostile comments on my posts and sent harassing messages. I've blocked them but they keep creating new accounts. I don't know how to make it stop and I'm starting to feel genuinely unsafe. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Accidentally Offending Someone With a Joke**
> Your friend says: "I made a comment I thought was funny during a casual conversation with a colleague, but I could tell immediately that it landed badly — they went quiet and the mood shifted. I apologised right away but they brushed it off and now things feel awkward between us. I don't know if I should bring it up again or let it go. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Being Deliberately Left Out of Work Communications**
> Your friend says: "I've noticed that a group of colleagues are having conversations, making decisions, and sharing information in a group chat that I'm not part of. I only find out about things after the fact. I don't know if it's intentional or just oversight, but it's affecting my work and making me feel excluded. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Roommate Conflict Over Shared Space Rules**
> Your friend says: "My roommate and I have very different ideas about cleanliness, noise levels, and guests. We've never sat down and agreed on any rules and now small irritations have built into real resentment. I want to address it before it gets worse but I'm afraid the conversation will make the living situation even more tense. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Rebuilding Reputation After a Workplace Misunderstanding**
> Your friend says: "Something I said in a meeting was completely misunderstood and word spread quickly in a way that damaged how people see me professionally. My manager now seems to view me differently too. I know what I actually meant but I can't go around correcting everyone without looking defensive. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Receiving Harsh Criticism in Front of Others**
> Your friend says: "My manager publicly criticised my work in front of the whole team in a way that felt humiliating and disproportionate to the actual mistake. I was so shocked I said nothing. Now I'm dreading going back to the office and I'm not sure whether to say something to my manager, report it to HR, or just move on. What advice would you give me?"
