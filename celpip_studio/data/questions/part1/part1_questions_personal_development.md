# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 18: Personal Development — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Severe Lack of Confidence — Holding Back in Every Area of Life**
> Your friend says: "I have very low self-confidence and it affects everything — my career, my relationships, and my ability to try new things. I know what I want but I talk myself out of everything because I assume I'll fail or be judged. I've been this way my whole life and I don't know if people can actually change. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Low confidence is one of the most universal personal struggles; touches career, relationships, and identity simultaneously.

---

**Q2. Chronic Procrastination Affecting Every Area of Life**
> Your friend says: "I procrastinate on everything — important tasks, personal goals, even small things I know I need to do. I feel overwhelmed before I even start and end up doing nothing. At night I feel guilty and promise myself tomorrow will be different, but it never is. My career and personal goals are suffering. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Procrastination is the most universally shared personal challenge; practical and psychological advice both essential.

---

**Q3. Fear of Public Speaking Limiting Career Growth**
> Your friend says: "I have a severe fear of public speaking. Even speaking up in small meetings makes my heart race, my voice shake, and my mind go blank. I've turned down promotion opportunities because they would require presenting to groups. I know it's holding me back but I don't know how to overcome it. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Public speaking anxiety is statistically one of the most common fears; career implications make advice stakes high.

---

**Q4. Setting Goals but Never Following Through**
> Your friend says: "Every January I set ambitious goals and every February I've abandoned them all. I get excited at the start, make detailed plans, and then lose momentum completely. I've done this cycle so many times I'm starting to doubt whether I have the character to achieve anything I set out to do. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Goal-setting failure is a near-universal experience; self-doubt component makes it emotionally rich.

---

**Q5. Comparing Themselves to Others — Paralysed by Inadequacy**
> Your friend says: "I constantly measure myself against people around me and against what I see on social media. I always come up short. People my age seem further ahead in their careers, relationships, and finances. I know comparison is destructive but I genuinely cannot stop doing it. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Social comparison is a defining modern struggle; social media amplification makes it increasingly urgent.

---

**Q6. Afraid to Take Any Risks — Life Feels Stagnant**
> Your friend says: "I've always played it safe — I've never changed jobs, moved cities, or made any decision that felt uncertain. My life is stable but it feels completely stagnant. I watch others take risks and move forward while I stand still. I want to change but fear holds me back every time. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Risk aversion and stagnation is a profound life challenge; tests ability to give nuanced rather than simplistic advice.

---

**Q7. Difficulty Saying No — Always Overwhelmed by Others' Demands**
> Your friend says: "I cannot say no to people — my boss, my family, my friends. I take on more than I can handle, I volunteer for things I don't want to do, and I let people take advantage of my time and energy. I end up resentful and exhausted. I know I need to set limits but I'm afraid of how people will react. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* People-pleasing is an extremely common and well-articulated struggle; tests advice on boundaries and self-worth.

---

**Q8. Imposter Syndrome — Feeling Like a Fraud Despite Success**
> Your friend says: "I've achieved things I'm proud of professionally, but I live in constant fear that people will discover I don't deserve any of it — that I've just been lucky and I'm not as capable as people think. Every positive outcome reinforces my fear of being exposed. It's exhausting. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Imposter syndrome is widely experienced and increasingly discussed; particularly relevant for professionals and immigrants.

---

**Q9. No Sense of Purpose or Direction in Life**
> Your friend says: "I'm 32 years old and I have no idea what I want from life. I have a job that pays the bills but means nothing to me. I have no strong passions, no clear direction, and no sense of what would make me feel that my life is meaningful. People my age seem to know who they are. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Purposelessness in adulthood is one of the most emotionally significant and common life crises; no single right answer.

---

**Q10. Negative Self-Talk and Inner Critic That Never Stops**
> Your friend says: "I have a relentlessly critical inner voice that tells me I'm not good enough, not smart enough, and that I'm going to fail. It comments on everything I do and undermines any confidence I start to build. I've tried ignoring it but it just gets louder. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Negative self-talk is a psychological issue that underlies many other struggles; rich for advice on reframing and self-compassion.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Wanting to Change Career but Lacking Confidence to Start Over**
> Your friend says: "I'm 38 and I want to completely change careers but I'm terrified. I'd be starting at the bottom in a new field, my current skills might not transfer, and I'd be taking a significant pay cut. I've wanted to make this change for five years and I still haven't done anything about it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Difficulty Accepting Criticism — Becomes Defensive and Upset**
> Your friend says: "Whenever someone criticises my work or gives me feedback, I become extremely upset and defensive — even when the feedback is constructive and well-intended. I know this is limiting my growth and damaging my relationships at work, but I can't seem to control my reaction. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Struggling With Time Management — Always Running Behind**
> Your friend says: "No matter what system I try, I'm always late, always behind, and always overwhelmed by my to-do list. I've tried planners, apps, and time-blocking and none of it sticks for longer than a week. I feel perpetually disorganised and it's affecting my reputation at work. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Feeling Stuck in a Comfort Zone — Too Scared to Grow**
> Your friend says: "I know that everything I want is outside my comfort zone, but every time I approach the edge of it I retreat. I want to meet new people, try new experiences, and build a bigger life, but the anxiety I feel when I try is overwhelming and I always go back to what's familiar. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Perfectionism Preventing Completion of Any Project**
> Your friend says: "I have dozens of unfinished projects — a business idea, a creative project, a fitness goal — because I can never get them to the standard I want before sharing them. I revise endlessly, never feel ready, and ultimately abandon things rather than put out something imperfect. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Anger Issues — Reacting Too Quickly and Regretting It**
> Your friend says: "I have a short fuse and I react to frustration and conflict far too quickly. I say things I regret, I respond to small problems with disproportionate anger, and I've damaged important relationships because of it. I feel ashamed afterwards but in the moment I can't stop myself. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q17. Reading Self-Help Books but Nothing Changes**
> Your friend says: "I've read dozens of self-help books over the past three years. I feel inspired during and right after reading them but nothing ever seems to change in my actual life. I'm starting to wonder if the problem is the books, or whether the problem is me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Building Self-Discipline — Knows What to Do but Can't Do It**
> Your friend says: "I know exactly what I should be doing — exercising, eating well, sleeping properly, working consistently — but I have very little self-discipline when it comes to actually doing these things. The gap between what I know and what I do is enormous and it frustrates me deeply. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. New to Journaling and Reflection — Doesn't Know How to Start**
> Your friend says: "My therapist has recommended I start journaling as a way to process my thoughts and feelings. I've bought a journal but every time I sit down to write I stare at a blank page and feel silly. I don't know what to write, how to start, or whether it can really help someone like me. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q20. Feeling Like Everyone Else Has Their Life Together**
> Your friend says: "Whenever I look at the people around me — colleagues, friends, family — they all seem to have their lives together while mine feels chaotic and unresolved. I'm 35 with no clear plan, no major accomplishments I'm proud of, and a growing sense that I've wasted years. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Unable to Ask for Help — Seeing It as Weakness**
> Your friend says: "I find it almost impossible to ask for help — at work, at home, or personally. I struggle through things alone long after it stops making sense, and I eventually burn out or fail unnecessarily. I see asking for help as an admission of incompetence. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q22. Habit Formation — Nothing Ever Sticks**
> Your friend says: "I've tried to build positive habits dozens of times — morning routines, exercise, reading, meditation — and I can never maintain any of them beyond a few weeks. I've read the books about how habits work. I understand the theory. But in practice I always fail. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q23. Overwhelmed by Too Many Interests — Can't Focus on One Thing**
> Your friend says: "I have so many interests and things I want to pursue that I spread myself too thin across all of them and never make meaningful progress in any one area. I start enthusiastically, get distracted by the next thing, and feel perpetually scattered. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Comparing Current Self to Younger Self — Feeling Like a Failure**
> Your friend says: "When I was young I had clear ambitions and a strong sense of who I would become. Looking at where I am now at 40, I feel like a lesser version of who I intended to be. I'm not sure if I should work toward those old dreams or accept a new version of success. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q25. Learning From Failure — Taking It Too Personally**
> Your friend says: "Every time I fail at something — a project, an application, a relationship — I take it extremely personally and it takes me a long time to recover. I know intellectually that failure is part of growth but emotionally I treat every setback as a reflection of my fundamental worth. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Wanting to Be More Present — Mind Always Elsewhere**
> Your friend says: "I'm rarely truly present in my own life. At work I think about home; at home I think about work. When I'm with people I love, my mind wanders. I've missed important moments because I was mentally somewhere else. I want to learn to be fully present but I don't know how. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Social Media Destroying Self-Image**
> Your friend says: "I know that social media shows only the highlight reel of people's lives, but knowing this doesn't stop me from feeling inferior every time I scroll. My self-image is significantly worse than it was before I started using these platforms five years ago. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Leadership Role — Doesn't Feel Like a Leader**
> Your friend says: "I was recently promoted to a leadership position, which I worked hard for, but now that I'm in the role I don't feel like a leader. I second-guess every decision, I avoid difficult conversations, and I worry my team can tell I'm unsure of myself. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q29. Difficulty Forgiving Themselves After a Past Mistake**
> Your friend says: "Several years ago I made a serious mistake that hurt people I care about. I've apologised, things have been repaired, but I cannot forgive myself. I return to it constantly and use it as evidence that I'm a bad person. It's been five years and the guilt hasn't faded. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q30. Wanting to Be More Optimistic — Natural Pessimist**
> Your friend says: "I'm a natural pessimist. I anticipate problems, expect disappointment, and prepare for the worst in most situations. I'm not depressed — this is just how my mind works. But I can see it affects the people around me and I sometimes wonder what life would feel like with a different perspective. What advice would you give me?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Feeling Like a Late Bloomer — Comparing Timeline to Others**
> Your friend says: "I feel like I'm behind in life — I finished my education later, started my career later, and reached milestones later than people around me. Even though I know everyone's timeline is different, I carry a persistent sense of being behind and of having wasted my early years. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q32. Spiritual or Religious Doubt Affecting Identity**
> Your friend says: "I was raised in a deeply religious household and faith was central to my identity. Over the past few years I've developed serious doubts. I can't share this with my family and I feel guilty and lost — like I'm betraying everything and everyone that formed who I am. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Building Emotional Intelligence — Doesn't Understand Own Feelings**
> Your friend says: "I've been told by several people, including a therapist, that I have difficulty identifying and expressing my own emotions. I know something is wrong but I can't name what I feel most of the time. I function well professionally but my personal relationships suffer from this. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Obsession With Productivity Leaving No Room for Rest**
> Your friend says: "I feel compelled to be productive every moment of every day. I can't rest without feeling guilty. I keep lists, track my habits obsessively, and measure my days by output. People around me admire my drive but I'm deeply unhappy. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q35. Developing Patience — Easily Frustrated by Slow Progress**
> Your friend says: "I'm deeply impatient. When I want to improve at something, learn something new, or achieve a goal, I expect rapid progress and I become frustrated and despondent when results come slowly. I've given up on many worthwhile things simply because I didn't see fast enough results. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. Struggling to Forgive Yourself After a Serious Mistake**
> Your friend says: "I made a serious mistake a couple of years ago that hurt people I care about. I've apologized and done what I could to make it right, but I still carry enormous guilt and it affects my self-worth every day. I can't seem to move past it no matter what I do. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Overcoming Deep Fear of Rejection — Affecting Relationships and Career**
> Your friend says: "I have a profound fear of rejection that affects every area of my life — I don't apply for opportunities because I'm afraid of not being chosen, I avoid expressing my feelings in relationships, and I hold back my opinions at work. I know this fear is limiting me but I don't know how to work through it. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Imposter Syndrome Despite Genuine Success**
> Your friend says: "I've been promoted, I've received praise for my work, and by every objective measure I'm doing well — but I feel like a fraud who will eventually be exposed. I attribute my success to luck and I'm constantly waiting to be found out. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Unable to Stop Comparing Yourself to Others on Social Media**
> Your friend says: "I know that social media is a highlight reel but I still find myself constantly comparing my life to what I see online — people with nicer homes, better bodies, more exciting lives. I've tried to limit my use but I always go back. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Wanting to Change Career Direction Completely at 40**
> Your friend says: "I've spent 12 years in a career that I've come to realize is not right for me. I want to make a complete change but I'm afraid of losing my seniority, my income, and starting at the bottom again at 40. I don't know if it's too late or too risky. What advice would you give me?"
