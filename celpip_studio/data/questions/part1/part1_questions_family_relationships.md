# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 3: Family & Relationships — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Parents Disapproving of Your Partner**
> Your friend says: "I've been with my partner for two years and I love them deeply, but my parents strongly disapprove of the relationship — they think we're not compatible and keep pressuring me to break up. I don't want to lose my family or my partner. I feel stuck in the middle. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Generational and cultural conflict — highly relevant to immigrant test-takers; emotionally charged with no easy answer.

---

**Q2. Considering Divorce After Years of Marriage**
> Your friend says: "My husband and I have been married for 12 years, but we've been unhappy for the last three. We barely talk, we argue constantly, and we've grown apart. We have two kids and I'm terrified of the impact on them. Part of me wants to try again and part of me wants to leave. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* One of the most serious and universally recognized life decisions; multiple advice paths; high emotional stakes.

---

**Q3. Long-Distance Relationship — Doubts About the Future**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I have been in a long-distance relationship for 18 months. We love each other but the distance is really hard — we only see each other every few months, we argue more often, and I don't know how much longer I can do this. There's no clear plan for when we'll be in the same city. What would you advise?"

*Why high:* Common situation for immigrants and young professionals; blend of emotional and practical advice needed.

---

**Q4. Serious Conflict With In-Laws**
> Your friend says: "My mother-in-law constantly criticizes how I raise my children, run my home, and even how I dress. My husband doesn't defend me — he says she means well. It's affecting my marriage and my mental health. Every family gathering leaves me feeling humiliated. What advice can you give me?"

*Why high:* In-law conflict is a near-universal experience and a classic CELPIP-style scenario.

---

**Q5. Supporting a Family Member With Depression**
> Your friend says: "My younger brother has been deeply depressed for months. He barely leaves his room, has lost his job, and pushes everyone away when we try to help. I'm scared for him but I don't know how to reach him without making things worse. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Mental health support within families is increasingly relevant; tests empathy and knowledge of appropriate steps.

---

**Q6. Adult Child Moving Back Home — Creating Tension**
> Your friend says: "My 28-year-old son moved back home six months ago after losing his job. At first I was happy to help, but now he doesn't contribute to the household, stays up all night, and treats the house like a hotel. I love him but I feel like my home isn't my own anymore. What should I do?"

*Why high:* A situation many Canadian families face; balances love with healthy boundaries.

---

**Q7. Partner Not Contributing to Household Responsibilities**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I both work full-time, but I do almost all the cooking, cleaning, and childcare at home. When I bring it up, they say they're tired and promise to do better, but nothing changes. I'm exhausted and starting to resent them. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Extremely relatable across all demographics; involves fairness, communication, and emotional wellbeing.

---

**Q8. Sibling Conflict Over Caring for an Aging Parent**
> Your friend says: "My mother is getting older and needs more help, but I'm the only one doing everything — the doctor appointments, the grocery runs, the daily check-ins. My two siblings live nearby but never offer to help and get defensive when I bring it up. I'm burning out. What should I do?"

*Why high:* Caregiver burden combined with sibling inequality is a deeply felt and common family situation.

---

**Q9. Communication Breakdown With Partner**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I used to talk about everything, but lately we barely communicate. We come home, eat in silence, and go to bed without really connecting. Neither of us is angry — we've just drifted apart. I love them but I don't know how to bring us back together. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Quiet relationship decline is harder to name but very common — realistic and nuanced.

---

**Q10. Reconnecting With an Estranged Family Member**
> Your friend says: "I haven't spoken to my sister in four years after a serious argument. Recently I've been thinking about reaching out, but I'm afraid she'll reject me, and I'm not sure I'm ready to forgive everything that happened. Our parents are getting older and I don't want regret. What would you advise?"

*Why high:* Reconciliation vs. self-protection is a rich, emotionally layered dilemma with no clear right answer.

---

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Telling an Aging Parent They Can No Longer Live Alone**
> Your friend says: "My father is 80 and has had two falls at home in the last year. His memory is also getting worse. He insists on living independently and becomes very angry when we suggest he move in with us or go to a care facility. We're scared something serious will happen. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q12. Partner Resistant to Couples Counselling**
> Your friend says: "My marriage has been struggling for a long time and I've suggested couples counselling many times, but my husband refuses — he says it's embarrassing and that our problems are private. I feel like we can't fix things on our own. What would you advise me to do?"

---

**Q13. Financial Disagreements With a Partner**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I fight about money constantly. I'm a saver and they're a spender — they've taken on credit card debt without telling me and I only found out recently. I feel betrayed and scared about our future. How would you advise me to handle this?"

---

**Q14. Blended Family Challenges — Stepchildren**
> Your friend says: "I recently married someone with two teenage children from a previous relationship. The kids are cold to me, don't follow any rules I set, and my husband always takes their side when there's conflict. I love my husband but I feel like an outsider in my own home. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q15. Cultural Differences in a Relationship**
> Your friend says: "I'm in a relationship with someone from a completely different cultural background. We love each other but we have very different expectations about family roles, holidays, and how to raise children. Our families on both sides are not fully supportive. What would you advise us to do?"

---

**Q16. Disagreement About Having Children**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I have been together for five years and we want different things — I want children and they don't. We've talked about it many times but neither of us has changed our mind. I'm 33 and I feel like time is running out. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q17. Overinvolved Parents — Setting Boundaries**
> Your friend says: "My parents call me multiple times a day, show up at my home without notice, and give unsolicited advice about every decision I make. I love them but I'm 32 years old and I need more independence. Every time I try to set limits, they get hurt and I feel guilty. What should I do?"

---

**Q18. Friend in an Unhealthy or Abusive Relationship**
> Your friend says: "I'm worried about my close friend — her partner controls what she wears, who she sees, and where she goes. She defends him and says everything is fine, but I can see she's unhappy and isolated. I'm scared to push too hard in case she cuts me off. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Supporting a Friend Through a Painful Divorce**
> Your friend says: "My best friend is going through a difficult divorce. She calls me every day, sometimes for hours, in tears. I want to be there for her but I'm starting to feel emotionally drained, and it's affecting my own life. How can I support her without losing myself?"

---

**Q20. Partner's Career Taking Over the Relationship**
> Your friend says: "My partner was recently promoted and now works 12-hour days and most weekends. We haven't had a proper conversation in weeks and we haven't spent quality time together in months. They say it's temporary but it's been six months. I feel like I'm living with a stranger. What advice would you give?"

---

## ★★☆ Medium Probability

---

**Q21. Discovering a Partner Has Been Unfaithful**
> Your friend says: "I recently found out that my partner cheated on me six months ago. They've confessed, apologized, and want to rebuild our relationship. We have a home and children together. I still love them but I can't stop thinking about the betrayal. Should I stay or leave?"

---

**Q22. Family Member Struggling With Addiction**
> Your friend says: "My brother has a serious alcohol problem that has cost him his job and is now affecting his health. Every time we try to help, he denies it's a problem or promises to stop and then doesn't. My parents refuse to stop enabling him. I don't know what else to do. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q23. Feeling Lonely After Moving Away From Family**
> Your friend says: "I moved to a new city two years ago and even though I like my job and my neighbourhood, I feel profoundly lonely. I miss my family and close friends intensely. I've tried to meet new people but nothing has turned into a real friendship yet. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Toxic Family Member Affecting Gatherings**
> Your friend says: "Every family event becomes uncomfortable because of one relative who is critical, disrespectful, and creates drama. Other family members refuse to address it because they want to keep the peace. I dread every gathering. How would you suggest I handle this situation?"

---

**Q25. Telling a Friend a Difficult Truth**
> Your friend says: "My closest friend is making a decision I think is a serious mistake — she's about to lend a large sum of money to someone I don't trust. She hasn't asked for my opinion, and I know she won't want to hear it. But I feel I'd be a bad friend if I said nothing. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. Sibling Conflict Over Family Inheritance**
> Your friend says: "My father passed away recently and my siblings and I have completely different ideas about how to handle the inheritance. One sibling wants to sell the family home, another wants to keep it, and the arguments have become very personal and hurtful. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q27. Friend Who Always Needs Help — One-Sided Friendship**
> Your friend says: "I have a friend who calls me every time she has a problem — and she always has a problem. I give advice, time, and emotional support, but when I'm going through something difficult, she's never really there for me. I care about her but I feel used. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q28. Parenting Disagreements With a Spouse**
> Your friend says: "My husband and I completely disagree on how to discipline our children. I believe in setting firm boundaries and he thinks I'm too strict. He undermines my rules in front of the kids and it sends them mixed messages. The kids are starting to play us against each other. What should I do?"

---

**Q29. Aging Parent Refusing to Accept Help**
> Your friend says: "My mother needs help with daily tasks but refuses to accept any assistance — she says she doesn't want to be a burden and insists she's fine. But she's clearly struggling and we're all worried she'll get hurt. She becomes upset when we push the topic. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q30. Feeling Disconnected From Your Own Children**
> Your friend says: "My teenage children barely speak to me anymore — they're always on their phones, spend all their time in their rooms, and give one-word answers when I try to talk to them. I feel like I've lost my connection with them and I don't know how to get it back. What advice can you give?"

---

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Interracial or Interfaith Relationship — Family Opposition**
> Your friend says: "My family strongly opposes my relationship because my partner is from a different religion and cultural background. They've threatened to cut ties with me if I continue the relationship. I love my partner and I love my family. I don't know how to navigate this. What would you advise?"

---

**Q32. Reconnecting With a Parent After Years of Estrangement**
> Your friend says: "My father left when I was a child and we had no contact for 20 years. He has recently reached out wanting to reconnect. Part of me is curious and part of me is still angry. My mother thinks I should ignore him. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Supporting a Partner Through Serious Illness**
> Your friend says: "My partner was recently diagnosed with a serious illness and I've become their primary caregiver. I love them deeply but I feel overwhelmed, scared, and sometimes even resentful — and then I feel guilty for that. I don't want to fall apart when they need me most. What advice can you give?"

---

**Q34. Deciding Whether to Tell Children About a Family Secret**
> Your friend says: "My husband and I have kept a significant family secret from our teenage children — something about their origins that they have a right to know. We've been afraid of how they'll react, but they're getting older and we worry they'll find out another way. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q35. Jealousy Towards a More Successful Sibling**
> Your friend says: "My younger sister is very successful — great career, beautiful home, wonderful relationship — and I struggle constantly with jealousy and feelings of inadequacy. I love her but family gatherings are painful for me. I'm ashamed of how I feel. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q36. New Baby Putting Serious Strain on the Relationship**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I were very close before we had our baby, but now we are both so exhausted, stressed, and overwhelmed that we barely speak kindly to each other. We argue constantly over small things and I sometimes feel I made a mistake — not about the baby, but about the relationship. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Sibling Asking for a Large Loan — Worried About the Relationship**
> Your friend says: "My brother has asked me to lend him a significant amount of money to help with a financial emergency. I want to help him but I'm worried — he has borrowed from family before and not paid it back, and I'm afraid that if I say no it will damage our relationship, and if I say yes and he doesn't repay it, it will damage it even more. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Ex-Partner Still in the Friend Group — Making Things Uncomfortable**
> Your friend says: "My ex-partner and I broke up six months ago after a painful ending. We share the same group of friends and they're still included in every social event. I feel tense and sad every time we're in the same room, but I don't want to ask people to choose sides. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Parent's New Partner After Widowhood — Hard to Accept**
> Your friend says: "My father remarried a year after my mother passed away. I know he was lonely and I want him to be happy, but I find it very hard to accept this new person in our family. My siblings feel the same. We've been polite but cold and my father has noticed. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q40. Partner From a Very Different Socioeconomic Background — Family Pressure**
> Your friend says: "I'm in a serious relationship with someone I love, but we come from very different financial backgrounds. My family has quietly but persistently expressed concerns — about lifestyle differences, long-term compatibility, and whether we want the same future. I find their comments hurtful and my partner has sensed the tension. What advice would you give me?"
