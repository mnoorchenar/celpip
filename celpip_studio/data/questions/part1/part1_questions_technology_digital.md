# CELPIP Speaking — Part 1: Giving Advice
## Category 8: Technology & Digital Life — All Practice Questions
### Sorted by Exam Likelihood (High → Lower)

**Total: 40 questions**

CELPIP Part 1 format: A friend, family member, or colleague describes a real-life situation and asks for your advice. You have 30 seconds to prepare and 90 seconds to respond.

---

## ★★★ High Probability (Most likely to appear)
*Universal, emotionally rich, multiple valid advice directions, deeply relatable across cultures*

---

**Q1. Child Spending Too Much Time on Screens**
> Your friend says: "My 9-year-old is on a screen from the moment they wake up until I make them stop at night — tablets, phones, video games, and TV. Their attention span is shrinking, they're irritable when I take the devices away, and they've lost interest in outdoor activities and reading. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Screen time for children is one of the most debated parenting topics today; universally relatable.

---

**Q2. Victim of an Online Scam — Lost Money**
> Your friend says: "I received what looked like an official email from my bank asking me to verify my account. I clicked the link, entered my details, and the next day $3,000 was withdrawn from my account. I feel embarrassed and stupid. I've reported it to the bank but they say they can't guarantee a refund. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Phishing and online fraud are extremely common in Canada; emotional shame + practical problem combined.

---

**Q3. Teenager Being Cyberbullied**
> Your friend says: "I discovered that my 14-year-old has been receiving cruel and humiliating messages on social media from classmates. They begged me not to get involved because they're afraid it will get worse if I do. But I can see it's affecting their mental health — they've stopped eating properly and don't want to go to school. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Cyberbullying affects families across all demographics; combines digital and emotional safety.

---

**Q4. Social Media Addiction Affecting Real Life**
> Your friend says: "I spend three to four hours a day on social media without even realising it. I compare myself to others constantly, I feel anxious when I can't check my phone, and I've noticed I'm less present with my family and friends. I've tried to cut back but I always go back. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Social media overuse is a defining modern struggle; psychological and practical advice both apply.

---

**Q5. Elderly Parent Struggling With Technology**
> Your friend says: "My 72-year-old mother has a smartphone but can barely use it. She can't figure out video calls, misses important messages, and has nearly fallen for scam calls twice. I live far away and I'm worried about her being isolated and vulnerable. I've tried explaining things but she gets frustrated quickly. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Digital divide between generations is a deeply relatable family challenge, especially for immigrant families.

---

**Q6. Privacy Concerns — Unsure What to Share Online**
> Your friend says: "I've been reading about how companies collect personal data from everything I do online — my searches, my purchases, my location. I feel like my privacy is completely gone but I don't know what I can actually do about it. I'm not sure which apps and settings to change or even whether it matters. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Digital privacy anxiety is increasingly mainstream; tests practical and philosophical advice.

---

**Q7. Working From Home — Technology Blurring Work and Personal Life**
> Your friend says: "Since I started working from home, I can never fully switch off. My work laptop is always nearby, my boss messages me in the evenings and on weekends, and I feel guilty if I don't respond immediately. I have no boundary between work and home anymore. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Work-from-home technology boundaries are a post-pandemic universal concern.

---

**Q8. Identity Theft — Personal Information Was Stolen**
> Your friend says: "I found out that someone has been using my personal information — they opened a credit card in my name and made thousands of dollars in purchases. My credit score has been damaged and I have no idea how my information was stolen or how to fix this. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Identity theft is a significant and growing concern in Canada; urgent, practical advice needed.

---

**Q9. Teenager Sharing Too Much Personal Information Online**
> Your friend says: "My 16-year-old posts everything on social media — their location, their school, photos of our home, and personal details about our family. I've tried to explain the risks but they say I'm paranoid and that all their friends do the same. I'm genuinely worried about their safety. What advice would you give?"

*Why high:* Teen online safety is a priority concern for every parent; generational conflict adds nuance.

---

**Q10. Job Interview Preparation — Online Presence Being Checked**
> Your friend says: "I just found out that employers routinely check social media profiles before hiring. I have years of posts I'm not proud of — opinions I've changed, photos from parties, and political comments. I have a big interview coming up and I don't know how to handle my online history. What advice would you give me?"

*Why high:* Digital footprint affecting employment is a modern, highly relevant concern for job seekers.

## ★★☆ Medium-High Probability

---

**Q11. Child Wants to Become a Content Creator — Parent Unsure**
> Your friend says: "My 15-year-old wants to start a YouTube channel and build a following online. They're passionate about it and say it could become a career. I'm worried about privacy, online predators, and whether it will distract them from school. But I also don't want to crush their dream. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q12. Online Relationship — Never Met in Person**
> Your friend says: "My friend has been in an online relationship for eight months with someone they've never met in person. They talk daily and have strong feelings for each other. I'm worried it might not be what it seems, but my friend gets defensive whenever I raise concerns. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q13. Dependence on GPS — Lost Basic Navigation Skills**
> Your friend says: "I rely completely on GPS for everything and I've realised I have no idea how to navigate without it. Last month my phone died while I was driving in an unfamiliar area and I panicked completely. I feel like I've lost a basic life skill. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q14. Receiving Threatening or Harassing Messages Online**
> Your friend says: "I've been receiving threatening and harassing messages from an anonymous account online. I don't know who it is or why they're targeting me. I'm scared but I'm not sure if it's serious enough to go to the police. Blocking them just leads to new accounts. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q15. Remote Work Setup — Productivity Problems at Home**
> Your friend says: "I work from home but my setup is terrible — I sit at my kitchen table, my back hurts constantly, my internet drops during meetings, and my family interrupts me throughout the day. My employer won't pay for equipment. My productivity is suffering. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q16. Deepfake or Fake Photo of Them Shared Online**
> Your friend says: "Someone has created and shared a digitally altered photo of me online that is humiliating and completely false. It's being spread by people I know and some I don't. I'm devastated and don't know how to get it removed or who to report it to. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q17. Technology Overload — Constant Notifications Causing Anxiety**
> Your friend says: "I have notifications constantly going off from emails, messages, apps, and news alerts. I feel like I'm always on edge, always interrupted, and I can never focus on one thing for more than a few minutes. I've tried turning things off but I feel anxious when I do. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q18. Child Accessing Inappropriate Content Online**
> Your friend says: "I discovered that my 11-year-old has been accessing violent and adult content online through a friend's device that had no parental controls. I don't know how much they've seen or how it may have affected them. I want to address it without shaming them. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q19. Smart Home Devices — Partner Has Privacy Concerns**
> Your friend says: "I want to install smart home devices — speakers, cameras, and sensors — to make our home more convenient and secure. My partner is strongly opposed because they think these devices listen to our conversations and collect data. We can't agree. What advice would you give us?"

---

**Q20. Negative Online Review Left About Their Business**
> Your friend says: "Someone left a very negative and I believe unfair review of my small business online. The review has already cost me customers and the platform won't remove it. I'm tempted to respond publicly but I don't want to make things worse. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q21. Email Hacked — Contacts Receiving Spam**

---

**Q22. Elderly Parent Victim of a Phone Scam**
> Your friend says: "My 78-year-old father received a call from someone pretending to be from the government saying he owed back taxes and would be arrested if he didn't pay immediately. He was frightened and sent $2,000 via gift cards before I found out. How do I protect him from this happening again?"

---

**Q23. Teenager Gaming Until Late — Sleep Deprived**
> Your friend says: "My 17-year-old stays up until 2 or 3 in the morning playing online games with friends in different time zones. They're exhausted at school, their grades are dropping, and when I try to enforce a cutoff time we have a huge argument. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q24. Oversharing on Social Media — Now Regretting It**
> Your friend says: "I've been very open on social media for years — sharing personal struggles, family conflicts, and strong political opinions. Now that I'm applying for jobs, I realise how much I've shared and I'm worried it will affect how employers see me. What advice can you give me?"

---

**Q25. Partner Constantly on Their Phone During Quality Time**
> Your friend says: "My partner is on their phone during every meal, every conversation, and even when we're watching something together. I've mentioned it many times but they say I'm overreacting. I feel like I'm not a priority and our connection is suffering. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q26. AI Tools at Work — Feeling Left Behind**
> Your friend says: "My workplace has introduced several AI tools that my younger colleagues are picking up quickly. I'm struggling to keep up and I'm embarrassed to admit I don't understand them. I'm worried I'll be seen as out of touch or even become redundant. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q27. Online Shopping Addiction**
> Your friend says: "I shop online compulsively — I browse late at night, add things to my cart impulsively, and by the time packages arrive I've forgotten I ordered them. My credit card balance keeps growing and I feel ashamed. I've tried deleting apps but reinstall them within days. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q28. Doxxed — Personal Information Shared Without Consent**
> Your friend says: "Someone has publicly posted my home address, phone number, and workplace online after a disagreement on a forum. I've started receiving unwanted calls and I'm scared for my safety. I don't know who did it or how to get the information removed. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q29. Child Wants a Smartphone — Parent Unsure**
> Your friend says: "My 10-year-old is begging for a smartphone. Most of their classmates already have one and they say they feel left out. I'm worried about screen addiction, online safety, and losing their childhood too soon. My partner thinks it's fine at this age. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q30. Video Call Fatigue — Exhausted by Online Meetings**
> Your friend says: "I have back-to-back video calls almost every day for work and I feel completely drained by the end of it. I'm more tired after a day of online meetings than I ever was commuting to an office. I can't concentrate and I'm dreading every call. What advice would you give me?"

## ★☆☆ Lower Probability (Still Possible)

---

**Q31. Dependency on AI for Decision Making**
> Your friend says: "I've started relying on AI chatbots for almost every decision — what to eat, how to respond to emails, even advice about my relationships. My friends say it's unhealthy and that I've stopped trusting my own judgment. I find it hard to decide anything without it now. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q32. Selling Personal Data — Is It Ethical?**
> Your friend says: "I heard about services that pay you to share your personal data — browsing habits, location, purchasing behaviour. I could earn a bit of extra money but I'm not sure if it's a good idea. It feels strange to sell my own information but I need the money. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q33. Child's School Using Surveillance Software on Devices**
> Your friend says: "My child's school installed monitoring software on all student laptops that tracks everything — websites visited, keystrokes, and even activates the camera. I'm uncomfortable with the level of surveillance but the school says it's for safety. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q34. Social Media Fame Gone Wrong**
> Your friend says: "A video I posted online went unexpectedly viral and now I have thousands of followers, most of whom I don't know. The attention feels overwhelming, some comments are disturbing, and I feel like I've lost control of my own image. I didn't ask for this. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q35. Deciding Whether to Raise Children Without Technology**
> Your friend says: "My partner and I are expecting our first child and we've been debating whether to raise them without smartphones, social media, and most screen-based technology for as long as possible. Our families think we're being extreme. We genuinely believe it's the right choice. What advice would you give us?"

---

**Q36. Deepfake Video Used to Impersonate You Online**
> Your friend says: "I discovered that someone created a realistic AI-generated video using my image and posted it online. It shows me saying things I never said and it's being shared widely. I don't know who made it or why, and I don't know how to get it removed or protect myself legally. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q37. Smart Home Devices Making Partner Feel Surveilled**
> Your friend says: "I love the smart devices I've set up at home — cameras, speakers, automated systems — but my partner has started to feel constantly monitored and says the devices make them anxious and resentful. I don't want to give up the convenience but I don't want to damage our relationship. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q38. Online Gaming Addiction Affecting Work and Relationships**
> Your friend says: "I've been playing online games until three or four in the morning several nights a week. My work performance is suffering, I'm chronically tired, and my partner has given me an ultimatum. I know it's a problem but the games fill a need that I don't know how to meet elsewhere. What advice would you give me?"

---

**Q39. Elderly Parent Falling for Phone Scams Repeatedly**
> Your friend says: "My mother has been targeted by phone scammers multiple times and twice has come close to sending money. I've explained the warning signs many times but she is trusting by nature and the scammers are convincing. I'm worried about her safety but she lives independently and I can't monitor every call. What advice would you give?"

---

**Q40. Tech Platform Shutting Down — Years of Data at Risk**
> Your friend says: "A platform I've used for years to store photos, notes, and creative work has announced it's shutting down in 30 days. I have years of content stored there and I'm scrambling to save it, but the export tools aren't working properly. I'm panicking about losing something irreplaceable. What advice would you give me?"
